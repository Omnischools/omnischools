Visiting day, organised

One parent. One teacher. One child. That's a parent-teacher meeting. Termly results discussion with the Form Master; an ad-hoc call about a recent test mark; Visiting Day slot allocation across 8 teachers and 47 parents. This surface handles all three flavours — and respects the SMS-first parent reality where the booking link itself is the SMS. What it doesn't handle is group PTA governance · for that, see the four-tier PTA module (Form / House / General / Emergency) shipped earlier in this batch.

Why two flavours, not one

A single "calendar app" surface would force visiting-day chaos through an ad-hoc-shaped pipe (or vice versa). The reality is that parent-teacher meetings have two distinct rhythms in Ghanaian basic schools: the termly event and the as-needed conversation. The same backend, two front-end shapes — admin learns one configuration surface and one inbox; parents see one SMS pattern that adapts to either flavour.

Calls worth flagging

  • Visiting day cadence is termly, predictable — it earns a configured event with all the rails. Ad-hoc cadence is weekly, responsive — it earns a lightweight conversation pattern. Not the same shape, deliberately.
  • The flavours share the underlying calendar — if a teacher is locked into a 2pm ad-hoc meeting on the visiting day Saturday, the visiting-day generator routes around that slot. One calendar, two entry points.
  • "Walk-ins" are a real pattern in Ghana — parents arrive on visiting day without booking. The system has to handle them, not pretend they don't exist. Section 04 covers that queue.
  • Both flavours funnel into the same per-student meeting history. When admin opens Akosua's record, every meeting (visiting-day or ad-hoc, with notes) is on the timeline.
  • SMS reply parsing is the same as the existing parent comms pattern (see schoolup-parent-conversations.html) — the inbox already knows how to route this.

No app required · the booking link is the SMS

Saturday 21 June · 09:00 AM

The invitation arrives. Akosua's mother (Mrs Asantewaa) reads it on her Itel basic phone. The slot is workable but she'd prefer 11 AM.

You'll get a reminder Friday evening and a 30-min ping on Saturday.

Reply CANCEL to release the slot (before 27 June 6 PM). — CTKJHS

What just happened

Three messages from school, two from Mrs Asantewaa. No app to download. No link to fail-to-load on a 2G connection. The reply codes (SWAP, CONFIRM, 1, 2, 3, CANCEL) work on every phone made since 1995.

Smartphone parents see the same flow with WhatsApp instead — same words, richer formatting. Omnischools routes per-parent (per the cross-module-delivery file).

Why this matters

The booking link is the SMS. Most Ghanaian parents do not have smartphones; the ones who do are often on metered data; the ones who aren't are at the end of patchy networks. A web booking interface would exclude the majority. SMS booking includes everyone — and is faster anyway. The medium is the design.

Calls worth flagging

  • Reply codes are uppercase in the prompts but case-insensitive at the inbox — admin's perspective is "SWAP looks more like a command", parent's perspective is "lowercase is one less thing to fight with".
  • The numbered options (1, 2, 3) are deliberately limited to three. More than three creates choice fatigue and increases parsing errors. "OTHER" escape hatch covers the long tail.
  • Omnischools does not require parents to type the time ("Reply with your preferred time") — that's an open text field that humans get wrong. Numbered options keep parsing reliable.
  • The "CANCEL before 27 June 6 PM" deadline is the cancellation policy set in Section 02. It appears in the confirmation SMS so parents always know the rule.
  • Ad-hoc requests (Flavour 02) follow the same pattern — admin sends 2-3 time options, parent picks a number. One vocabulary, learned once.

Day-of philosophy

The teacher's day-of view does three jobs at once: (1) shows what's coming, so Mrs Akua can prepare for the next conversation; (2) shows what just happened, so she can save notes; (3) handles the chaos — no-shows, swaps, walk-ins, parents waiting in the corridor — without forcing her to leave the screen. The walk-in card is the most-used button on visiting day. Plan for the chaos, design for the flow.

Calls worth flagging

  • The 11:00 row is interesting — that's where Mrs Asantewaa was originally scheduled before her SMS swap. Now it shows "Reassigned · slot now open", and the walk-in card suggests filling it with Mr Boateng. Wasted slots become walk-in opportunities.
  • The "topic" line under each parent (where set) is captured at booking time — parents can reply with a topic via SMS ("performance", "behaviour", "fees") so the teacher walks in prepared. Free-text "not specified" is fine; topic is optional.
  • Notes are saved against the student record, not against the meeting record. When admin opens Akosua's profile, every parent-teacher conversation appears on the timeline alongside attendance, billing, and grades.
  • No-shows trigger automatic SMS follow-up (gold-tinted note: "SMS follow-up queued"). The follow-up offers the parent an ad-hoc rebooking — Flavour 02 takes over. The two flavours hand off cleanly.
  • The "started 10:01 · 14m elapsed" sub-meta is gentle — Mrs Akua sees that her current meeting is running long without a flashing red "OVERTIME" warning. Trust the teacher to manage her own time.
