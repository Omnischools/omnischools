Why this rhythm · and not just one big conversation
Editorial · the 5-phase structure as scaffolding
The rhythm is the design

Five phases · because the work is different in each

It would be simpler to give the Form Master a single sixty-minute block and say "talk about Patriotism." Simpler, and worse. The five-phase rhythm is doing several things at once, and removing any one of them collapses the surface area of what students can do with the time.

Phase 1, Opener, is five minutes. Not ten. The FM frames the value in a sentence and poses today's question. Any longer and the FM is teaching, not framing — and once it tips into teaching, the class settles into receive-mode and stays there. Five minutes forces the FM to set the table and step back.

Phase 2, Small groups, is twenty-five minutes. This is the time that matters. Half the class with a Peer Guide, the other half with the other. Same value, same question, different conversation. The small-group containment is what makes truth-telling possible — a forty-person classroom is a stage; a ten-person group is a room. Students who would never speak up in plenary will sometimes say the thing they actually think when only nine peers and a PG are listening. The PG's job is not to lead the discussion to an answer; it's to keep the group on the question and keep time.

Phase 3, Plenary, is fifteen minutes. Each group shares back. This is where the class hears the range of what their classmates think — including the things they didn't think themselves. The FM moderates the share-back, doesn't preach, doesn't summarise toward a conclusion. The range is the conclusion.

Phase 4, Reflection, is ten minutes of silence. No discussion, no group, no PG, no FM looking over shoulders. Each student writes in their reflection book. The entry is private to the student and to the Form Master who will read it later in the week. The journal is append-only — entries can be added to but not edited, not deleted. Across twenty-two sessions, the journal becomes the document.

Phase 5, Close, is five minutes. FM closes formally — acknowledges the PGs by name, previews next week's value or the continuation, releases the class. Same five minutes every week. The rhythm is the design.

What if a phase runs over?

  • The system absorbs ±3 min. Phase end-times shift but Phase 5 still closes at 3:33. If you blow past 3:33, you've eaten next class's transition time.
  • The reflection phase is non-negotiable. If anything has to be cut short, it should be the plenary, never the journal time. The silent ten minutes is where the work consolidates.
  • FM can pause if a flag arises mid-session. The Pause button stops the clock; nothing advances until FM resumes. Pastoral takes precedence over schedule.
