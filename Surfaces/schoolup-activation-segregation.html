Two signatures, one activation

Bookkeeping activation determines what figures appear on bank-ready PDFs and loan applications — too consequential for a single admin to set alone if the school has more than one. This file adds segregation of duties: the headmistress configures, a chosen co-signer reviews and approves. For solo-admin schools the path stays open with an explicit acknowledgement card; the system never blocks activation, it just documents who decided what.

Solo-admin path

  • Different school in the example: Sunrise Basic School in Tema, with Esther Mensah as the only admin. Avoids confusion with the Christ the King example from State A — the dual-admin and solo paths are clearly different scenarios, not states of the same school.
  • Warn-tinted card (not gold) distinguishes solo-activation from the standard co-signed path. The warning isn't "you're doing something wrong" — it's "the system is documenting that segregation isn't possible here." Honest acknowledgement, not gatekeeping.
  • Heading: "You are the only admin at this school" states the fact without judgement. Body text explains: most schools have two admins; smaller schools commonly don't; "that's fine and common" reassurance.
  • The "if you ever apply for a bank loan" framing connects the acknowledgement to a real future scenario — banks asking auditors to verify books configuration. Concrete consequence rather than abstract policy.
  • Acknowledgement row with checkbox in warn-tinted card contains explicit language: "I am activating the bookkeeping module without a second admin's review · the configuration choices recorded here will be attributed to me alone · this acknowledgement is timestamped and stored in the audit trail." Esther understands what she's accepting by ticking it.
  • Workaround visible in the acknowledgement: "I can add a co-signer to future proposals by promoting a staff member to admin in Staff → Permissions." The solo path isn't a one-way door; if Esther later hires an assistant, she can switch to dual-control for future configuration changes.
  • Save bar shows the timestamp + signature being recorded: "logged with timestamp 22 Oct 2025 · 2:14 PM GMT and your name Esther Mensah." The audit record is shown before it's created, not after. Esther sees exactly what gets stored.
  • Gold CTA changes wording: "Acknowledge & activate →" instead of "Activate" or "Send for co-signing." The verb matches the act; she's both acknowledging the absence of segregation and activating in the same press.
  • What this design deliberately doesn't do: doesn't refuse activation when only one admin exists, doesn't auto-promote a non-admin staff member to admin to satisfy the rule, doesn't fall back to a "lite" mode without proper P&L. Solo schools deserve the full system; the design just documents the trade-off.
