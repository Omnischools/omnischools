Why this layout

  • Three-pane shell: app sidebar / register list / active register. The middle pane is the desktop addition — a teacher who teaches multiple classes sees all of today's registers without leaving the page. The mobile flow assumed one class at a time; desktop uses the room.
  • Register list shows progress at the top: "3 of 5 done" in Fraunces with gold rule. The bar visualises the same fact. A teacher with 5 classes always knows where they are without doing arithmetic.
  • Filter pills (All / Pending / Done) let teacher cut the list. Most useful right after lunch — "show me what's pending so I can knock them out before going home."
  • Each register row has a status sparkline — coloured proportions of present/late/excused/absent for completed registers, solid grey for pending. The colour bar tells the story of the class day at a glance — a register that's mostly green looks normal, one with a chunk of terra catches the eye.
  • "Earlier this week" section header separates today's pending+done from past locked registers. Yesterday and Wednesday show with "Locked" status pills — teacher can click through to view but not edit. Continuous scroll surfaces the recent history; admin/teacher rarely needs to dig further than 2-3 days back.
  • Active register header carries class identity in big Fraunces — "JHS 2A · Friday 2 May" — same brand language as everywhere else. The lede line gives all context (student count, class teacher, term, day count).
  • Two header buttons: "Copy from yesterday" and "View term grid". Copy-from-yesterday is the desktop-specific bulk operation: when a class has the same exceptions multi-day (excursion week, exam week with consistent absentees), starting from yesterday's marks saves work. View-term-grid pivots to Section 02's view.
  • Five-stat tile strip instead of mobile's four-chip. Desktop adds an "Unmarked" stat — useful when teacher is mid-marking and wants to see how many rows still need attention. Each tile shows the count + a sub-line with specifics (which student is late, why excused, how many consecutive days absent).
  • Sub-lines on stat tiles are personalised: "Kwame Adu · 8:23 AM" on the Late tile rather than a generic counter. Stats become specific stories when there are only one or two of each. With 12 absentees, the sub-line would say "12 students · view list."
  • Action bar with bulk operations on the left, keyboard shortcuts on the right. Three bulk buttons: All present (primary), Excursion, Reset. Three shortcut groups visible: P/L/E/A for status, ↑↓ for navigate, ⏎ for reason. The shortcuts panel is the desktop's superpower — teacher doesn't have to discover; they're shown.
  • Roster as a real table with columns: # / Student / Term attendance / Status / Reason. Five columns, each with purpose. The desktop has the room mobile didn't; we use it for the reason column that mobile hid in a bottom sheet.
  • Row numbering (01, 02, 03...) in mono — matches paper-register convention. Older teachers grew up with numbered registers; the system shouldn't abandon the convention just because the medium changed.
  • Term attendance column shows percentage AND days fraction — "96% · 45/47 days." Both forms because different teachers think in different terms. The percentage is fast scan; the fraction is concrete and auditable.
  • Status segmented control is wider on desktop (36px tiles vs 30px on mobile) and uses the same gold/navy/terra fills. Hover state lifts the tile so keyboard focus is visible. Selected tiles are filled solid; unselected sit on the bg.
  • Reason column shows the auto-populated detail inline — "Medical appointment · mother called at 7:40 AM, dental" / "No contact · 3rd consecutive day · admin alert sent" / "Late · 8:23 AM · 23 min after start." On mobile this required tapping into the bottom sheet; desktop puts it in the row.
  • Em-dash for empty reason (rather than blank) so the column is visually anchored even when most rows have nothing in them. Matches our em-dash convention from the rest of the system.
  • Focused row has gold outline + gold-bg tint. Keyboard navigation moves the focus; teacher pressing ↑↓ sees the gold ring move through the table. The status keys (P/L/E/A) act on the focused row. This is the keyboard-driven workflow — mouse is optional.
  • Submit footer is sticky. Progress count ("36 of 36 marked") in Fraunces; meta line tells teacher exactly what the system will do next. "2 absence SMS will go out at 3:00 PM today · last edit window closes tomorrow 3:42 PM" — both consequences spelled out so teacher commits with informed eyes.
  • Submit button shows ⌘⏎ shortcut. The shortcut panel above explains the workflow, the button reinforces the commit shortcut. Teacher who's been keyboard-driven all the way through can finish without ever touching the mouse.

Why this layout

  • This is the screen that wins skeptical older teachers. The paper class register has had this exact layout for a hundred years — students down the left, days across the top, P/L/E/A in each cell. Putting the same layout on screen makes the system feel familiar, not alien.
  • The grid is a reading view first, an editing view second. Teacher mostly opens this to see the term — who's been absent, when patterns started, how the class is trending. Editing happens by clicking a cell, but the overwhelming use case is reading.
  • Sticky student column on the left, sticky summary column on the right. Both have a 1.5px navy border to distinguish them from the day cells. As teacher scrolls horizontally through the term, names and totals stay anchored. Without sticky columns, this view would be useless beyond two weeks of dates.
  • Two-row header: day-of-week letter on top, day number below. M T W T F gives the rhythm at a glance; 21 22 23 24 25 anchors the calendar. Weekend columns are subtly tinted; today's column is gold-tinted with a gold underline.
  • Holidays use diagonal-line hatching matching the GES return manual-fill pattern. Same visual = same meaning ("nothing happens here"). Future days are dashed-border empty cells. The grid distinguishes "no school" from "not yet" from "didn't mark" — three different absences that look the same on a paper register but matter differently here.
  • Cells are saturated colour blocks with letter inside. Green P, gold L, warn E, terra A. Pattern detection comes free — James's row is mostly red, Esi's row has a Friday-coloured warn pattern, Akosua's row is a clean green stripe. The teacher sees stories, not data.
  • Today's column gets gold ring outline on the active cell. Teacher's eye lands on today first; from there they can scan back and ahead.
  • Summary column shows the breakdown in colour-coded mono — "P 45 · A 2" or "P 29 · A 18" or "P 34 · E 8 · A 5." The same dense format paper registers use. Mono not Fraunces because these are counts, not headlines; auditor can compare two students' totals at a glance.
  • Per-student attendance percentage in the left column in colour-coded mono — green for 90+%, gold for 70-89%, terra for under 70%. Days fraction below ("45/47") for transparency. Same colour mapping as the row tags in Section 01; consistent semantics.
  • Footer row with class totals per day. "33/36" means 33 of 36 students were marked anything-other-than-absent. The bottom-right cell shows term average ("93.8% avg"). Class teacher who wants to know how the class is trending across days has it in one row.
  • Cells are clickable — opens the same exception drawer mobile uses. Editing inside the edit window is one click. Editing past the edit window prompts the request-edit flow. The grid isn't read-only; it's editable as far as policy allows.
  • Tooltip on hover reveals the day name and status ("Mon 21 Apr · Present"). Teacher who isn't sure what date a column represents gets the answer without leaving the screen. The tooltip is also where excused-absence reasons would surface ("Wed 30 Apr · Excused · medical appointment").
  • "Export PDF" button in the header generates a printable version of this exact view — the term register as a PDF for filing or for the headmistress to review on paper. Same data, three surfaces (mobile mark, desktop edit, paper file).
  • Legend strip in the controls bar covers all seven cell types. The legend appears once, prominently, so teacher learning the system has a key. Removed once familiar; for now it's a tutorial that doesn't feel like a tutorial.
  • Month nav at the top of the controls with Apr - May 2026 in Fraunces. Teacher can scroll through the term in 2-week windows, or pick the whole term from a dropdown. Why two weeks at a time: 14 days × 30px column width = 420px, fits comfortably on a 1440px screen with sidebar + sticky columns.
