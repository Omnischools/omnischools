Why these layouts

  • Parent above the fold: child name, average, rank, fees owed, pay button. The five things they open the app to check.
  • Child switcher is permanent, not buried in settings — many parents have multiple kids in school.
  • Pay CTA is gold, not navy. Single most important parent action; deserves the brand's most visible color.
  • Each metric carries its own delta: 82% with "↑ +6 pts", 4th with "↑ 2 places". Pairs the absolute value with personal progress in one read — encouraging for low-ranked students who are still climbing, motivating for top students who can still improve.
  • Parent and student see the same numbers in the same shape. Reinforces shared accountability — no surprises at parent-teacher meetings.
  • Assignments show progress bars, not just deadlines. Visible completion state encourages start.
  • Achievements have a gold gradient tile — the only screen element that gets celebratory treatment. Earned, not default.
  • 5-tab bottom nav on both. Consistent so a parent who is also a teacher (common) doesn't get confused.

Why this works

  • Spreadsheet model is non-negotiable — teachers know it. Inline cell editing, click to enter score, tab to next.
  • Three CA pills separate Continuous Assessment from Term Exam from Final Grade. Ghanaian basic-school grading uses both; collapsing them confuses.
  • Empty cells show a dash, not zero. A missing assignment ≠ a 0 — that's a workflow choice that matters for fairness.
  • Grade column auto-computes from total. Teachers don't manually assign letter grades; the system does it from school's grade boundaries.
  • Class average row at the bottom is constant. Tells the teacher whether a question was too hard at a glance.
  • Save lives top-right. Auto-save indicator handles anxiety; manual save handles control.

Why this works

  • 4 KPIs across the top — billed, collected, outstanding, overdue. Headmistress's morning glance.
  • Discounts cascade through the panel, not the table. Table shows net billed with a small −50% or Exempt badge so admin can scan; the panel breakdown shows the math (subtotal → discount → net) for transparency and audit.
  • 100% exempt students still get an invoice (GHS 0.00) so attendance, audit, and ministry reporting all see consistent records — no special-casing them out of the system.
  • Logo upload is a dashed gold placeholder in the sidebar — the first thing a new admin sees on day one. Single click to upload; appears for teachers and students once set.
  • Status pills are filters, not just labels. One click to see "show me only overdue".
  • Right-rail invoice panel updates on row select. No modals, no page nav. Drastically reduces clicks for the 50+ payments recorded weekly.
  • MoMo lockup (yellow MTN tile) is intentional — admin needs to confirm a number quickly when a parent calls. Live integration comes in MVP2; v1 is manual confirm.
  • History audit trail answers the question "did we send a reminder?" and "who approved this discount?" — meets the audit-log requirement in your spec.
  • + Record payment is the one gold button on screen. Opens a drawer to log amount, method, MoMo TXN ref, and triggers receipt + SMS confirmation.
