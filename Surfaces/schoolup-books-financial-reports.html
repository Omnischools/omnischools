P&L & the balance sheet

Two states: the in-app reports view (interactive, with prior-year comparisons and editorial framing) and the bank-ready PDF preview (formal, statement-grade, what banks expect to see for loan applications). Both produced from the same underlying data — the P&L composes from expense entries + income entries, the balance sheet composes from fixed assets + outstanding fees + bank balance + retained earnings.

In-app reports

  • Two-up grid: P&L on left, balance sheet on right. Side-by-side because they're two views of the same data — admin can scan both without scrolling. One report shows performance over time (P&L, the term), the other shows position at a moment (balance sheet, today).
  • P&L surplus highlighted in green-tinted card with italic gold "32,072" — the most important number on the page. Same italic gold convention as user-tunable values; here it signifies "this is the answer."
  • Depreciation expense as its own line on the P&L (GHS 10,500). Schools rarely think to record depreciation as an expense, but it's required for an honest P&L and a properly-balancing balance sheet. The line connects to fixed assets register.
  • Surplus shifted from GHS 42,072 (dashboard) to GHS 32,072 (P&L) because depreciation hit the income statement. Honest accounting: the dashboard view shows operational surplus; the formal P&L includes depreciation. Both are "right" depending on what you're asking.
  • Balance sheet balances: GHS 1,161,180 = 1,161,180 (assets = liabilities + equity). Visible balance check in gold-tinted card at the bottom of the BS — admin sees the accounting identity hold. If it didn't balance, the system would surface an error.
  • "Bank loan · GCB · classroom block · 2018" as a line item. Real Ghanaian school finance pattern: schools take out loans for capital improvements and pay them down over years. The 2018 loan for the annex block (recorded as a CTK-BLD-002 building asset) is the matched liability.
  • "Proprietor's capital + retained surplus" structure matches private school accounting. For public schools the structure flips to "Capital (PTA + government) + Reserves + Term surplus." Same data model adapts to school type.
  • 4 report tabs: P&L + BS / P&L only / Balance sheet only / Cash flow · MVP3. Cash flow statement deferred to MVP3 with explicit tag — banks ask for it eventually but it's harder to build correctly.
  • "Export bank-ready PDF" gold CTA in the page header takes admin to the formal PDF view (state B). Two output formats from same data: interactive in-app vs formal PDF.

Bank-ready PDF

  • 4-page structure: cover + identification / income statement / balance sheet / declaration. Same pattern as the GES termly return and annual census — schools learn the document format once. Same masthead, same identification block, same declaration with stamp + signature.
  • Comparative columns showing prior year on every figure. Banks expect this — they want to see trend, not just snapshot. Same period last year (Term 1 2024/25) for the P&L; year-end (31 Aug 2025) for the balance sheet.
  • "Basis of preparation" section on the cover page using accounting language: accruals basis, cost less depreciation, straight-line, GHS. Tells the bank reviewer the framework in their own vocabulary; positions Omnischools's output as serious.
  • "For the bank reviewer" narrative box on the cover acknowledges that one term of statements isn't enough for most loan applications and points to the full-year availability. Honest about the limitation; positions the school as understanding what banks need.
  • Period commentary on the P&L: "Surplus increased by GHS 3,032 (10.4%) over the prior year, driven primarily by lower repairs & maintenance..." The system narrates the variance so the reviewer doesn't have to compute it.
  • Note on outstanding fees in the BS section: "expected to reduce to under GHS 12,000 by 31 January 2026 based on prior year patterns." The receivable balance looks scary in isolation (GHS 67,980 vs GHS 12,400 prior year-end); the note explains why — Term 1 receivables are normal mid-term, they collect through the year.
  • Declaration explicit about "not externally audited." Omnischools's output is internal management quality; banks will sometimes accept it, sometimes require external auditor sign-off depending on loan size. Honest about scope rather than implying audit-grade.
  • Notes for the recipient include CSV transaction-level export availability — banks that want to verify can ask the school for the underlying data. Drill-down available on request.
  • Same school stamp and Florence Addo signature as on GES return / annual census. Visual consistency across all formal Omnischools PDFs; the school's identity is recognisable everywhere.
