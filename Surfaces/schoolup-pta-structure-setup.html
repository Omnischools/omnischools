Four tiers, four conversations

A PTA in a Ghanaian SHS isn't one body — it's four. The Form PTA handles class-level concerns. The House PTA handles boarding and pastoral matters. The General PTA is the school-wide governance body. The Emergency PTA is the body convened when something can't wait. Each tier has its own attendance register, its own officers, sometimes its own dues. This surface is where the admin tells Omnischools which tiers are active at this school, what they look like, and how they're funded — if at all. Dues are optional everywhere. Set once, change anytime.

Before you configure

A PTA in Ghana isn't one body, it's four.

Most SHSs run all four tiers in some form. Day schools may skip the House PTA if Houses are nominal. New schools often start with only the General PTA and add the Form tier in year two. Whatever combination works for your school, configure it here. You can switch tiers on or off later — existing records are preserved.

Dues posture · the Omnischools rule

Dues are forward-only. If you change Form PTA dues from GHS 50 to GHS 75 mid-term, students already invoiced at GHS 50 keep their existing invoice unchanged. The new amount applies to all new invoices from the effective date onward. Same pattern as discounts and fee structures across Omnischools. Past records stay accurate; future records reflect the change. Every dues change is audit-logged with timestamp, admin, and reason.

Design notes

  • Four cards, four toggles. Each tier is independently switchable. Most schools run all four, but the model allows the awkward cases — day-school with nominal Houses, new school starting only with General.
  • Dues are optional everywhere. Each tier has its own dues toggle. Default state matches operational reality: Form tier collects, House tier usually doesn't, General tier collects, Emergency tier raises ad-hoc.
  • Forward-only changes. Amount edits don't retroactively re-invoice. Past records stay; future records reflect new amount. Same audit pattern as the discount management surface.
  • Per-family vs per-student. School chooses per tier at setup. Default: Form tier per-student, General tier per-family. Matches Ghanaian operational practice.
  • Auto-generation preview. The four-tile preview at the bottom is the contract: "here's what we'll create when you save." No surprise instances.

Schema this drives

  • pta_tiers_config · one row per school per tier · active flag, frequency norm, officer roles JSON, dues_enabled, dues_amount, dues_basis, dues_cadence
  • ptas · the generated instances · tier_type, scope_ref (class_id, house_id, or NULL for General)
  • pta_dues_config_history · append-only · every dues change with effective_from date · drives forward-only behaviour

The four conversations · what each tier actually does
Editorial · why four and not one
Tier 1 · Form PTA

"My child's class · what's going on?"

A Form 2 General Arts A parent wants to discuss the WAEC mock results, the upcoming class trip to Cape Coast, and whether the class would contribute to a leaving gift for the outgoing Class Prefect. This isn't a Headmaster-level conversation. It belongs at the Form PTA — Form Master in the room, the 32 parents who care, two hours one Saturday a term.
Who
32 parents · Form Master · subject teachers if invited
When
Once per term · Form Master convenes
Tier 2 · House PTA

"My child's House · welfare, boarding, sport"

An Aryee House parent wants to raise concerns about the dining hall menu, the inter-House athletics fund, and the proposed renovation of the House dormitory. This isn't a Form-level concern and the Form Master can't action it. It's a Housemaster + House parents conversation. Often aligns with Visiting Day so out-of-town parents can attend.
Who
Parents of House students · Housemaster · House matron
When
Once or twice per year · often Visiting Day-aligned
Tier 3 · General PTA

"The school · policy, fees, leadership"

The school-wide PTA. Elected executive (Chair, Secretary, Treasurer, etc.). Discusses tuition fee increases, the BOG's proposed new classroom block, the choice of WASSCE prep materials, the Speech Day budget. The Headmaster reports here. Resolutions passed at General PTA are binding governance decisions. AGM annually with officer elections.
Who
All parents · Headmaster (ex-officio) · executive officers
When
Once per term + AGM · executive convenes
Tier 4 · Emergency PTA

"Something happened · we need to meet"

A serious dormitory incident. A funding crisis. A government policy change that needs parental discussion before next term. The Emergency tier is the convene-button when the next scheduled General PTA is too far away. Headmaster or PTA Chair can call it. 48-hour notice typical. SMS-blast to all parents. Resolution passed → school can act on it.
Who
Default: General PTA scope · or specified subset
When
On-demand · convener-triggered · audit-logged

Why four tiers

  • Different conversations belong at different levels. A class trip isn't a school-wide concern; a tuition increase isn't a Form-level concern. The four-tier structure routes the conversation to the right room.
  • Different officers, different mandates. A Form PTA Chair has different authority than the General PTA Chair. The matrix surface (next file) handles who holds which role at which tier.
  • Different dues, different uses. Form dues fund class activities. General dues fund school-wide projects. House dues (rare) fund House welfare. Emergency levies fund the emergency.
  • This shape was the operational reality before Omnischools. The four-tier model isn't introduced by the platform — it's how Ghanaian SHSs already work, made legible.

Mobile considerations

  • Tier list is the entry point. Mobile admin sees the four tiers at a glance, taps in to configure each.
  • Dues edit flow is the high-frequency mobile use case. Admin getting a call from PTA Chair: "we agreed at last night's meeting to raise dues to 75." Admin pulls up phone, edits, captures the reason in audit, done in 30 seconds.
  • The reason field is mandatory. Especially for forward-only changes that affect parent bills — audit needs to know why the change happened, not just that it did.
