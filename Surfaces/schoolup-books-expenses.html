Recording what went out

Two states matching the dual-mode design choice: the per-transaction ledger (detailed mode) for schools that record every expense as it happens, and the monthly aggregate entry (aggregate mode) for schools that summarise category totals at month-end. A school can switch from aggregate to detailed without losing historical data — old months stay as summaries, new months go into the ledger.

Detailed mode

  • Mode pills sit just below the headline with "Detailed · per transaction" active and a meta line: "Switching to aggregate keeps your historical entries · only changes how new entries are recorded." Honest about the consequence of switching modes; admin doesn't lose work by changing her mind.
  • Filter pills with counts per category give Joyce one-click filtering to a category. Each pill shows the per-category count in mono — Salaries 17, Utilities 6, Supplies 8 etc. Visible breakdown without drilling in.
  • Day-grouped rows with day totals: "Today · Wed 22 Oct · GHS 1,840 · 4 entries" header rows separate the ledger into operational chunks. Days are how schools think about money flow; flat chronological lists lose that rhythm.
  • Six-column table: Date / Category / Description & payee / Method / Receipt / Amount. Description column carries both line one (the what) and a sub-line (the why or context) — "Caterer · weekly invoice / Aunty Ama Catering · 5 days · 312 students". Real Ghanaian operational specifics.
  • Method column uses three states with colour vocabulary: Bank transfer (neutral), MoMo · MTN (gold-tinted matching the established MoMo identity), Cash (green-tinted). Same payment method language as the billing/receipt module.
  • Receipt column shows attachment state explicitly: "PDF · 84 KB" (green-tinted, attached) or "No receipt" (warn-tinted, missing). Missing receipts are a real audit risk; surfacing the state at table level makes "fix the missing receipts" a discoverable task.
  • Realistic Ghanaian operational entries throughout: ECG bill, GWCL water, Goil Adabraka petrol, Aunty Ama Catering, MTN business internet, Madina market bulk rice. Same localisation depth as the rest of the system; no generic SaaS placeholders.

Aggregate mode

  • Three month overview cards at the top: August (Complete · GHS 14,200), September (Complete · GHS 32,800), October (current, gold-tinted, In progress · GHS 32,840). Joyce sees the trail of past months alongside the one she's working on.
  • "How aggregate mode works" notice band explains the model in plain language: "Enter the total amount you spent in each category. No need to record individual transactions." Sets expectations; aggregate-mode users shouldn't feel guilty about not having a transaction-level ledger.
  • Eight category rows mirror the chart of accounts from the dashboard. Each row has swatch / category name + sub-line / input field / context meta. Same category vocabulary across all bookkeeping surfaces.
  • Inputs use mono font with GHS currency prefix outside the input. Filled inputs have gold border (the user-tunable signal); the empty "Other" row shows italic Fraunces "—" placeholder. Visually consistent with all other amount inputs in the system.
  • Context column on the right is the most useful design move: "Last month GHS 24,400 · ↑ 1.6% · within range" / "Last month GHS 2,160 · ↑ 50% · flagged on dashboard". Joyce sees deltas as she enters — she can sanity-check the number while typing it. The "flagged on dashboard" link connects this entry to the anomaly callout on the dashboard.
  • "Auto-tracked from Hubtel" line on the Bank fees row tells Joyce that Hubtel fees are populated automatically from the integration. She only edits if there are additional bank charges. Cross-module integrity; data flowing from one MVP2 surface into another.
  • Subtotal row at the bottom is read-only with terra-coloured big total. "October total: GHS 32,840 · vs September GHS 32,800 · ↑ 0.1% · steady." The total auto-computes; admin can never make it not match the sum of inputs.
  • Sticky save bar with two actions: "Save and continue later" (draft state) / "Mark October complete →" (gold-primary, finalises the month). Two-state workflow: drafts can be edited freely; complete months are append-only via void/correction (same audit principle as billing).
  • The notice "past months stay as summaries, new entries go in the ledger" in the mode switch meta line confirms that the dual-mode design is non-destructive. Schools migrate from aggregate to detailed when they're ready; nothing forces them.
