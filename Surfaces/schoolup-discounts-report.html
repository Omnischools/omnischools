Why this layout

  • Page anchors on the total in the featured KPI: GHS 8,420.00 discounted this term, 5.5% of total billed, +0.7 points vs last term. The accountant gets the headline number and the trend in one card; the rest of the page is breakdowns and detail.
  • Four-tier breakdown row with colored top-bars per tier (gold for sibling, green for staff, terra for bursary, navy for scholarship). Each tier shows total amount, application count, and share-of-discounts bar. Sibling at 52% confirms it operating as designed — the largest tier is the auto-applied rule, not a manual policy.
  • Application timeline as a stacked bar chart. Stacked because each week's bar shows all four tiers contributing. Week 1 dominates — that's when invoices first issue and sibling auto-applies; subsequent weeks show case-by-case bursary trickling in. The shape tells the policy story without a written summary.
  • Top recipients list on the right ranked by amount. Staff children at 100% lead because their full fee is waived; Akosua appears at #3 with stacked discounts noted ("Sibling + Bursary"). Useful for board review — "who's getting the most help, are they the cases we intended?"
  • "Active applications" table as the load-bearing list. Every discount currently affecting Term 1 invoices, one row per application. Stacked discounts appear as separate rows (Akosua appears twice — sibling row and bursary row) because each is a distinct policy decision and audit subject.
  • "Term value" column shows the actual cedi amount that was discounted from this term's invoice — not the percentage in the abstract. A 50% discount on Akosua's GHS 280 tuition is GHS 140; that's the number the accountant copies into the P&L.
  • Applied date + by-whom in the same column. "auto · sibling rule" for system-applied; named admin for manual ones. Auditor reads the column and immediately sees governance — system rules vs human decisions.
  • Filter pills mirror the tier breakdown. Sibling 23 / Staff 4 / Bursary 7 / Scholarship 9. Click one to filter the table to just that tier — board members reviewing bursary applications click "Bursary" and see the seven cases with their applied dates.
  • "Stacked" tag in student meta when a student has multiple discounts. Discreet visual signal that this isn't a single-tier case; clicking through shows the full picture.
  • Forward-only model preserved. The report shows discounts that affected Term 1 invoices. A discount applied in Term 1 but configured for "this term only" won't appear in the Term 2 report. Period filter at the top governs scope.
  • Export CSV is the accountant's primary action. The accountant copies the totals into spreadsheet reconciliation; the application list is the supporting detail. Export PDF is for board meetings — same data formatted for paper.
