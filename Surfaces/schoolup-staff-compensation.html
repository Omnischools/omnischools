People & payroll

Bundles staff qualifications (the partial column in the GES annual census) with compensation tracking (which feeds the salary line on the P&L). Three salary statuses: school-paid (private), GES-paid (government-seconded teachers, public schools), or allowance-only (volunteer or part-time). The compensation total flows to the bookkeeping module's salaries category; GES-paid staff appear as a footnote, not on the school's books.

Why this layout

  • "School-paid · 17 of 17" tile shows that for Christ the King (private) every staff member is on the books. The denominator preserves the dual-mode design; a public school with 12 GES-seconded staff and 5 PTA-paid would show "5 of 17 · school-paid" with 12 GES-paid in a sibling tile.
  • Qualifications column with category-coded tags: degree (green), diploma (gold), cert (neutral), missing (warn). Three rows show "Missing" tags — Stephen Antwi, Robert Nuamah, Charles Boateng. Same 3 staff that the GES annual census flagged as "qualifications not yet captured." Single source of truth across modules.
  • Real Ghanaian universities throughout: UCC (Cape Coast), KNUST (Kumasi), UEW (Winneba), UPSA, OLA College, Accra Polytechnic. Specific institution + graduation year matches what GES expects on staff returns.
  • SSNIT and PAYE deductions in dedicated column. SSNIT = Social Security and National Insurance Trust (Ghana's pension scheme, 13% employee contribution). PAYE = Pay As You Earn income tax. Real Ghanaian payroll deductions; the lowest-paid staff (Kwame Otu, GHS 900) show "—" for both because they fall below the SSNIT threshold and tax-free PAYE band.
  • Subtotal row at the bottom shows gross GHS 31,700 and net GHS 24,800 — the GHS 24,800 net is what flows to the P&L salaries line on the bookkeeping dashboard. Cross-module integrity: this number = Salaries category in the expense entry aggregate mode (GHS 24,800).
  • "Missing data" filter pill in gold-tinted state with count "3". The same 3 staff identified across census + dashboard + here; admin can quickly filter to just the rows needing attention.
  • Public school context note in gold-tinted card explains how the same UI adapts for public schools — split hero, GES-paid status tag, footnote treatment. Honest about dual-mode design even when showing private school view.
  • Tenure column carries operational meaning: "11 years · since Sep 2014" — useful for retention analysis, end-of-year recognition, and the GES staff return that asks for years of service.
  • Three salary statuses (School / GES / Allowance) cover the full Ghanaian basic-school landscape. "Allowance only" handles part-time religious instructors, occasional external coaches, NSP volunteers — paid sometimes in goods rather than cash.
