Boarding · Surface 07

Boarding discipline & deboardinization.

The disciplinary ledger that sits alongside boarding operations. Five rungs from informal note to deboardinization, each with prescribed counter-actions, all signed by named staff, all append-only. Deboardinization — removal from the boarding roll — requires three co-signs and is reversible only by the Board. The point at which discipline first crosses into billing: three times the boarding fee per unauthorised day, generated as an invoice line item the moment the action is logged.

Architecture
DESIGN NOTES · BOARDING 07

Discipline is the most consequential routine action a school takes. A test score affects one transcript; a deboardinization changes where a child sleeps for the next year of their life. The surface treats the work with that weight: nothing is deletable, every action is signed by named staff, and the ladder is locked into the schema so that no school can quietly invent a sixth rung between Bond and Suspension.

The five rungs are schema-locked against the canonical General SHS Rules: Note, Warning, Bond, Suspension, Deboardinization. Each rung specifies its required co-signs — one for Note and Warning (HM alone), three for Bond (student, HM, Senior HM), two for Suspension (HM + Headmaster), three for Deboardinization (HM, Senior HM, Headmaster). The co-sign count is enforced at the database — a Deboardinization with only two signatures simply cannot be written.

The most surprising design choice is treating discipline as a cross-module trigger into billing. The "three times the boarding fee per unauthorised day" rule from the General SHS Rules creates an invoice line item the moment the action is logged, not at end of semester. The Headmaster has discretion to adjust the figure (PEN-2026-009 above was reduced from GHS 1,500 to GHS 510 at Head's discretion) but the adjustment itself is logged with a reason. This is the first time we've had discipline and billing share a record.

Deboardinization carries a special status: reversible only by the Board, never by the school. A Headmaster who imposes a deboardinization in week six cannot quietly reverse it in week eight if the student's parents apply pressure. The Board meets termly. K. Donkor's record above shows the Board review window in operation — a 14-year-old caught two days into the action, parents present, Head filing a motion for Bond reinstatement. The architecture lets that motion exist as a first-class object on the record, regardless of outcome.

The pastoral protection cross-reference is the humane layer over the punitive one. A. Quartey, escalated through the VLC pastoral flag system in the prior batch, appears here not as an offender but as a flagged student whose discipline route runs through the Dean instead. Near-incidents that would have produced a Note for another student are re-routed before they enter the ledger. The ladder pauses where pastoral cases run. This is a hard line: no child carrying social services involvement should accumulate disciplinary points from the boarding system the same way a peer would.

The bond-of-good-behaviour artefact is the most ceremonial element of the surface. The standard text is rendered in Fraunces serif because the document is meant to feel like what it is — a signed promise between three named people, archived against the student's file for the rest of their school career. The digital surface produces a printable copy; both the digital record and the paper copy carry the same identifier. The signing room is a room. The 14:30 appointment for G. Aboagye's bond happens at a table with three chairs and a witness; the software just makes sure the right people show up with the right paper.

Architectural decisions

  • 5-rung ladder schema-locked against the canonical General SHS Rules · no sixth rung permitted
  • Co-sign requirements enforced at the database · a Deboardinization with two signatures cannot be written
  • Append-only ledger · no infraction is deletable · corrections appear as additional rows
  • 3× boarding fee penalty per unauthorised day · cross-module trigger into billing · Head has discretion to adjust with reason logged
  • Reversible only by Board · deboardinization carries that status as a field, not a culture
  • Board review window first-class · motions filed and outcomes appended regardless of decision
  • Pastoral protection flag · students with active pastoral cases bypass the disciplinary ladder and route through the Dean
  • Bond as printable artefact · serif-rendered standard form, three signatures, paper and digital identical identifier
  • "Unauthorised return" detection · cross-references deboardinized roll against bunk-inspection roll at 06:10 each morning
  • House → HM chain · every infraction routes to the resident HM by default, Senior HM sees cross-house view
  • Auto-escalation rules · 3 Notes in a term → Warning · 2 Warnings in a term → Bond eligibility (HM decides)
  • Parent notification at Warning and above · SMS via the existing communications module
