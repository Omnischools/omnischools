Why this layout

  • Fees pillar in navy/gold dominates the overview row showing GHS 246,400 with "Automatic · no entry needed" tag in green. The biggest income source needs no manual work because it auto-flows from billing; this surface is about everything else.
  • "All other income" summary card on the right shows the GHS 7,840 broken down by 4 categories with mini swatches. Same colour vocabulary as the dashboard pie/stack. Joyce sees that non-fee income is 3.1% of total — meaningful context for how much effort to put into recording it.
  • Four income-type cards in 2×2 grid: Government subventions / Donations / Fundraising / Rental & misc. One card per income type rather than a single mixed list because each type has different operational rhythm and metadata (donors have names, fundraising has events, rental has counterparties).
  • Government subventions card has "Public schools" tag in green and shows empty state for Christ the King (private). The card always exists in the UI regardless of school type so the data model is unified, but the empty state explains why it's blank for private schools.
  • Donations card shows real entries with donor names and restrictions: "Mrs. Esi Mensah · alumna · class of 1998 · for library books / Anonymous donor · cash drop at office · no restrictions noted." Donor identity and restrictions both matter operationally — anonymous donations need different treatment, restricted gifts need to be spent on the right thing.
  • Fundraising card has gross/net handling implicit: "PTA term 1 levy · Voluntary contributions · 178 of 312 families." Real Ghanaian fundraising patterns — PTA levy participation rates (178/312 = 57%) tell a story about parent engagement.
  • Rental & misc card holds the most diverse entries: hall rental for weddings, bank interest, sale of old furniture. Realistic miscellaneous income at a Ghanaian basic school — these add up over a year.
  • Each card has a dashed-bordered "+ RECORD ..." action at the bottom for quick entry into that specific category. Category-specific entry rather than one generic "Add income" because the form fields differ by type (donations need donor name + restrictions; rental needs counterparty; subvention needs source agency).
  • Recent income table at the bottom shows ALL income across sources sorted by date (fees + non-fee mixed). Provenance column distinguishes auto-from-billing vs manually-entered: green "✓ Auto · billing" pill or neutral "Manual · Joyce" pill. Joyce knows what came from the system and what she had to type.
  • Realistic Ghanaian source detail throughout: "Akosua Boateng · JHS 2A · part payment of Term 1 fee" / "GCB savings interest · September" / "Hall rental · Saturday wedding · cash · receipt #218". Real names, real banks (GCB), real receipt numbering conventions.
