Six PLCs, one Friday afternoon

The NTC mandates 20 CPD points per teacher per year for licence renewal — and 8 of those must come from school-based Professional Learning Communities. PLCs are the weekly hour where the Maths teachers sit together to look at a sample of pupils' work, where the new teachers compare notes on classroom management, where the ICT integration group plans how to use the new lab. This surface is where the admin tells Omnischools what the school's PLC architecture looks like: how many PLCs, what type (subject / cross-cutting / new-teacher), who facilitates each, who's a member, and what they're working on this semester. Configure once. The weekly session register and CPD ledger inherit from here.

Design notes

  • Three types of PLC. Subject-based (the bulk), cross-cutting (voluntary, multi-department interest groups), new-teacher (mandatory for first-2-year teachers, supports NTC induction). Border colour distinguishes.
  • School-wide cadence is the default. Same Friday 3:30 PM for all PLCs makes the time protectable. Individual PLCs can override (ICT-in-Pedagogy meets biweekly) but the default forces alignment.
  • Facilitator pill on every card. Almost always the HoD for subject PLCs. For cross-cutting and new-teacher PLCs, a designated facilitator with the right experience.
  • Member gaps surface here. Sciences PLC has 11 of 12 — the new Physics teacher hasn't been added. Surfaces as warning on the card, not just a global counter.
  • Term focus is free-text. No template. Each PLC writes what they're working on this semester. Becomes the headline of the session register surface.
  • The CPD points contract is the bottom of this page on purpose. Reading order: configure the PLC architecture first, then see what teachers earn from it. The 0.5 attended + 0.5 reflection = 1 point model is the school's contract with the teacher — change it here, change it everywhere.

Schema this drives

  • plcs · one row per PLC instance · school_id, type (subject/cross-cutting/new-teacher), name, facilitator_staff_id, cadence_override
  • plc_memberships · staff in PLC · plc_id, staff_id, role (member/facilitator), joined_at, left_at (nullable)

Three types · why we don't just have "PLCs"
Editorial · the type distinction
Type 1 · Subject-based

"My department, every Friday"

The Maths department sits down to look at the Form 2 mock paper. Item 14 — the one about quadratic graphs — was missed by 60% of students across all four classes. The conversation is: why? And what do we do about it next week? Subject PLCs run on shared curriculum, shared assessments, shared subject knowledge.

The ICT-in-Pedagogy PLC has the English HoD, two Science teachers, one Maths teacher, the History teacher, and the ICT teacher. They share an interest, not a subject. Type 3 · New-teacher

"My first two years"

A teacher in year 1 has a subject PLC too — but they also need the New Teacher PLC. Different conversation entirely: how do you settle a class in 60 seconds? What do you do when a parent calls? How do you mark a stack of 35 essays without losing your weekend? Run by a senior Deputy Head, mandatory, supports the NTC's mandatory induction programme.

Why three types matter

  • Different rooms have different conversations. A new teacher asking how to handle a defiant Form 1 student in their subject PLC slows down the curriculum work everyone else needs. The new-teacher PLC is the right room.
  • NTC distinguishes induction PLCs. First-2-year teachers earn induction credit from new-teacher PLC participation — a separate track on the NTC portal from regular CPD. The schema needs to capture which type a session was.
  • Cross-cutting PLCs are voluntary. Anyone can opt in. Departments can't force their staff to attend ICT-in-Pedagogy. The membership flag matters for absence rules.
  • This isn't novel — it's GES guidance. The 2019 NTC PLC handbook recommends this three-type split. Omnischools's data model adopts it.
