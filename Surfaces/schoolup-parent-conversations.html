Why this layout

  • Sub-tabs across the parent record: Profile / Children / Billing / Conversations / Notes & flags / Audit log. Conversations is one tab among six — the parent record has many facets; communications is the new MVP2 addition.
  • Tone summary card at the top of the timeline in editorial Fraunces: "18 events across 3 channels — 11 outbound from school, 5 inbound from Patricia, 2 system. Last contact 2 hours ago. Communication is healthy." Tells Florence the story before the data; same editorial framing as the collection trend report.
  • 4-stat strip beneath the tone summary: Outbound (11) / Inbound (5, gold-italic, green) / Avg response time (2h 14m) / SMS cost (GHS 0.15). "Avg response time from Patricia · fast" is the operationally interesting metric — responsive parents are different from unresponsive ones, and that distinction matters when deciding who to lean on for fee collection.
  • 6 filter pills: All / WhatsApp (12) / SMS (3) / Email (1) / System events (2) / Internal notes (2). Channel-coloured dots match the locked vocabulary (WA green, SMS gold, email neutral). Florence can isolate just SMS to see what bills/reminders went out, or just WhatsApp to see the conversational layer.
  • Vertical timeline with 4 event types distinguished by left-anchored circle colour: gold (outbound from school), green (inbound from parent), neutral (system events), terra (failed delivery). Visual narrative of who's saying what at a glance.
  • Day markers as pill-style chips ("Today · Wed 22 Oct") with the day name in italic gold. Month markers with horizontal lines ("September · 5 events · scroll to expand") for chunking time visually. Schools think in days within a term and in terms across a year.
  • Each event has 3 parts: head (channel badge + meta + timestamp) / content (the actual message) / foot (delivery state + author/source). Inbound messages get a green-bordered content block visually distinguishing parent voice from school voice — same convention as the WhatsApp inbox.
  • System events visible inline: "Auto-routed to Joyce Owusu-Ansah · billing topic detected by inbox routing rules". The conversation history shows automation, not just human messages; if a parent later complains "you ignored my message," the audit trail proves the routing fired.
  • Internal notes preserved in timeline with gold dashed border + "Visible to admins only · not sent to Patricia" footer. Notes contextualise the conversation for the next admin who picks it up — Patricia's reliability note from Florence becomes part of the institutional memory.
  • Failed email + SMS fallback shown as paired events: "⚠ Bounced · invalid email" then 1 minute later "SMS fallback · ✓ Delivered". System resilience visible; admin sees that even when one channel fails, communication actually reached the parent. This is reassurance more than action.
  • "Part of broadcast to 312 families · open to view parent received" footer on the announcement event — connects this individual record to the cross-module delivery detail (file 3). One click drills from Patricia's view into the full broadcast's delivery report.
  • Right sidebar is sticky and shows Patricia's full context: 1 child (Akosua Boateng JHS 2A), billing snapshot (terra GHS 340 balance, green GHS 410 paid), communication preferences (preferred channel WhatsApp, English, quiet after 8 PM, SMS+WA opt-in, email missing), frequency check ("within healthy range · 11 of typical 8—15"), quick action buttons.
  • Communication frequency progress bar showing 11 of 15 (55%) in green. Visualises the "are we bothering them?" question: green range 0—15, yellow 15—20, red 20+. Florence knows at a glance whether Patricia is over- or under-contacted.
  • Communication preferences are loaded from billing module + onboarding: "Quiet hours · After 8 PM" means scheduled sends would respect that. Cross-module integrity; preferences set once apply everywhere.
  • "Email on file · Missing" in warn red tells Florence why the 6 Oct email bounced and why all subsequent communication has gone via SMS. Diagnostic context embedded in the relationship view.
  • Quick actions in dashed gold buttons: "+ NEW WHATSAPP MESSAGE / + NEW SMS / + INTERNAL NOTE". Three actions, three different scopes: outbound to parent on preferred channel, outbound on alternate channel, internal-only. The choice is intentional, not buried in a menu.
