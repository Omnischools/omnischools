Why this layout

  • Gold accent strip across the top. The first 6mm of the page reads as "this is from Christ the King" — provenance signal before any text is read. Survives photocopying, faxing, low-quality printer output.
  • School identity dominates the header. School name in Fraunces, school address and contact below. The receipt number sits to the right but visually subordinate. The school is the issuer; Omnischools is the platform.
  • School ID line ("GES-AC-JHS-0184") matters for the ministry pitch. Ghana Education Service registers schools with unique IDs; including this on every receipt makes the document audit-ready for any government inspection.
  • "Received from" / "For the account of" is the legal pattern for school receipts. The payer (guardian) and the beneficiary (student) are explicitly distinguished. This is how a parent claims tax/employer reimbursement.
  • The amount banner is the visual anchor. "GHS 340.00" in 28pt Fraunces with the in-words spelling next to it. Both required for legal records — numerals can be altered, words cannot. The "three hundred and forty Ghana cedis" line is a forgery defense.
  • Line items sum to the actual payment, not the invoice total. A receipt documents what was received in this transaction — GHS 340.00. The two line items (tuition remaining balance + books) sum to exactly that. The receipt does not subtract the previous payment from itself — that prior payment had its own receipt, and adding it here would double-count.
  • Prior-payment context in a footnote box, not in the totals math. Tells the reader: "an earlier GHS 60 payment was made; this GHS 340 settles the rest of the GHS 400 invoice." Provides full picture without cluttering the receipt body.
  • Three-cell payment method strip. Method (with phone for MoMo), Transaction ID (mono font for legibility), and the human who recorded it. Each cell answers a different question: how, what proof, who.
  • Stamp + signature line + name. The dashed circle is the school's stamp impression; in production schools upload a real stamp PNG. The "authorised officer" framing matches what GES inspectors expect to see.
  • QR code for verification. Anyone scanning lands on a public page that confirms the receipt is real. Defends against forgery — receipts can be photographed and edited; verification URL can't be faked.
  • Bottom strip is intentionally quiet. "Issued on Omnischools" is the only platform mention. Doesn't compete with the school's identity above.

Why this layout

  • Gold allocation summary band appears between the amount banner and the line items. Tells the reader at a glance: this single MoMo transaction cleared two invoices. The two invoice IDs are visible up front, before the per-line breakdown.
  • Line items sum to the GHS 440 paid in this transaction. Term 3 carryover (100) + Term 1 remaining balance (340) = 440. Not the sum of both invoice totals minus the prior payment — that math is correct but harder to read on a receipt. The receipt documents the GHS 440 transaction; the invoice page in-app shows the full historical math.
  • Same receipt number for both invoices. One transaction, one receipt — even if it cleared multiple invoices. Matches the parent's mental model of "I sent one MoMo payment, I got one receipt."
  • Footnote box explains the full settlement context. Tells the reader that both Term 3 and Term 1 invoices are now fully paid, and references the earlier GHS 60 payment that contributed to Term 1. Same pattern as the standard receipt — context lives in prose, not in the totals math.
  • Allocation summary uses the gold palette instead of the default white. Visual signal: this section is special; it explains what kind of receipt this is.
  • Header / payer / method / footer all unchanged from the standard receipt. Rule of thumb: layout consistency between receipt types, only the body section flexes.

VOID

Why this layout

  • VOID stamp angled diagonally, 5px terracotta border, 64pt Fraunces. Reads as a real ink stamp would on a printed receipt — the universal visual convention for "this is no longer a valid record."
  • Stamp opacity 0.85, not opaque. The original content is still readable underneath — auditors need to see what the original receipt said to understand what was voided. A fully-opaque stamp would obscure the audit trail.
  • Original receipt body slightly desaturated (55% opacity). Pulls visual weight away from the body, reinforces that the document is historical not current. Header and footer remain at full opacity so school identity is unmistakable.
  • Void banner above the header contains the reversal facts: when, who, why, replacement number. This is the most important text on the void receipt — it explains what happened. Terracotta treatment matches the danger color in the system.
  • Replacement receipt ID shown inline (RCT-2026-0578). Auditor can immediately follow the trail to the replacement. Makes the void recoverable as a story, not a dead-end.
  • "Reversed by MTN" replaces "Confirmed by MTN" on the transaction ID line. Single-word change that signals the chain-of-custody for the actual money — it went back to the parent through the same channel it arrived on.
  • Verify URL footer says "VOIDED". If anyone scans the QR or visits the URL, the public verification page also says voided — no way to mistake an old, voided receipt for a current one.
  • Same receipt number, not a new one. RCT-2026-0541 stays attached to the void event. Replacement is a separate document with its own number. This is how financial audit trails work — voids don't erase, they overlay.
  • The void itself is the fact being recorded. A void is a financial event with its own audit log entry (who, when, why). The receipt PDF reflects that event; it doesn't pretend the original payment never happened.
