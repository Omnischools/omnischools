Drafting view

  • Preamble is auto-filled from the register. Date, venue, chair, secretary, attendance stats — all locked from the attendance surface. Secretary doesn't retype.
  • Classify-as-you-go. Each agenda item is tagged Discussion / Action / Resolution. The classification cascades: actions extract to a table, resolutions extract to formal records, discussions stay as searchable narrative.
  • Action items need owner + deadline. Can't submit minutes with an action lacking either. The validator panel on the right surfaces this in real time.
  • Resolutions capture vote counts. For/against/abstain. Binding-flag is automatic for General PTA, optional elsewhere.
  • Distribution is per-meeting. Form PTA minutes go to that class's parents. House PTA minutes go to House parents. General PTA minutes go school-wide. The channel toggles let the Secretary tailor.
  • Two-stage approval. Secretary drafts → Chair reviews → adopted. Adopted minutes can't be edited; corrections happen by amendment in the next meeting.

Schema this drives

  • pta_minutes · one row per meeting · status, secretary_id, adopted_at, distributed_at
  • pta_agenda_items · structured list per minutes · classification (discussion/action/resolution), narrative text
  • pta_action_items · auto-extracted · owner, deadline, status, completed_at
  • pta_resolutions · auto-extracted · resolution text, votes_for/against/abstain, binding flag
02
Why three classifications · the downstream divergence
Editorial · what each classification does after the meeting
Classification 1

Discussion · narrative

Captures what was said. No commitments, no follow-through. Searchable record for posterity and context.
Example: "The class average rose from 64% to 71% in Term 2. Concerns raised about Friday afternoon attendance dropping. Parents asked the Form Master about possible interventions."
Downstream effect
Stays in the minutes record. No system actions triggered. Indexed for full-text search so future meetings can reference. No SMS, no nudges, no accountability.
Classification 2

Action · someone owns it

A specific commitment. One person owns it, one deadline, one outcome. Auto-tracked until done.
Example: "Mr Adjei to confirm Cape Coast trip date with the Headmaster by 7 June. Reminder SMS day-before. Status visible to Form PTA Chair and Secretary."
Downstream effect
Adds to owner's task list. Sends SMS reminder day before deadline. Surfaces as overdue if deadline passes without status update. Next PTA meeting's agenda auto-populates with outstanding actions.
Classification 3

Resolution · binding governance

A formal decision adopted by vote. Constitutes the PTA's collective will. May trigger financial, operational, or policy consequences.
Example: "Resolution Form-2-GA-A-2026-Q2-001 · Cape Coast trip funding adopted at GHS 150/student + GHS 1,600 from Form PTA surplus. Treasurer mandated to issue invoices by 5 June."
Downstream effect
Creates a numbered resolution record citable in future meetings. If financial, auto-creates the fee category and invoicing schedule. For General PTA, escalates to Headmaster for awareness. Cannot be edited after adoption — only amended by future resolution.

Why classify at all

  • Traditional PTA minutes file everything together. A typed page with all the discussion, all the decisions, all the actions in one block of prose. Nothing happens after filing.
  • Classification routes the output. Discussion → record. Action → tracker. Resolution → binding registry. Same Secretary, same notebook, different downstream behaviour.
  • It's also how the General PTA tracks Form PTAs. When a Form PTA resolves on something that affects the school (e.g., dues levy proposal), the resolution surfaces up. Discussions don't.
  • The Secretary doesn't choose lightly. A "resolution" tag means a vote was held — vote counts are mandatory. If there was no vote, it's discussion or action.
