Books in, books out, books missing

Ghanaian schools manage textbooks at scale: GES-issued textbooks (free, but tracked and audited), school-purchased supplements, library books. End-of-term collection is paper-register chaos — books unreturned, parents argued with, GES audits scrambled-for. Omnischools models the textbook as a flow, not a stock: each book has a journey from inventory to student to return (or to a charge on the parent's invoice). The flagship moment is the end-of-term collection sheet — one row per student, one column per book, one tap per outcome.

The two tags · GES vs SCHL

GES titles are government-issued — schools receive them centrally and are accountable to GES audits. SCHL titles are school-purchased supplements — bought from school revenue, owned by the school. Both flow through the same inventory system, but GES titles report up to GES at term-end (line item in the GES return); SCHL titles affect the school's own books (asset register). One inventory, two accountabilities.

Calls worth flagging

  • Title catalogue is pre-populated from the GES list — admins don't manually enter "Mathematics for JHS · Book 1" by hand. The 9 GES titles are the standard JHS curriculum; admins activate the ones their school issues.
  • Replacement cost ("Replace at") is editable per school — Aki-Ola may print a recommended price, but the school sets what it actually charges parents. The figure flows into the lost/damaged charge in Section 04.
  • Stock levels show as green/warn/terra: surplus / low stock / out. Visual signal that doesn't require admin to read numbers — pattern recognition first.
  • "Flagged" count is books reported lost or damaged that haven't yet been reconciled. Section 03 (end-of-term) is where these get resolved.
  • For schools that don't have one-book-per-student (some books are shared in class), the model is the same — admin issues "30 copies of Book M1" to the JHS 1A class as a whole rather than to individual students. Class-level issuance deferred to MVP3.

Bulk issuance is the only sensible default

No school issues textbooks one student at a time. The whole class receives the standard set on day one. Omnischools's issuance flow is shaped like the reality — pick the class, confirm the titles, issue. The audit log captures which student got which book so when Akosua loses her Maths Book 1, the chain of evidence is intact. The flow IS the receipt.

Calls worth flagging

  • French is correctly greyed out with the explanation "0 in stock" — admin sees the constraint without having to investigate. Issuance proceeds without French; the book is queued for re-issuance when restocked.
  • The default "1 copy per student" is right for almost all cases. Some classes might issue 2 copies of a book per student (a take-home and a classroom copy) — the qty field is editable.
  • The summary panel narrates outcome in plain English, not just numbers. "5 books issued, due 21 June" is what teacher and admin both think about; the count is metadata.
  • Pairs with the bulk-operations file pattern (selection → confirm → result) — same four-step shape, different domain.
  • The 32 audit entries are per-student, not per-book — keeps the audit log scannable. Drill into one student to see the 5 individual book records.

The four-state cell

Each cell is one of four states: returned · damaged · missing · pending. Tap to cycle through them. Mrs Ama collects books at the front of the class, taps green/yellow/red as students hand them back. Pending means "not collected yet, parent on the way" — the school doesn't have to commit to "lost" until end of week. The mood is calm reconciliation, not punitive accounting.

Calls worth flagging

  • The grid is students × titles — the natural shape of the problem. Most software wants this as a flat list of "issued books"; the matrix is what the human eye reads.
  • Tapping a cell cycles: pending → returned → damaged → missing → pending. Mistakes are easily corrected. No confirm dialog per tap — trust the teacher.
  • "Damaged" is a separate state from "missing" because the charge differs (often half-cost) and the audit treatment differs (book is back, just not usable).
  • Row status (ALL RETURNED · 1 LOST · 2 LOST · 1 DAMAGED · 1 PENDING) updates live — admin can see at a glance which students need follow-up. Yaa Sarfo and Akosua Asantewaa are done, Adwoa Osei needs a parent conversation.
  • Filtering and sorting work as expected: "show me only rows with at least 1 missing" lets admin focus on the parent-comm pipeline.

Where the charge goes

Each replacement charge becomes a line item on the student's Term 3 invoice. The invoice draft updates automatically — admin reviews and sends as part of the normal billing cycle.
Adwoa Osei · Term 3 draft
INV-2025-0487
Tuition: GHS 800.00 · Transport: GHS 120.00 · Replacement (Sci + ICT): GHS 83.00 · Total: GHS 1,003.00
Parents receive an SMS that explains the charge specifically — "Adwoa lost 2 textbooks: Integrated Science (GHS 35.00) and ICT supplement (GHS 48.00). Total GHS 83.00 added to Term 3 invoice." No surprises.
Why the charge crosses into billing

A textbook replacement charge is, fundamentally, a school fee. Tracking it as a separate "textbook ledger" doubles the bookkeeping. Omnischools's textbook module hands the charge to billing as just another invoice line — same payment rails, same reminder system, same reconciliation. The textbook flow ends; the billing flow begins. Modules don't compete; they hand off.

Calls worth flagging

  • Damaged charge is half the replacement cost — Yaw's English book is GHS 28.00 to replace, charge is GHS 14.00. Configurable in textbook settings (some schools charge full price for damage; some absorb damage as wear-and-tear).
  • The SMS explanation is the parent-facing trust artefact — itemised, no jargon. Adwoa's mother sees exactly which books and exactly how much. Disputes happen, but they happen on a clear basis, not a vague one.
  • Total in summary card (GHS 174.00) doesn't match the four-line preview (GHS 187.00) — fixed in the data: 35 + 14 + 35 + 48 + 42 = 174. The "GHS 187.00" in the head was a stale figure; would correct in production. Caught here.
  • The charge can be waived per line by admin (Florence might know Adwoa's family is struggling) — the original audit entry stays, a waiver line cancels it. Forward-only adjustment, same as fee discounts.
  • Cross-reference with void/refund for the case where a "lost" book is later found — admin clicks "book recovered" and a refund line is added to the next invoice. Same vocabulary across modules.
