Sync to the regulator

The National Teaching Council's portal is the official record of CPD points · the school keeps its own ledger, but at the end of the month every entry needs to flow to the NTC where licence renewals will be assessed. This surface is the pipe between Omnischools's ledger and the NTC portal. Pending queue, sync history, error queue with retry logic, manual override for entries the portal rejects, and reconciliation between what Omnischools says and what NTC says. If the NTC portal disagrees with Omnischools, Omnischools is wrong by definition — the portal is the regulator. The reconciliation view is how the school catches drift early.

The sync architecture

  • Daily automated push at 02:00 AM. Off-hours, low contention. The batch ships every queued entry. Sync state on each ledger entry flips QUEUED → SYNCED.
  • Manual push for urgency. When a workshop certificate arrives or a teacher is about to renew, the admin can trigger an out-of-cycle push. Audit-logged.
  • Failed entries don't block the batch. One bad entry doesn't fail the whole push. Successes commit, failures move to the failed queue with retry counters.
  • 3-strikes on retry. Each failed entry gets 2 auto-retries (overnight). After 3 attempts the entry is locked for manual intervention. Prevents infinite loops on schema errors.
  • The reconciliation step is monthly. Pulls NTC's view of cumulative points back and diffs against Omnischools. Catches drift early. Most months the delta is just "pending tonight's push" and there's nothing to do.
  • Schema versioning is brittle but visible. When NTC updates their API (rare but real, ~2x per year), Omnischools will catch the mismatch on first failed entry and flag the version. Auto-patch deployed within days, retry resumes.

Schema this drives

  • ntc_sync_batches · per push · batch_id, scheduled_at, completed_at, status, entry_count, success_count, failure_count, duration_ms
  • cpd_points_ledger.ntc_sync_status · enum: QUEUED, SYNCED, FAILED · plus ntc_sync_batch_id and ntc_response_code
  • ntc_sync_failures · per-failed-entry · ledger_entry_id, error_code, error_message, attempt_count, resolution_status
  • ntc_api_health · 5-minute health checks · timestamp, response_ms, status_code · drives the API HEALTHY pill

Why this exists · the NTC is the regulator
Editorial · the integration boundary
The integration boundary

Omnischools's ledger is operationally useful. The NTC portal is what the regulator looks at.

The teacher's licence renewal isn't decided by Omnischools. It's decided by the NTC, looking at whatever's in their portal at the moment of renewal application. If Omnischools shows 20 points and NTC shows 18, the licence renewal proceeds with 18. This is the architectural reality: Omnischools is the operational ledger, the NTC portal is the regulatory truth. The job of this surface is to keep the two in sync continuously rather than scrambling at year-end.

The NTC's portal API didn't exist at the time we designed the first version of Omnischools. This surface is built against an assumed API — the schemas, error codes, and batch model match what the NTC has publicly described in their 2024 digital licence renewal roadmap. When the real API ships, Omnischools will adapt. Until then, the fallback is the same one schools use today: monthly PDF export from Omnischools, manual upload to the NTC portal by the Headmaster.

The reconciliation step is the most important part. Most integrations fail silently — entries don't make it across, but no one notices until renewal time. Active reconciliation (Omnischools pulls NTC's current state and diffs against its own) means drift is caught within a month, not within a year. The admin sees "we have 1,247, they have 1,206" and either understands why (queued batch) or investigates (something's wrong). The diff is the alarm.

The integration policy

  • NTC portal is source of truth for licence decisions. Omnischools's ledger is operational; the portal record is what counts.
  • Daily push, monthly reconciliation, yearly audit. Three rhythms, each catching different categories of drift.
  • Fallback is manual PDF. If the API is down or unavailable, monthly PDF export → manual upload preserves the workflow.
  • Audit-logged everywhere. Every entry's sync state is audit-tracked. Every manual override has an actor and a reason. Every failed entry has an error code and resolution path.
