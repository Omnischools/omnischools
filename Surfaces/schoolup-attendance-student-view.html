Why this layout

  • This is a tab on the student profile, not a separate page. Profile head with avatar, name in big italic Fraunces, ID tags, and the tab strip below — Overview / Attendance / Grades / Billing / Achievements / Documents / Communication. Attendance tab has the badge-mini "!" dot to flag the active issue.
  • "Open follow-up →" button in the profile head is a primary action because admin opening this page during meeting prep often wants to refresh the follow-up drawer with new info. The drawer is one click away from anywhere on this page.
  • Five regions, top to bottom, each numbered. 01 At a glance · 02 Term register · 03 What the system noticed · 04 Flags & follow-ups · 05 Communication log. Reading order matches meeting prep: headline → details → patterns → history → conversations.
  • Region 1 — Headline card with terra-tinted background because this student is currently a problem case. The hero stat "61%" in extra-large terra Fraunces. Three supporting stats: streak count, vs last term, class rank. "Class rank 36 / 36" is brutal but accurate — James is the lowest-attendance student in his class. The headmistress will need to know this when she meets Mary.
  • "Below 70% threshold" pill under the headline number ties the percentage to the flag system. Same colour, same vocabulary as the dashboard. The reader instantly knows this is an automatic flag, not a subjective assessment.
  • Term toggle ("Term 1 · current / Term 3 / Term 2") top-right of the region head. Headmistress can pivot to see how James was in earlier terms. Last term was 79% — context for "is this a new problem or chronic." (It's new.)
  • Region 2 — Term register as week strips, not the desktop's grid. Each week is one row: week label on the left (Wk 7 · 28 Apr – 2 May · P 1 · A 4), seven day tiles to the right (Mon Tue Wed Thu Fri + faded Sat Sun). Larger tiles than the teacher's grid because we have one student to show, lots of canvas.
  • Day tiles are saturated colour blocks with day-num in big Fraunces + day-name + status word. James's current week is a wall of terra blocks with one green Monday at the start. Visually striking. The today indicator (gold ring) is on Friday's terra block.
  • Hover popover on each day shows what was marked, why, who marked it. "Marked at 8:08 AM by Mr. Mensah." Auditable detail without crowding the visible surface.
  • Holiday weeks rendered as full row of hatched tiles (week 4, Eid). Visually distinct from absence — "school was closed" vs "James didn't come." This matters because the term decline pattern in Region 3 references the holiday as a contextual landmark.
  • "5 earlier weeks · scroll to load · Wks 1–2: 10 of 10 days present" truncation note. The page doesn't render all 10 weeks because the recent ones are what matter for meeting prep. The 100% start of term is summarised in one line. Compression where compression is OK; the recent weeks remain detailed.
  • Term summary strip below the register in 5-cell layout: 29 present / 1 late / 0 excused / 17 absent / 47 total. "0 excused" in warn 0 is significant — none of James's absences have been formally excused with a reason. They're all "no contact" absences.
  • Region 3 — Pattern analysis as 3 cards: Day-of-week / Term decline / Notable streaks. Each card has a label, headline, detail paragraph, and visual element.
  • Day-of-week card with vertical bar chart showing absences by day of week. Tuesday and Friday tower over the others (75%, 60%) in terra; Wednesday is tiny (15%) in green. The visual makes "James misses Tuesdays and Fridays" undeniable. Specific finding: "Tuesdays and Fridays show highest absence" with concrete fractions.
  • Term decline card with line chart — green start (Wk 1: 100%), gold dashed line at the Eid week, terra line declining through the post-holiday weeks. The mid-term break is annotated as the inflection point. "Sharp drop after mid-term break" as the headline tells the story before the chart confirms it.
  • Streaks card lists all 3+ day absences chronologically. Active 5-day streak in terra, two past 3-day streaks in warn. Each row shows date range and reason. The historical streaks contextualise the current one — James has done this before, but never for this long.
  • "Past · 3d · overlapped with Eid · partial holiday" shows the system understands context. The April 9-11 absence wasn't a real 3-day streak; it overlapped with the Eid break. Surfacing this prevents the headmistress from over-counting the pattern.
  • Region 4 — Flags card with three rows. Active flag (terra, with critical pill, currently the focus) takes the active-flag styling — terra-tinted bg, terra left border, indented 3px. Two resolved flags below in the standard row treatment.
  • Each flag row shows: type, severity, opened/resolved dates, follow-up count, last action. Auditor scanning the column knows which flags fired when, what was done, what closed them. The arrow at the right indicates these are clickable into the follow-up drawer.
  • Resolved flag attribution: "auto-closed on return" is the system being honest — the flag closed automatically when James came back, not because Florence explicitly resolved it. Different from "Resolved by Florence Addo"; both valid, both audit-trailed.
  • "Consolidated into the long-absence flag for unified follow-up" on the Below 70% flag explains why two separate triggers became one workflow. Two flags fired simultaneously; admin handled them as one. The system tracks the consolidation.
  • Region 5 — Communication log is the thickest section. Nine entries spanning the term. Auto-sent SMS (3 PM absence notices), admin-sent SMS (Florence to Mary), inbound WhatsApp from Mary, phone call attempt logs, internal notes from class teachers. Every conversation about James's attendance, in one place.
  • Filter pills: All / SMS / WhatsApp / Phone / Notes. Default All shows everything chronologically. Filtering helps when admin wants to review just the WhatsApp thread or just the phone attempts.
  • Each communication has icon + type + direction + message + attribution + time. Icons distinguish channel (gold SMS box, WA-green W for outbound WhatsApp, white-with-green-border W for inbound, navy phone, neutral note). Direction pill (Outbound / Inbound / Internal) in tiny caps.
  • Inbound message from Mary (Mon 28 Apr) styled differently — WA-green left border, light green bg. Visual distinction between "what we said" and "what they said." The most recent inbound is two weeks ago — a long silence that the headmistress will want to ask about in the meeting.
  • Internal note from class teacher (Fri 18 Apr · "James seemed quiet on Friday") is the early signal. Mr. Mensah noticed something off two weeks before the system flagged anything. The communication log preserves human observations alongside automated activity; this is the kind of context that turns a meeting from "James has missed days" into "James has been struggling since mid-April."
  • Time stamps in mono with relative day below. "Thu 1 May · 2:30 PM" gives the explicit calendar reference; "2:30 PM" relative gives the human anchor. Audit precision and human readability both served.
  • Auto-sent SMS attribution explicit: "auto-sent at 3:00 PM · delivered" distinguishes system messages from human messages. The headmistress meeting Mary won't claim "I texted you 5 times" when actually the system did 4 of those — the log shows what's automated and what's personal.
  • "In reply to fee_reminder template" on Mary's WhatsApp message ties it to its trigger. She wasn't proactively reaching out about James's attendance — she was responding to a fees reminder, and mentioned attendance as an aside. Context that matters: Mary doesn't initiate contact about attendance issues.
