What this surface is

  • The journal accumulates over a year. Each session's reflection joins the stream · entries are append-only · no editing, no deletion, no backdating.
  • FM pastoral notes sit alongside the journal · separate visibility (FM + Dean only) · the student does not see them · they're FM's record for FM's work.
  • PG observations are time-stamped and signed. They feed into the character paragraph but are visible to FM only (not to other PGs, not to other students, not to the student themselves).
  • Auto-draft of the character paragraph regenerates after each session. Uses reflections + notes + observations · FM owns the final wording · paragraph goes on the school-leaver letter, not the academic transcript.

Visibility matrix

  • Student: their own reflections, read-only · cannot see FM notes, PG notes, character draft
  • Form Master: everything · adds notes · finalises character paragraph
  • Dean of Students: reads notes (case oversight) · cannot edit · cannot delete
  • Headmaster: sees finalised character paragraph only · no journal access
  • Parent / guardian: sees nothing on this surface · they receive the character paragraph at school-leaver
  • Peer Guides: log their own observations · cannot read others'

Why append-only · why private · why nine months
Editorial · the architectural choices that make the surface work
The shape of the journal

A journal that cannot be edited is a journal that can be trusted.

Three design choices on this surface are non-negotiable, and each is doing real work.

Append-only. The student cannot edit a previous entry. Not even a typo. Not even a sentence they're embarrassed by next week. This is the point. A reflection edited in retrospect becomes a performance for a future reader — the student starts writing what they wish they had felt, instead of what they did feel. Append-only forces the entry to be a snapshot of that Wednesday afternoon, and only that Wednesday afternoon. Across twenty-two snapshots, you get something a polished essay could never produce: a record of how a person moved.

Private to student + Form Master only. The student writes for an audience of two: themselves, and Mr Mensah. Not the parent. Not the head of house. Not the headmaster. Not the future employer. Not other students. This narrow audience is what makes truth-telling possible. A reflection a parent might read becomes a reflection a parent wants to read. A reflection a peer might see becomes a performance. Two readers — and one of them is the writer themselves — is a manageable audience for a fifteen-year-old. More than that is not.

Nine months. Not one session, not one term — a full academic year. The journal's value is cumulative. A single entry tells you almost nothing about a person. Twenty-two entries across nine months, with the absences and the short entries and the long entries and the FM notes between them, tell you something a CV cannot. When the school-leaver letter is written and the character paragraph is needed, the FM is not improvising — they are summarising a document that wrote itself.

The pastoral note layer is the FM's working notebook. It sits beside the journal but is structurally separate — different visibility, different lifecycle, different purpose. The journal is the student's record of becoming. The notes are the FM's record of attending. Both are needed. Neither replaces the other.

What we deliberately don't do

  • No "engagement score" on reflections. Length is not depth. A 28-word entry from a grieving student is not a worse reflection than a 200-word entry from a comfortable student.
  • No keyword detection or AI summarisation of journal contents. The FM reads each entry weekly. A machine extracting themes would distort how the FM holds the student.
  • No analytics on what students wrote. Trends across the school stay at the level of attendance and submission rate — never content.
  • No exports of journal text outside the system. The FM can view, the FM cannot bulk-download. Reduces the surface area of accidental disclosure.
