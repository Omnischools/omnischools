Teacher view philosophy

  • One subject, one cohort. Mr Asiedu teaches Chemistry to 28 F3 SCI candidates. The view is the people on his roster, not the school.
  • His own marking. Mock 1 and Mock 2 Chemistry grades were marked by him personally — the cohort he sees is the cohort he assessed. Diagnostic value beats grade inflation risk.
  • Countdown to paper, not to last paper. 24 days to Chemistry, not 36 days to last WASSCE paper. The teacher's clock is the subject clock.
  • 100% credit rate is the floor. Mr Asiedu's cohort has not seen a sub-credit (D7–F9) in Chemistry since 2023. The strongest subject in F3 SCI.

Intervention plan rules

  • Three tiers, not five. Urgent · Focused · Consolidate. Too many tiers fracture attention.
  • Each tier has its own time budget. Tier 1 students get 8 hours of teacher time; Tier 3 gets 0 hours of teacher time (peer-tutor only).
  • Calendar shows the journey, not just the destination. Six remaining lessons + boost session + paper day.
  • Parents are looped in once per week, not per intervention. Less noise, more signal.
  • Plan exports as PDF for the HOD & Head of Academics — Mrs Owusu-Ansah co-signs it.

Benchmark design

  • Four levels · my cohort, school, region, national. Each level grounds the next.
  • Every column carries a source line. Provenance shown explicitly so the teacher knows what is direct measurement versus interpolated.
  • Region is the weakest source · honestly labelled. WAEC's regional summaries do not consistently publish subject-level breakdowns; the WR figure is directional, marked with a warn-coloured dot and a caveat note.
  • National anchored on WAEC chief examiner report. Released annually after WASSCE marking · the strongest external benchmark available.
  • Multi-school pooling is the long-term fix for region. Once 5+ Omnischools schools sit in the same region, the WR figure can be derived from anonymised cohort data rather than interpolation.
  • Teacher's own record visible. Three-year cycle · feeds NTC licence review.
  • Not for parent eyes by default. The teacher's own performance view.
