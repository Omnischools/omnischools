Process design

  • Five steps; not more. If the process feels longer than this, the parent is being given too much information.
  • School owns 1–4. The school files, follows up, schedules, and accompanies. The parent and clinician own step 5 (fit-to-sit confirmation).
  • Active step is animated. The current step pulses; done steps are checked; pending steps are quiet.
  • Reference number on the card. WAEC's ref number is what the parent quotes when asking about the case.
  • Make-up is at WAEC, not at school. Stated explicitly so the parent doesn't expect Yaa to sit again at the school.

The thread design

  • Chronological, top-down. Earliest event at the top; today's last scheduled event at the bottom.
  • "Called you" not "called the parent". Second-person tone; the parent is reading her own thread, not a record about a third party.
  • Each event shows who, what, channel, status. The parent never has to ask "did you call me?" — the answer is in the log.
  • SMS verbatim quoted in quotes. Phone-call notes are summarised, not verbatim.
  • Scheduled events are visible. The parent knows what is coming next; they are not surprised by the 21:00 SMS.

Questions you might have
Will Yaa fail because she missed the English papers?
No. The SC-12 special-consideration process is exactly for this. WAEC will reschedule the three English papers within the WASSCE window. Yaa's aggregate calculation will include the English grades from the make-up sitting, not zeros.
When will we know the make-up sitting date?
After the fit-to-sit confirmation on Mon 1 Jun. WAEC then schedules the date and notifies the school and you via SMS. Typically 5–10 working days after the confirmation.
Will Yaa be able to sit Mathematics on Wed 3 Jun?
That depends on the matron and clinician's assessment on Mon 1 Jun. If Yaa is well enough, she sits at the school centre as scheduled. If she is not, the matron will file an additional SC-12 for Mathematics. Mrs Owusu-Ansah will update you on Mon 1 Jun by phone.
Is the school open today and tomorrow?
Yes. The school is fully operational and Yaa's classmates are sitting their papers. You do not need to come to the school for anything; everything that involves you happens by phone or via this portal.
What does the projected aggregate of 10 mean now?
It is the same projection from Mock 2 in March. We do not adjust it for the missed papers — we wait for the make-up sitting to score and update with real data. If Yaa scores the same B3 in English at the make-up that she did in Mock 2, the aggregate of 10 holds.
How will I know about the make-up papers when they happen?
You will get a phone call from Mrs Owusu-Ansah at least 48 hours before, and a follow-up SMS confirming the date, time, and transport plan. On the day, the school will collect Yaa, accompany her to Sefwi-Wiawso, and bring her back. You will be informed after each step.

Why this view ends here

  • Readiness statement is the receipt. Parent agreed in March; the page surfaces what she agreed to and the signed PDF.
  • Four info cards · the practical context. Hospital, NHIS, school contacts, WAEC details. Nothing the parent has to dig for.
  • FAQ is the longest section by intent. Most of what a parent worries about can be answered without a phone call.
  • No benchmarks, no cohort data. The parent's view never compares Yaa to others. That is the school's analytical work.
  • End the parent view with reassurance, not action. The closing FAQ is what we want them to read last.
