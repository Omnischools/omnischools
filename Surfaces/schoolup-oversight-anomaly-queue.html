Omnischools Oversight · where signals get worked

The queue of things to check.

The dashboards surface a short list of flagged signals; this is the full queue behind them. The anomaly engine runs rule-based heuristics over every nightly sync — enrolment spikes, attendance collapse, fee outliers, performance drops, coverage gaps — and raises an anomaly when a rule trips. The queue is where a district director triages them: each anomaly carries the rule that raised it, a severity, and a status that moves from new through review and assignment to resolved or dismissed. Crucially the engine is rule-based and explainable, not a black box — every anomaly can state exactly why it fired. The demo is the Wassa Amenfi West queue, nine open anomalies, with the Samreboi cluster the dashboards have pointed at all along.

A queue, not just a list of alarms

  • The full queue sits behind the dashboard shortlists. The district dashboard showed four flagged signals; this is all nine, with the rest of the engine's output. The dashboard answers "what is urgent today," the queue answers "what is outstanding" — and gives each signal a status that moves through triage rather than just blinking.
  • Every anomaly states the rule that raised it. PTR-ESC-30, PERF-DROP-15, COV-GAP — a named, inspectable rule on every card. The engine is rule-based heuristics, not an opaque model; an anomaly that cannot say why it fired could not be triaged, and could not be trusted.
  • Severity and status are separate axes. Severity is how much it matters (high/medium/low); status is where it is in the workflow (new, in review, assigned, resolved, dismissed). A low-severity fee outlier and a high-severity PTR breach both need a status — the queue tracks both dimensions because triage needs both.
  • Correlated anomalies are linked, not listed twice. Samreboi's PTR breach and its BECE drop carry a "correlated" tag pointing at each other — the engine recognises they are likely one root cause. A queue that showed them as two unrelated cards would invite two separate, half-effective responses.
  • Some anomalies resolve by onboarding, not investigation. The coverage-gap card says it outright — "resolved by onboarding, not investigation." The queue is honest that not every flag is a problem to investigate; some are a reminder to close the platform's blind spot.
  • Dismissal is a first-class outcome. The 90-day KPI counts 6 dismissed "as expected" — the Dadwen attendance dip may simply be the cocoa harvest. A queue where everything must be a crisis would train directors to ignore it; dismissing-with-reason keeps it credible.

Triage turns a flag into a worked case

  • The cluster is the unit of work, not the single anomaly. Samreboi's three signals are triaged as one case A-0218, because they likely share a root cause — three unfilled vacancies. Working them separately would produce three partial responses; the cluster view forces one coherent fix.
  • The rule is shown as actual readable logic. IF ptr > 30 AND enrolment up AND staffing flat — the director sees the exact conditions, confirms Samreboi met all three, and judges the signal themselves. This is what "rule-based, not a black box" means concretely: the heuristic is legible.
  • Triage has explicit stages. Raised → reviewed → assigned → resolved-and-verified. The case sits at "assigned, in progress" with an owner; a regional director glancing at it sees not an unattended alarm but a managed case. Status is a workflow, not a label.
  • Resolution requires verification, not just a fix. The final step closes only when a later sync shows PTR back within band — the engine confirms the fix worked. An anomaly cannot be marked resolved by assertion; the data has to agree.
  • The queue rolls up the tiers. The same cluster appears in the regional and national anomaly queues attributed to the district — consistent with how the dashboards roll up. A regional director sees Wassa Amenfi West has a known, owned case rather than an unexplained weak spot.
  • This completes the anomaly queue. The full triaged queue (section 1) and a worked cluster case (section 2) — the engine the dashboards have drawn on all along, now shown as the place signals are actually resolved. The second surface of Batch 3.
