Why the vitals are a timeline

  • A sickle cell crisis is not a snapshot. It evolves over hours. A single reading can read "fine" while the underlying crisis is brewing.
  • The current row is gold-tinted — at a glance Matron sees what's "now" without re-reading the timestamps.
  • Sick Bay Prefect F. Tetteh took the intake. The Matron's 09:14 confirmation is the medico-legal anchor — the Council's scope of practice is clear that clinical assessment must be by the registered nurse.
  • The chronic flag sits in the patient header where no Matron, doctor, or covering staff can miss it. SCD students cannot be treated like a generic ailment patient.

  • The Matron documents her working impression — what she clinically thinks. She does not write "diagnosis"; that's a doctor's word.

Append-only log. Once a dose is recorded it cannot be deleted — only corrected with a footnoted amendment. The witness column is required for any drug taken from school stock by a non-Matron staff member; this is the N&MC double-signature standard adapted to Sick Bay Prefect supervision.

Why three drug-source tags

  • Standing — pre-approved in the sickbay configuration. Matron can give without doctor authorisation. Paracetamol, ORS, malaria first-line.
  • Scheduled — patient's own chronic medication, surrendered to sickbay on arrival per Ghana boarding protocol. Hydroxyurea here. Dispensed at her usual time, not a clinical decision.
  • PRN — "pro re nata", as-needed. Specific criteria must be met (pain ≥4/10 here). Each PRN dose creates an audit trail showing the criterion was satisfied.
  • If a fourth tag — Doctor-ordered — appears, the consult timestamp is hyperlinked from that row. Nothing untraceable.

Why discharge is a checklist, not a feeling

  • Matrons rotate shifts. Mrs Antwi takes over at 17:00. She needs to know exactly what "ready for discharge" means for this patient, not "Mrs Bediako thinks she looked fine."

  • If the 4th criterion fails at 16:00, the parent is re-notified before 18:00. The three-tier rule doesn't end at admission; it covers stay extensions too.

The two parent notifications

  • A phone call is not enough on its own. Mrs Mensa is at Asankrangwa market; she might have answered while distracted. The SMS gives her something to re-read at home.
  • The SMS is short, plain, and ends with "Reply CALL to request callback" — that's the safe-channel back to the Matron. A parent never has to guess how to reach the sickbay.
  • The HM and Headmaster notifications are medical-detail-light. The Headmaster's morning digest line says "SCD pain crisis · mild" because he needs to know there's a sickbay event, not the granular vitals.
  • The four cross-links mean any future Matron — or any auditor — finding this record can reach the chronic register, today's attendance, the boarding roll, and the billing trail in one click each. No re-keying student names. No silos.
