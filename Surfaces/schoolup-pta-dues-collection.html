Dues that aren't tuition

PTA dues are not tuition. They're not boarding fees. They're not feeding fees. They're their own thing — collected by elected parent Treasurers at four different tiers, with their own dues schedules, their own audit, their own collection reports. Omnischools models PTA dues as a separate fee category that lives alongside Tuition, Boarding, Feeding, and Exam fees in the billing module. The Setup tab handles dues configuration (with forward-only change posture). The Collection tab gives Form, House, and General PTA Treasurers the daily-use view: who's paid, who hasn't, how much is outstanding, what to chase. Same billing engine, separate accountability, different people doing the chasing.

Setup tab

  • One category, four tier-rates. PTA dues is a single fee category. Each of the four tiers has its own rate (or "no dues"). The category isn't broken into four categories because they're conceptually one thing — parents pay PTA dues, just at different tiers.
  • Tiles match the PTA structure setup. Same four tiers, same on/off state, same amounts. This screen is the financial face of the governance screen. Change in one place reflects in both.
  • Forward-only is reinforced. Every change in the audit log records "effective from" date. Past invoices keep their original amounts. Cancelled or restructured tiers preserve old invoices.
  • Change log is append-only. Same pattern as discount changes elsewhere in Omnischools. Audit, audit, audit.

Collection tab

  • Summary strip is the morning glance. Treasurer opens, sees expected vs collected, week-on-week direction. If outstanding is creeping up, the aging tile tells them to escalate.
  • Per-tier cards drill into instances. Form PTA shows 20 classes ranked by collection rate. The laggard surfaces. The Treasurer knows which class to walk into next.
  • Aged escalation is its own block. Anything outstanding > 30 days gets the warning treatment · also reveals if the family has fees outstanding in other categories, because the chase strategy differs.
  • SMS bulk send is one click. "SMS all outstanding" composes a templated reminder, sends to all 23 families, audit-logs the send. Single SMS each.
  • Instalment plans are visible. Mrs Bediako responded last time and is on a plan. The action button changes to "Plan" instead of "SMS" so the Treasurer knows the conversation is in progress.
  • Cross-category awareness. Mr Opoku owes PTA dues AND tuition. The PTA Treasurer can see this even though tuition isn't their remit · helps them prioritise who to escalate.

Schema this drives

  • pta_dues_invoices · one row per parent per term per tier · amount, status, paid_at
  • pta_dues_payments · payment events linked to invoices · MoMo refs, manual receipts
  • pta_dues_reminders · audit of SMS reminders sent · prevents reminder fatigue
  • pta_dues_plans · instalment plan records · agreed amount per instalment, target dates

Why a separate category, not a tag on tuition
Editorial · the architectural call
The architectural call

PTA dues are structurally different from tuition. They needed their own category.

It would have been simpler to tag PTA dues onto the existing tuition fee structure — one less category to maintain, one less ledger line. We chose not to. Tuition is collected by the school's bursar and goes into school operating accounts. PTA dues are collected by elected parent Treasurers and go into PTA accounts that are not the school's money. They fund different things, get spent by different decision-makers, and answer to different audit chains. Conflating them would have created problems no UI affordance could fix.

The setup tab makes this concrete: PTA dues live in the fee-category sidebar alongside Tuition, Boarding, Feeding, and Exam fees — visible as one of six sibling categories. The collection tab is treasurer-shaped (per-tier breakdowns, aging by family), not bursar-shaped (per-term reconciliation against operating budget). Same plumbing, different accountability.

Reason 1 · accountability
Different people own the money

Tuition: school bursar. PTA dues: elected parent Treasurer. The PTA Treasurer must be able to see exactly what's collected without seeing tuition. Separation is a permissions boundary.

Reason 2 · audit
Different audit chains

Tuition flows audit through GES financial audits. PTA dues flow audit through PTA Treasurer's report at AGM and the General PTA's own oversight. One commingled ledger would make either audit impossible.

Reason 3 · spend governance
Different decision-makers

Tuition spend is the Headmaster's authority. PTA spend is the PTA's authority — by resolution. The Cape Coast trip is paid from PTA dues because the parents voted to fund it. Conflating funds would conflate authority.

The fee-category model

  • Six categories total. Tuition, Boarding, Feeding, Exam, PTA dues, Other. PTA dues is one of six. Equal citizen of the billing module.
  • One invoice can span multiple categories. A parent's term invoice might show tuition GHS 2,500 + boarding GHS 1,800 + Form PTA GHS 50 + General PTA GHS 200 = GHS 4,550 total · with category-level breakdown on the receipt.
  • Payment can be category-allocated. If parent pays GHS 4,000, the bursar allocates first to tuition (per school policy), and PTA dues can remain outstanding even when tuition is paid. The Treasurer sees PTA outstanding cleanly.
  • This was the right call even though it cost a schema table. The alternative (PTA tag on existing invoices) would have made every collection report into a complex query and every audit into a guessing game.
