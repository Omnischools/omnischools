Operating in the active state

  • Two cards, two stories. Inpatient (terracotta border, day counter) is the higher-stakes worry; returning (warn border, ETA timer) is the wind-down. Visual rhythm matches the operational rhythm — Mrs Bediako knows at a glance which deserves her next phone call.
  • The seven-line card pattern. Diagnosis, hospital, transport, NHIS, parent, status, expected back. Every active referral fills the same seven lines. Anything missing is a real gap — not a UX gap. Forces the right operational discipline.
  • NHIS card number is visible. Matrons present it physically at ER. Mono-font, copy-pasteable, visible without drilling. The most operationally important number on the page.
  • Cross-module strip is honest. Attendance gets the fact, HMs get the fact, billing gets the cost — none of them get the diagnosis. Privacy bleeds outward only where it has to.

Cross-module handoff
Sickbay → Billing → Comms
The matron does not chase money. When a referral incurs an out-of-pocket cost, three things happen automatically:
1.
Billing module creates the line item against the student's account with a "sickbay referral" tag. Parent sees it on the next invoice.
2.
Comms module sends a one-line SMS to the parent at the moment of incurring: "Referral today incurred GHS 80 for cast materials. NHIS-covered items are 0. Details on your statement."
3.
Reconciliation surface (here) shows the open balance, age, and SMS history. The Bursar opens this when the over-30 list grows.
Matron, Bursar, parent — three audiences, one source of truth. No spreadsheet, no double-entry, no "did the parent get told". The SMS at moment-of-incurring is the rule that closes most of the disputes before they start.

The reconciliation discipline

  • Most lines say zero. Ten of twelve recent referrals carry no parent-due cost. NHIS works. The reconciliation surface exists for the two-out-of-twelve where it doesn't.
  • "Within window" is gentle. Today's K. Boateng GHS 80 is not a chase. Adwoa's GHS 45 from last week is not a chase. They appear here because they're open — not because they're overdue.
  • Over-30 changes the tone. J. Tetteh's GHS 215 has been open for 22 days; at 30 the SMS escalation script changes from courteous reminder to formal demand, and at 60 the Bursar adds a phone call. The age column is the operational signal, not the amount.
  • NHIS card health is preventive. The 92.3% coverage rate is fine; the 83 students with expired-or-expiring cards before 2026/27 are the actionable number. Sending an SMS now saves real referral money in three months.
  • The matron never sees billing. Mrs Bediako can read this surface (her name appears in the audit) but the bursar owns it. Sickbay creates the cost; billing carries it. Clean handoff, clean separation of concerns.
