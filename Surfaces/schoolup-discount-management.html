Omnischools · Discounts

Fee discounts, made tractable

Schools handle a handful of recurring discount types — siblings, scholarships, staff children, hardship cases. Omnischools models these as reusable tiers defined once, then applied to students. One-off arrangements use a custom path. Three screens: tier templates, applying a discount, and the student billing view that shows the cascade.

Discount tiers

Define the discounts your school offers once. Apply to specific students from their billing page — or use a custom amount for one-off arrangements.

How stacking works

When a student qualifies for two discounts (e.g. sibling 50% + staff child 50%), the rates compound rather than add up. 50% then 50% again gives 75% off, not 100%. This protects the school from accidental full waivers and keeps the math predictable.

Why this layout

  • Tiers as reusable templates. Schools have 3-5 standard discount types; defining them once lets admin apply repeatedly without re-typing rules. The card layout makes the catalogue scannable.
  • Each tier carries its full rule set — percentage, what it applies to, duration, approval needed, documentation required. The card answers every question the admin would have at the moment of applying it.
  • Variable percentage tier (Bursary). "Var" in italic — admin sets the actual percentage at the moment of applying. Necessary because hardship cases differ by circumstance, but they share the same approval workflow and documentation requirements.
  • Color coding by discount character. Sibling (gold, default), Staff children (green, free attendance), Bursary (terracotta, case-by-case sensitivity), Scholarship (navy, formal). Visual distinction reinforces the conceptual differences.
  • Stack rules block at the top with a worked example. The 50%+50%=75% math is non-obvious; surfacing it before the tier list prevents misuse. The toggle lets schools opt out of stacking entirely.
  • "Applied to N students" footer tells the admin which tiers are actively used. Useful for end-of-year review — the early-payment tier shown archived had 0 applications, justifying its retirement.
  • Archived tiers stay visible at reduced opacity. Historical record matters — a parent might ask "did you offer the early-payment discount last year?" and the answer is in the catalogue.
  • "Add a discount tier" as a dashed card matching the path-tile pattern from the empty states. Consistent affordance for "create new" across the product.
  • "View applied" page header button goes to a list of every active discount application across all students — for end-of-term audit.

Why this layout

  • Tier picker shows pre-qualified context. "Sibling discount · auto-applied · 1 sibling on file (Kofi, JHS 1B)" tells the admin this discount already exists for Akosua because her sibling is in the system. They don't need to re-apply it; the picker shows reality.
  • Custom one-off is the last option. Visually muted ("define below" instead of a percentage). It's the escape hatch, not the default — most discounts should use a tier.
  • Bursary tier reveals its own fields below the picker — discount amount (because variable), applies-to, duration, case note (required), document upload. Different tiers reveal different fields. Sibling tier wouldn't show case-note input because it's automatic and rule-based.
  • Live preview shows the cascade with existing discounts. "Sibling discount (already applied) −GHS 140.00" lets admin see the full picture before stacking. Surface the math at the moment of decision; admin doesn't need to do mental arithmetic.
  • "Subtotal after sibling" line in the preview makes the stacking order explicit. The new discount is applied on top of the post-sibling number, not the original gross. This matches the stack-rules toggle from the tiers page.
  • Document upload is optional but available. Attaches to the audit log entry, not visible on the receipt or invoice. Schools that want to keep records (death certificate for bereavement bursary) can; schools without upload capability still proceed.
  • Forward-only application is explicit. The footer note tells admin this discount applies to future invoices only. Voiding existing invoices to reissue at the new rate is a separate, more deliberate action.
  • Apply button shows the percentage and tier name — "Apply 40% bursary" — anchoring the action to the specific decision being made.

Why this layout

  • Discount pill in the student header tells admin at a glance that discounts apply. Without this, you'd have to scroll into the billing detail to know — easy to miss when reviewing many students.
  • "Active discounts" panel as its own section, not buried in the invoice line items. The discounts are policy decisions about this student; they deserve their own surface where admin can see, manage, and remove without going through an invoice.
  • Each discount row carries its provenance. Sibling discount shows "Auto · per term" in gold; bursary shows "Year · Florence Addo" — who applied it and on what cadence. Auditor reads the row and sees the full lineage.
  • Remove button is danger-styled on each discount. Removing a discount is a deliberate act with audit consequences (next term's invoice will issue at the higher rate). Visual treatment matches the consequence.
  • Fee cascade card shows the math. Each step labelled: subtotal → first discount → after first → second discount → net billed. Indented sub-lines with "→" arrows make the order explicit.
  • "After sibling" intermediate subtotal shows the stacking base. Bursary 40% applies to GHS 180.00, not GHS 320.00 — this is non-obvious and the cascade makes it visible. Without this step the math reads as 50% + 40% = 90%, but the actual saving is 66.25% (GHS 212.00 of GHS 320.00).
  • Across-the-year strip at the bottom projects the savings forward. Helps headmistress understand the financial impact of the policy choices she's made — GHS 636 across three terms is real money to track.
  • "Term 2 / Term 3 projected" assumes the discounts auto-renew at current rates. If a discount is set to "this academic year only" the projection shows; if "this term only" the future terms revert to gross. The system handles the projection correctly per discount duration.
  • Two CTAs in the header ("+ Apply discount" and "+ Record payment"). The most common student-billing actions, both discoverable from the top.
