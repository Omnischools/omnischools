Omnischools · SMS copy library

Every message, written tight

SMS is the most expensive channel in the product and the most critical for reaching parents on feature phones. Every message in this library has been written to land in 1 SMS segment (160 characters) wherever possible, identify the school by name, give the parent a clear next step, and treat their attention as borrowed.

Standard segment

160chars
GSM-7 alphabet. Going over splits to 2 segments and doubles cost.

Per-message cost

~GHS 0.05per send
Hubtel / mNotify pricing band, single recipient. Rate negotiable at scale.

School broadcast

~GHS 30.00per send
School of 400 students × 1.5 guardians average = 600 recipients per blast.

Sender ID

CTK-JHS
Up to 11 chars, registered with the gateway. Built from school short-name.

The seven principles behind every message

  1. Identify the school in the body, not just the sender ID. Parents receive SMS from many sources; they need to know in the first three words.
  2. Land the action in the first 50 characters. Many feature phones truncate previews; the gist must arrive before the cut.
  3. One SMS, one ask. Mixing "fees due" with "PTA Saturday" creates a message that does neither well.
  4. Use plain ASCII. No emoji, no curly quotes, no special bullets. Some feature phones render them as garbage.
  5. Always include a contact path. A short URL, a callable number, or a person to ask. Never end with "contact the school" alone.
  6. Refer to the student by first name. "Akosua's fees" is warmer and more legible than "your child's fees" or "the student's fees".
  7. Match urgency to channel. An achievement endorsement doesn't need an SMS — that's an in-app notification. Reserve SMS for things that actually need a phone to vibrate.
A
Transactional
System-triggered · individual
Sent automatically by the system in response to a specific user action. Recipient expects them, often within seconds. These prove that the system did what it said it would.

Sent when admin adds a guardian during student creation or import. Contains the magic link for first sign-up.

Template
{student_first}'s school has added you as her guardian on Omnischools. Tap to confirm and see her grades, fees & school updates: omnischools.gh/i/{invite_token} - {school_short}
Notes
Leads with student's first name + "her/his school" — recognition before brand. Closes with school name in plain text because some feature phones don't render the sender ID prominently. Pronoun (her/his) varies by student record. Do not use "your child" — feels generic and sometimes is wrong (e.g. grandparent receiver).

Notes
Deliberately generic — no school name, no user name. OTP messages are autofilled by phones; fewer variables = fewer autofill failures. Says "OTP" explicitly, not "code" — most Ghanaian banking and telco SMS use "OTP" as the term, so users recognise it instantly and don't hesitate. The "don't share" line is the standard security anti-phishing nudge required by most regulators.

C
Broadcast
School-triggered · mass
Sent to many or all guardians at once. Highest cost per send and highest scrutiny — the messages most likely to draw complaints if they feel like spam. The product surface should make schools think twice before sending these.
C1 · School-wide announcement
Admin-composed announcement broadcast to all guardians. The product caps message length and shows estimated cost before sending.
Broadcast
From CTK-JHS · 4:30 PM
PTA meeting moved to Saturday Oct 5, 9 AM in the assembly hall. Please confirm attendance: omnischools.gh/n/pta-oct5 - Christ the King JHS
Delivered to 600
Length
135chars1segment
Recipients
All guardians (~600)
Cost
~GHS 30.00per send
Composition
Free-form admin text with appended school name footer. Admin types the body into the announcement composer; system enforces 140 char body limit (leaves room for "- {school_short}" footer to keep total under 160). Counter and cost estimate visible while typing.
Notes
Schools with announcement budget concerns can disable SMS broadcast and rely on in-app + email only. Confirmation modal before send shows: recipient count, estimated cost, character count. Admin must explicitly confirm. The product makes this slightly inconvenient by design.

Class label first ("JHS 2A:") so parents who get messages from multiple class teachers can pre-sort. Footer is teacher's title+last + school short — different from school-wide which is school only. Identifies the human author.

Reserved for genuine emergencies — early closure, weather, security incident. Bypasses normal opt-out preferences.

Length
121chars1segment
Recipients

Behaviour
Bypasses SMS opt-outs. A parent who turned off all notifications still receives this — by signing up, they consented to emergency notices. The "URGENT - " prefix signals urgency in the preview. No URL — emergencies should be self-contained.
Notes
Triggering an emergency notice requires two admin approvals for schools larger than 200 students — prevents accidental fires. The compose UI is visually distinct (red treatment) so admins know they're in the high-stakes flow.
