Twenty points, one year at a time

NTC requires 20 CPD points per teacher per year for licence renewal. Of those, 8 must come from PLCs. The rest come from workshops, online courses, mentoring, conference attendance, action research. This is the teacher's own ledger — running total, source breakdown, what's already counted, what's still needed, and the gentle math of "are you going to make it by 30 September." The ledger is also a permanent record · three years of points roll forward into the licence renewal evidence pack. Built for the teacher to glance at on the way to work, not for the auditor to pore over at year-end.

Design notes

  • The target hero is the most-glanced piece. Teacher opens this surface to answer one question: "am I going to make it?" The hero answers in one line: 14.5 of 20, 138 days, on track. Everything else is detail.
  • Source breakdown shows capped vs uncapped. PLC has an 8-pt cap (matching NTC rule). Workshops, online, mentoring, conferences are uncapped. Tiles communicate the difference with a CAP pill.
  • The ledger is read-only and append-only. Most entries auto-post (PLC, attendance-driven). External entries (workshops, online courses) require evidence upload — certificate, completion proof.
  • Three-year cycle is the big picture. Annual target is the immediate concern; cumulative 60-pt cycle is the renewal concern. Year 1 complete is a green tick — Mr Mensah's already in good shape.
  • Gap analysis is opinionated. It doesn't just say "you need 5.5 pts" — it suggests the 4 most realistic paths given Mr Mensah's specific situation. Mentoring suggests Mr Yeboah because they're in the same PLC.

Why 20 and 8 · the NTC rules in plain language
Editorial · the regulatory frame
The rules Omnischools enforces

20 points a year. 60 over the 3-year licence cycle. 8 of every 20 must come from PLC.

The National Teaching Council sets the framework. Every licensed teacher must accumulate 20 CPD points per year across recognised activities. The licence cycle is 3 years — so 60 cumulative points across that cycle to renew. At least 8 of every year's 20 must come from school-based Professional Learning Communities, which is why this batch puts the PLC programme first. The remaining 12 are flexible: workshops, online courses, mentoring, conferences, action research, professional body participation.

Omnischools's job is not to invent the rules. It's to make them legible. The teacher should never have to ask "how much have I got" or "what counts" — the ledger answers both. The school's job (Headmaster, HoD) is also helped: they can see across the staff who's at risk and intervene early. The NTC's job is helped too: the sync surface (file 5 in this batch) pushes the synced ledger to the NTC portal so the regulator doesn't depend on year-end paperwork.

The 0.5 + 0.5 split on PLC sessions is a Omnischools design choice, not an NTC rule. NTC says PLC participation earns points; it doesn't prescribe how each session is weighted. We weight half the point on attendance (you showed up) and half on reflection (you thought about it). This rule is in the school's PLC settings and could be changed if a school disagreed — but the default makes the participation contract clear.

NTC rules · what the platform enforces

  • 20 pts/year mandatory. Falling short two years in a row puts the licence at risk. Teachers see this; HoDs see this on the school dashboard.
  • 8 pts/year from PLC. Hard floor. A teacher can't compensate by going to extra workshops if they skip PLC.
  • 3-year cycle. 60 cumulative pts. Year-on-year over-performance carries forward through the cycle but doesn't reduce the per-year floor.
  • NTC sync is the proof. Synced entries are the official record. A pending entry is real but provisional until pushed.
  • Evidence required for external entries. Certificate upload mandatory for workshops, courses, conferences. PLC entries auto-evidence from the session register.
