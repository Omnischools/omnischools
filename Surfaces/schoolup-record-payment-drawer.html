Why this layout

  • Drawer over modal. The billing table stays visible behind a dimmed overlay — admin sees what they're working from. Closing the drawer doesn't lose the page context.
  • Student-level context, not invoice-level. A payment now starts from the student and flows down to whichever invoices it covers. Akosua has 2 open invoices totalling GHS 440.00 — Term 1 partial plus a Term 3 from last year still outstanding. Real Ghanaian school reality.
  • "Change ↺" still pivots to a different student without closing — important when a parent calls about a sibling mid-conversation.
  • Amount entry comes before allocation. Admin types the amount the parent paid; the system then helps allocate. Reverses the typical flow of "pick invoice, type amount" because parents pay round numbers, not invoice-specific amounts.
  • Quick-fill chips reflect the multi-invoice reality: "All open GHS 440.00" pays everything, "Term 1 only GHS 340.00" addresses just the current term, "Half" for partial-and-progressing parents.
  • Allocation list shown empty until amount is entered — no work to do yet, but admin sees what invoices exist. The hint text below explains what'll happen.
  • Auto-allocation policy: oldest invoice first (FIFO). Most parents paying multiple terms are catching up on the oldest balance first; this matches the implicit intent and reduces clicks.
  • Each invoice row shows its overdue/partial badge right next to the balance. The Term 3 from 2025 says "Overdue 142d" so admin knows that even though the parent says "this is for Term 1," the priority should be settling the oldest first.

Why this layout

  • Auto-allocated to oldest first. GHS 440.00 entered, system applied GHS 100.00 to the overdue Term 3 carryover, GHS 340.00 to Term 1 — both settled. Admin can override per-row by typing in the amount field on the right.
  • Allocation row turns gold when amount is assigned. Visual reinforcement that this invoice is being paid; mirrors the gold of payment-related affordances throughout the product.
  • "Auto-allocate (oldest first)" action sits at the top right of the allocation list — for when admin has manually edited and wants to reset to the default policy.
  • Allocation summary trio (Payment / Allocated / Unallocated) makes the math visible and balanced. GHS 440.00 paid, GHS 440.00 allocated, GHS 0.00 unallocated — green confirms the books match.
  • Invoice rows preserve their overdue/partial badges through the allocation step. The 142d-overdue tag is a constant reminder of why this invoice is being prioritised.
  • MoMo TXN ID is required for MoMo, optional for cash. Each method has its own validation rules.
  • Phone number captured for MoMo because it's the only thing that ties a Omnischools record back to MTN's confirmation SMS the parent received. Critical for dispute resolution.
  • SMS toggle on by default with the guardian's name and phone shown. Admin sees exactly who will be notified before submitting.
  • Pre-flight summary in gold reads back the consequences of submission for each invoice. No surprises. The two "settled" lines are explicit about what changes.
  • Submit button confirms the total ("Record GHS 440.00 payment") not the line items. Anchors to the parent's experience: they paid GHS 440.00, that's the canonical number.
  • "Save as draft" matters for multi-line because the call from a parent is often "I just sent you GHS 440.00 via MoMo" before they have the TXN ID — admin can record student + amount + allocation, save, return when the TXN comes through.

Why this layout

  • Success is celebratory but brief. Big green check, the amount and student name in a Fraunces line — feels like an acknowledgment, not a brag.
  • Receipt preview shows the allocation breakdown. "↳ Term 3 · 2025 · GHS 100.00" and "↳ Term 1 · 2026 · GHS 340.00" make the multi-line nature explicit. Total at the bottom matches the parent's GHS 440.00 paid — both views of the same truth.
  • Sub-line says "2 invoices settled" to confirm the multi-line outcome up front, not buried in the receipt.
  • Two action tiles for the immediate next steps: download PDF (admin's records) or email (parent without phone). Both optional — the SMS already went out.
  • Activity strip confirms the SMS fired. Important for trust — if the admin saw the toggle "send SMS" but doesn't see confirmation it actually sent, they'll wonder. Explicit closure removes the doubt.
  • Background table updated. Both Akosua rows now show GHS 400.00 paid in green, status "Paid". Reinforces that the payment is real, not just promised.
  • Single receipt for multiple invoices. Parent gets one receipt with two line items — matches their experience of one MoMo transaction. Avoids the confusion of receiving two SMS messages for one payment.
  • "Record another payment +" is the primary footer action. Admins recording payments come in batches.
  • No "edit this payment" affordance in success state. Mistakes happen — but corrections go through the audit trail (a void + new entry), not a silent edit. Protects the record.
