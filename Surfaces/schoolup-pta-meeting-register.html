Live attendance state

  • Two registers, side by side. Teachers on the left (smaller, faster to mark). Parents on the right (longer list, search and filter helpful).
  • Three states per attendee. Present · Late · Absent. The fourth state is "unmarked" (still expected, no signal yet). At meeting close, unmarked converts to Absent.
  • Officer tags surface in the register. Mr Adjei's "Form PTA Chair" tag matters because his presence affects quorum and his attendance is reported up to the General PTA. Tags are pulled from the officer matrix.
  • RSVP context shows in the unmarked rows. "RSVP yes · awaiting arrival" tells the secretary this person was expected and might still come. "RSVP no" tells them not to chase.
  • Quorum changes the action set. Below quorum: "SMS the absent" suggested. At quorum: "Close attendance and proceed" enabled. Binding-resolution UI gates on quorum.
  • Walk-in flow. A parent who didn't RSVP can still be added via "+ Add" at the bottom. Same for teachers who weren't invited but turned up to listen.
03
Why two registers, not one combined list
Editorial · the GES reporting requirement
The reason · GES reporting needs both

Teacher attendance at PTA meetings is a separate compliance metric from parent attendance.

GES tracks parent engagement (parent attendance rate) and teacher participation (teacher attendance rate at PTA meetings) as two different signals. A school where 80% of parents show up but only the Form Master attends is a different school from one where 50% of parents show up alongside 4 subject teachers and the Headmaster. Combining them into one register would lose this distinction.

There's also a practical reason: the secretary marking attendance treats teachers and parents differently. Teachers are a small, fixed list known in advance. Parents are a longer list with arrivals scattered across the meeting's opening 20 minutes. Different UI ergonomics. Two registers, displayed side by side, makes both fast.

Teachers register

Small list, marked once

Typically 1-4 teachers per Form PTA meeting (Form Master + invited subject teachers). House PTA usually 1-2 (Housemaster + matron). General PTA can be 8+ if the full leadership team attends. Small, fixed, known in advance — secretary marks once at the start.

Parents register

Long list, marked over time

Form PTA: 20-40 parents. House PTA: 100-300. General PTA: 500-2000. Long lists, arrivals staggered, search and filter become essential. The secretary marks throughout the first 20-30 minutes of the meeting. Late arrivals flagged distinctly so the minutes can note them.

The two-register model

  • GES counts both. Termly returns from Omnischools will populate both metrics into the GES district return.
  • The Oversight tier will surface both. When the district director looks at PTA compliance, they want to see "school X · parent attendance 67% · teacher attendance 92%" — two numbers, not one blended one.
  • Different UI patterns for different lists. Teachers: tap to mark. Parents: search + filter + tap. Same data model, different display.
