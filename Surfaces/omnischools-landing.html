About Omnischools

Built here, for the way schools work here.

Most school management systems are designed somewhere else, then translated — fees in dollars, terms that don't match ours, gradebooks that have never heard of BECE. Omnischools is the opposite: every workflow was designed against how a Ghanaian school actually runs, then the software was built to match.

That means mobile money is a first-class payment method, not an add-on. It means the gradebook understands a WASSCE qualification rate, not just an American GPA. It means attendance can be taken on a phone with patchy data and sync later. And it means the data stays in Ghana, governed by Ghanaian law — because for a product holding children's records, where the servers sit matters.

Three products, one platform: Basic for KG/Primary/JHS, Senior for SHS with boarding, and Oversight — the GES dashboard that lets districts, regions and the Ministry see the system whole.

No. STPSHS is WAEC's regulator ledger — it captures three terminal weighted-total figures per subject across F1, F2, F3, and that is its purpose. Omnischools is the school's operating system underneath: the five-category in-term score ledger (assignments, mid-semester exam, end-of-semester exam, project work, portfolio) that produces those terminal figures, plus everything that depends on the same data — parent report cards, the WASSCE predictor, Oversight aggregation. At term end Omnischools generates an STPSHS-ready printable score sheet from the ledger, with Assessment Reference IDs in the order STPSHS's per-subject capture screen expects, so single-entry to STPSHS is as fast as the regulator's interface permits. If WAEC adds bulk-upload or API capability, the export becomes machine-to-machine; the rest of the workflow is unchanged.

Yes, completely. Omnischools supports three capture paths for the SHS score ledger and the teacher chooses per (subject × class × semester). Path A auto-compiles the semester's scores from in-semester Omnischools entries. Path B scans the teacher's existing paper ledger and extracts scores with verification-first OCR — every cell is teacher-confirmed before commit, low-confidence cells visually flagged. Path C is direct digital entry on the grid for teachers who prefer typing. We also offer a free Omnischools-branded ledger book with pre-printed student names and QR codes per page for higher OCR accuracy, but your existing book works too. The paper ledger is a feature, not a transitional artifact we are trying to phase out — three ways to keep the same five categories underneath.

SSP is NaCCA and CENDLOS's AI-assisted lesson-planning tools for teachers, backed by T-TEL and the Mastercard Foundation and distributed to roughly 84% of SHS teachers through Professional Learning Community handbooks. It serves a different purpose than Omnischools: SSP is a productivity tool for the teacher's planning workflow, while Omnischools is the school's operating system for the operational workflow around students. We do not build a competing AI lesson-plan generator — that is NaCCA's domain, not ours. Where it helps, we link out to the official tools at curriculumresources.edu.gh from the teacher's planning surface. Two tools, two purposes — no overlap intended.
