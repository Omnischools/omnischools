Omnischools · Statutory filing · Annual census

The annual, larger

The termly return is operational; the annual census is structural. Filed once a year (typically September), it captures everything in the termly return PLUS infrastructure (classrooms, latrines, water, electricity), detailed staff records (qualifications, certifications, GES vs private), special needs enrolment, age-by-class breakdowns, and dropout patterns. It's the document that informs national education planning and budget allocation. Two states: the generation drawer (longer checklist than the termly return) and the multi-page PDF preview.

Why this layout

  • Drawer follows the same 4-section pattern as the termly return (year/identification/checklist/output) so admin who's filed the termly return already knows the flow. The differences are scope — bigger checklist, longer manual list — not structure.
  • "The big one · filed once a year" lede sets the tone honestly. The annual census is significantly more work than the termly return; admin who's never filed one before should know what they're getting into. 10 pages, 16 sections, ~20 minutes of manual entry — concrete expectations.
  • Year selector simpler than termly return's term selector — there's only one current academic year. "Filing for: Academic year 2025/26 · Term 1 in progress · day 47 of 187" gives temporal context. The "census window: 1 Sep — 31 Oct" reminder is operationally critical because filing late triggers GES penalties.
  • Identification preview mirrors the GES form fields exactly: School name / GES School ID / District / Circuit / Region / Year established. Six fields in a 2×3 grid, mono for ID and year. Same identification pattern as the termly return — admin learns it once, applies it everywhere.
  • Auto-fill progress band as the headline. "71% of sections auto-filled · 5 sections still need your hand · estimated 20 minutes." Big italic gold "71%" in display Fraunces, with a green-and-hatched progress bar showing auto vs manual proportions visually. Admin sees the workload before drilling into details.
  • The progress bar uses two textures: green solid for auto, warn-coloured hatched for manual. The hatched pattern is the same as the manual-fill blocks in the PDF — visual consistency across the document and the drawer. Admin sees "29% hatched" in the bar and recognises that as the same pattern she'll see in the PDF preview.
  • Checklist organised into 5 sections (A through E) mirroring the GES census form structure. Section A: Enrolment & demographics / B: Staff / C: Attendance & academic / D: Infrastructure / E: Programmes & resources. Each section header in italic Fraunces gold with a horizontal rule trailing — visual divider that matches GES form conventions.
  • 16 checklist rows total, far more than the termly return's 8. The longer list is honest about the breadth of the census without being overwhelming because of the section grouping. Admin can scan section by section rather than facing a wall of rows.
  • Three status states: Auto (green) / Partial (warn-bordered) / Manual (warn-tinted with manual tag). "Partial" is new vs the termly return — used for staff qualifications where 8 of 14 are captured but 6 need filling. Honest about partial completeness rather than calling it Auto or Manual.
  • Section A: Enrolment & demographics — 5 rows, 4 auto + 1 manual (special needs). The auto rows show specific numbers in their meta lines: "312 students · 9 classes · 157 boys, 155 girls" / "+18 admissions · −6 withdrawals · ±5 transfers" / "14 repeaters across 9 classes." Inline data verification at the checklist level.
  • Section B: Staff — 4 rows showing the system's partial coverage of staff data. Teaching staff list is auto (names, roles, gender, subjects); qualifications are partial (8 of 14 captured); non-teaching is auto (3 staff); salary status is manual (not in Omnischools). The granularity of the breakdown is meaningful — admin sees that staff data is mostly there but not entirely.
  • Section C: Attendance & academic — 3 rows, all auto. "Attendance rate · last full term: 93.5% · current term to date: 93.8%" is the annual figure that the termly return only touched on briefly. BECE results auto-pull from the previous academic year. Academic performance auto-fills.
  • Section D: Infrastructure — 3 rows, all manual with warn-tinted bg. Classrooms, water/electricity/sanitation, library/ICT/kitchen. This entire section is manual because Omnischools doesn't track facilities. The visual cluster of warn-tinted rows in this section makes the workload concrete — "this is the section that takes time."
  • Section E: Programmes & resources — 2 rows, both manual. Feeding programme and textbook availability. Smaller section but important operationally; both are funded externally (feeding by gov, textbooks by GES) and the census records what's actually being delivered.
  • Output card with terra PDF tag matches the termly return drawer. "A4 portrait · 10 pages · CTK_GES_Census_2025-26.pdf." The 10 pages is honest — the termly return was 6, this is 10, admin knows what to expect.
  • Filing deadline reminder: "submit two copies before 31 Oct 2025" in the output footer. Operational urgency — annual census deadlines are statutory; missing them has consequences. The bold deadline date sits at the bottom of the drawer where admin's eye lands before clicking Generate.
  • Drawer footer: "11 of 16 sections auto-filled" with italic gold "auto-filled". Compresses the checklist into one digestible fact, same pattern as the termly return's footer ("6 sections auto-filled · 1 left blank" updated). Different denominator (16 vs 7) reflects the bigger document.
  • What's not in the drawer: No options to skip sections (you file the whole census, not parts of it). No alternate output format (GES expects PDF). No upload-to-GES button (SRIMPR portal upload is outside Omnischools's scope; admin uploads the generated PDF themselves).

Why this layout

  • 10 pages instead of 6. Annual census needs more sections than the termly return. Each page focuses on a coherent topic — cover, identification + enrolment, age + repetition, special needs + movement, teaching staff, infrastructure (2 pages), attendance + BECE, programmes + textbooks, declaration + filing.
  • Cover page anchors the document. Larger crest, "Annual Census" title, academic year, school identification, then a 4-stat summary (enrolment / teaching staff / attendance / established). The cover stat block lets the District Officer triage the census in one glance before turning the page.
  • "Established 1962 · 63 years" stat is small but meaningful. Schools with long histories carry institutional weight; the District Officer reading dozens of these can sense the school's tenure from the cover. Honest historical context framed as data.
  • Page 2: identification grid in 2×4 instead of the termly return's 2×5. Annual census includes "Headteacher" name (a stable annual fact) where the termly return doesn't repeat it. Same identification pattern, slightly different field set.
  • Enrolment table includes "Last yr" and "Δ" columns mirroring the termly return but at the year level. Subtotals per year group (JHS 1 / 2 / 3) help the District Officer see the school as three cohorts. Auto-cell green dots on the Total column — same convention as termly return for "this number came from the system."
  • Page 3: age distribution by class. The most under-used GES form section in practice but operationally important — over-aged students are a flag for late starters or repeaters. "Over-aged students (above 15 in JHS 3): 6 students" contextual note explains what the data means. Without that note, the District Officer would compute it themselves; with it, the school has done the homework.
  • Page 3 also: repetition table with year-end transitions. "JHS 1 → JHS 2 · 96 promoted, 5 repeated, 1 withdrew, 4.9% repetition." Three transitions, one summary row. The "Repetition rate" column is the metric GES tracks; the system computes it.
  • "Overall repetition 4.6% within GES national benchmark of 5%" interpretive note tells the school they're within range. Same kind of contextual interpretation as the GES return's anomaly callouts — the system surfaces benchmarks.
  • Page 4: special needs as full hatched manual block. 12 fields in a 2×6 grid (visual / hearing / physical / intellectual / speech / other × boys/girls). Same hatched aesthetic as termly return; identical visual language for "this is the section that's manual". Admin who filed termly return knows immediately how to interpret the hatched block.
  • Page 4 also: movement during the year. Auto-filled, broken down by reason (admissions, withdrawals · relocation/financial/other, transfers in/out). Same pattern as termly return but at the annual scale. Net change row at the bottom in total style.
  • Page 5: teaching staff list as a 14-row table. Names, sex, role, qualification, salary. Qualification and salary columns marked with "Manual" inline pills in the header — admin scanning the column understands those cells need hand-fill. Column-level auto/manual distinction rather than just row-level.
  • Manual cells in the staff table use hatched diagonal background with thin underline blanks. Same texture as the manual-fill blocks but applied at the cell level. Some cells have data (qualifications captured during onboarding), some don't — visually mixed within the same table. "Partial" status from the drawer made concrete in the PDF.
  • "Pupil-teacher ratio: 312 ÷ 14 = 22.3 to 1 · within GES recommended range for JHS (≤ 25:1)" contextual note. Same as termly return — saves the District Officer doing the calculation, gives admin protective context for what the number means.
  • Pages 6-7: infrastructure across two pages. 8 sub-sections (A through H): classrooms / water / electricity / sanitation / library / ICT / kitchen / furniture. Two pages because infrastructure is the largest manual section; cramming it onto one page would make hand-fill clumsy.
  • Each infrastructure sub-section has its own micro-design. Counts use hatched count cells with underlined blanks; categorical choices use checkbox grids (circles for "tick one", squares for "tick all"); free-form labels use small headed cards. Form vocabulary varies by data shape — appropriate input for each kind of fact.
  • Water source uses radio-circles ("tick one") for 6 options: Borehole / Piped (GWCL) / Hand-dug well / Rainwater harvesting / Tanker delivery / None. Real Ghanaian water source categories — GWCL is Ghana Water Company Limited, real categorical labels not generic SaaS placeholders.
  • Electricity: 4 options (National grid (ECG) / Solar / Generator only / None). ECG is Electricity Company of Ghana — same real-Ghana naming. Acknowledges that schools may have solar or generator-only setups, common in rural areas.
  • Sanitation breaks into 2 parts: latrine counts (numerical) + latrine type (categorical: Water closet / KVIP-VIP / Pit latrine / None) + hand-washing facilities (multi-select: tippy taps / wash basins / Veronica buckets / none). Veronica buckets are real Ghanaian hygiene infrastructure — common at school entrances during COVID and still used. Local vocabulary throughout.
  • Library has 3 fields: status (3 options) + book count + library staff FTE. Realistic granularity — no need for "library books by subject"; the District Officer just needs a rough scale.
  • ICT lab combines status + counts + connectivity. Status options include "Computers but no power / connectivity" — a real failure mode where schools received computers from GES but can't run them. Honest about the realities rather than assuming binary "have/don't have" ICT lab.
  • Kitchen / feeding facilities separates kitchen status from cooking fuel. Cooking fuel is multi-select because schools often use multiple (LPG when funded, firewood as backup). Real operational complexity reflected in the form.
  • Page 8: attendance auto-block with year-by-term breakdown. Three terms shown plus year average. "Year average 93.7%" within "GES target 90%+" context. The attendance section was new in the termly return and now appears in the annual census in the same auto-fill style.
  • BECE results on page 8 with subject-by-subject pass rates and average grade. "BECE scale reminder: grades 1—9 where 1 is highest and 9 is lowest · pass threshold is 6 · aggregate is sum of best 6 subjects" — same scale-reminder pattern as the school stats academic page. Anyone unfamiliar with BECE conventions could misread without it.
  • Page 9: programmes and textbooks. Feeding programme (manual) + textbook count (manual table). Need column auto-filled with 312 (the enrolment count) so admin only fills the in-stock count. The system computes what's needed; admin reports what's actually there.
  • Textbook table by subject × class × need. Real Ghanaian JHS subject list (English, Math, Integrated Science, Social Studies, R.M.E., French, Ghanaian Language, Career Tech, Creative Arts, ICT). 10 subjects × 3 classes = 30 cells to hand-fill. Tedious but operationally important; this is the data that drives supplementary book distribution.
  • Provenance note on textbook table in gold-bordered small italic: "Need column is computed from current enrolment (312 students). Fill the boxes with the number of books actually in stock." Tells admin exactly what the form expects; no ambiguity about whether the column is targets or stock.
  • Page 10: declaration with explicit auto vs manual responsibility. "I understand that auto-filled sections derived from Omnischools records and manually-completed sections filled in by hand are equally my responsibility." This sentence is critical — acknowledges the hybrid document while preserving full headteacher accountability. Auto-fill doesn't reduce signing responsibility.
  • Filing instructions expanded to 9 numbered steps (vs termly return's 5). More steps because the annual census has more manual sections to walk through. Step 2 specifically tells admin to "walk through the school grounds with a clipboard" — operationally concrete instruction. Not abstract "complete infrastructure section"; the system tells admin how.
  • "Retain a signed copy for a minimum of 5 years" in the filing instructions. Annual census has a longer retention requirement than termly return (3 years) because it's the structural record. Honest about the legal requirements the school is taking on.
  • Stamp + signature pattern identical to termly return. Terra circular stamp at -8°, simulated handwritten "Florence Addo" in italic Fraunces. Visual continuity across statutory documents — admin filing both knows the signature block looks the same.
  • What's not in this PDF: No school finances or budget reporting (separate to GES, goes to a different ministry). No staff salary amounts (privacy + the form only asks for status not amount). No student-level data (privacy; aggregate only). No photos of facilities (would require image upload; outside MVP scope).
  • What's also not here: No comparison to district or national averages. No graphs or charts. The annual census is a record, not a dashboard. Charts belong in the school stats report; this is a form GES processes into their own analytics.
