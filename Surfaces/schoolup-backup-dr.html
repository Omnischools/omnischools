What we promise, in writing

This is the platform-side commitment: Omnischools will not lose your data, and if something goes wrong, here is exactly how we make you whole. Distinct from data-export, which is about taking your data out. Backup and DR is about not losing it while it's in — and it's a trust artefact for school admins, MoE inspectors, and auditors who need to know the platform is safe enough for sensitive student records.

The promise

Continuous protection, 30-day recovery window, geographic redundancy.

Every change a teacher makes is captured to a transaction log within seconds. Every night at 02:00 GMT, the system snapshots the full database. Snapshots are encrypted at rest, replicated to a second cloud region, and retained for 30 days. Recovery is admin-triggered, audit-logged, and never destructive.

Schools shouldn't have to take any of this on faith. This page is the contract — published, dated, and tied to actual mechanism.

Why this is published

Most SaaS platforms describe backup in vague marketing language — "enterprise-grade backups" means nothing concrete. Omnischools publishes specific RPO/RTO targets, snapshot times, retention windows, and verification cadence because schools and ministries need to evaluate the platform on technical merit, not adjectives. If we can't say it in writing, we shouldn't say it at all.

Calls worth flagging

  • RPO (Recovery Point Objective) = maximum data loss allowed. RTO (Recovery Time Objective) = maximum downtime allowed. The 5-second RPO and 90-second RTO are aspirational targets, not guarantees — but they're the contracts the engineering team holds itself to.
  • Snapshot at 02:00 GMT = 02:00 Accra time (Ghana is on GMT year-round). Chosen because it's the lowest-traffic hour for Ghanaian schools. UK/EU schools (if Omnischools expands) get the same window — works for them too.
  • 30-day default retention is the industry-standard sweet spot. Long enough to recover from "we noticed a deletion 3 weeks later"; short enough that storage costs don't become absurd. Audit-extended add-on (90 days) is for schools with longer audit cycles.
  • Test-restore on Sunday is critical — backups that aren't tested are not backups. Many DR programs fail not because backups don't exist but because they were never verified to actually restore. Omnischools verifies weekly and pages on failure.
  • The "Last good backup: 02:00 GMT today" stat in the hero is dynamic — admins see freshness in real-time. Trust signal, not a static promise.

Recovery is forward-only

The 27 April deletion stays in the audit log. The 2 May restore is a new event — restored from snapshot, by Florence, with reason captured. The audit chain shows: record existed, was deleted, was restored. No event is erased; the timeline is continuous. This is the same forward-only principle that governs voids, refunds, and bulk-undo across Omnischools.

Calls worth flagging

  • Recovery is admin-only — teachers can't trigger it. Requires the admin who triggered the original deletion (or another admin if not available) to authenticate and provide a reason. Recovery is a sensitive action; co-signing may be added in MVP3 for high-risk recoveries.
  • The timeline shows the gap honestly — 5 days of data captured between 22 Apr and 27 Apr will need to be re-entered. Florence sees this before she clicks restore. No silent data loss; visible cost.
  • Snapshot SCH-CKJHS-2025-04-22-0200 is per-school, not per-record — restoring a single student record reads only that student's data from the snapshot. The snapshot is the source; the restore is targeted.
  • Reason field is required — unlike bulk-undo where reason is optional. Recovery happens days/weeks after the original event; the reason is critical for auditors who'll ask "why was this restored?"
  • For deletions older than 30 days, recovery requires a support ticket — beyond the standard window, the data still exists in cold storage but retrieval needs engineering. Surfaced in a clear "Beyond 30 days? Contact support" path.

Time-machine, not just a backup list

Most backup UIs show a list of file dumps and a restore button. Omnischools lets admin browse the system state at any snapshot point — see the school's roster as of 22 April, see Akosua's profile as of 15 March, see the audit log on the day of a disputed event. Read-only. The view answers questions auditors actually ask: "What did the records show at the time?"

Calls worth flagging

  • The "View →" button opens a read-only mode of the entire app — same UI Florence uses every day, but every page is showing data from the chosen snapshot. Banner across the top: "Viewing 22 April · changes disabled."
  • "Stats since yesterday" line in the snap-meta gives admin a quick narrative — "+2 enrolled, +47 attendance entries" — instead of just a file size and timestamp.
  • The 27 Apr row carries a narrative annotation: "last snapshot before Akosua deletion at 14:23". Snapshots are linked to the audit log; admin can see why a particular date matters.
  • The 22 Apr row shows it was used for a restore — links snapshots to recovery events. Admin can audit "which snapshot has been restored from, when, by whom."
  • Expiring snapshot (3 Apr, expiring tomorrow) is dimmed — gentle visual signal. Admin who needs to extend can purchase the audit-extended add-on to retain it; otherwise it rolls off.

Why this matters · honesty over silence

Most platforms hide their incidents until they have to acknowledge them. Omnischools publishes by default — status page visible to anyone, post-incident reviews public, the 14 April SMS delay shown on the front page even though it was minor. Schools choose Omnischools partly because the MoE asked "how do we know it's reliable?" The answer is not "trust us" — it's "read the public history."

Calls worth flagging

  • SMS for Sev-1 only is deliberate restraint. Schools see Omnischools SMS as parent-comm channel; flooding it with platform alerts dilutes the signal. One platform SMS per quarter is the goal; if it's more, something is wrong.
  • Status page hosted on a different provider than the main app — when Omnischools's primary cloud has a regional outage, the status page is on a CDN that survives. Lessons learned from companies whose status pages went down with their main service.
  • Uptime bars show 30-day rolling history. The slight warn dot in the SMS dispatch row is from 14 April (the 23-min Hubtel-side delay). Historical context, not just current state.
  • Post-incident reviews follow a standard structure: timeline · impact · root cause · remediation · process changes. Schools and the MoE can compare reviews over time and judge how the platform learns.
  • For school-side incidents (admin lost password, lost archive, accidental deletion), this is not the right comm channel — those go through normal support. This page is for platform-side issues that affect multiple schools.
