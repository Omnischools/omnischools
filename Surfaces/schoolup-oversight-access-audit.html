Omnischools Oversight · GES auditing itself

Who looked at what, and why.

Decision 6 set the principle: GES holds the authority to access named records, and the discipline on that authority runs upward and lateral within GES — not down to schools. This is the surface that makes the discipline real. Every named-record access from the compliance record view writes an append-only entry here, and regional directors, the national tier, and GES internal audit review them. The log answers the questions an auditor asks — was the access necessary, was the reason genuine, is one officer accessing far more than their peers — and lets a reviewer query an entry that does not look right. It is GES policing its own officers' use of a power they legitimately hold. The demo is the national audit view, with one access flagged for query.

The log is the accountability, made operational

  • This is the other end of the compliance record view. Every justification-gated access in that surface writes exactly one entry here — accessing officer, reason, record, timestamp. The compliance surface is where the power is used; this is where its use is held and reviewed. The two surfaces are one mechanism.
  • Append-only is stated as a hard property, not a setting. The banner is unambiguous — no one, including national administrators, can edit or delete an entry. A reviewer can attach a query or note; the original record is immutable. An alterable audit log would be worthless, so the surface leads with the guarantee.
  • Named-record access is shown to be rare. 284 entries in 90 days — 0.4% of all Oversight sessions. The KPI makes the proportion explicit: the overwhelming majority of Oversight use is aggregate, and the log proves the gated path is genuinely the exception, not a routine workaround.
  • The reviewer's question is "is this proportionate," and the log is built for it. Reason, officer, frequency all visible — an auditor can spot an officer accessing far more records than peers, or a reason that does not fit the record. 279 cleared, 3 queried: the log surfaces the few that warrant a second look.
  • A queried entry is visibly flagged. R-0461 is tinted and marked "Queried" — the accessing officer has been asked to explain. The query does not erase or alter the entry; it attaches to it. Section 2 shows what a query looks like worked.
  • Safeguarding entries stay case-restricted even here. R-0455 shows the access happened, by whom, for what reason — but the record identity is withheld even in the national audit log. Child-protection cases carry the extra restriction at every tier, consistent with the compliance record view.

Why this was queried
The reasons that justify a named-record access are a controlled set — claim verification, anomaly, audit, safeguarding. This entry selected Free SHS claim verification, but the written explanation describes a parent enquiry with no case reference. A parent asking a question is not, by itself, a compliance ground. Audit's query is whether a genuine claim-verification case exists behind this access.
What the query cannot do
The query does not undo the access — the officer already saw the record, and the log says so permanently. Review is after the fact: it cannot un-see data, only hold the officer to account and deter the next loose access. That is why the gate in the compliance record view matters — prevention is at the gate, accountability is here.

A query worked — and the limit of what review can do

  • The query is specific, and it reads the explanation against the reason. R-0461 selected "Free SHS claim verification" but its written explanation describes a parent enquiry with no case reference. The reviewer is not second-guessing a number — they are checking whether the stated ground genuinely fits. That is exactly the judgement the free-text explanation field exists to enable.
  • The entry is immutable; the query attaches to it. Nothing about R-0461 is rewritten — the officer's original words stay exactly as entered. The query and its eventual outcome are added alongside. The log accumulates; it never edits.
  • The timeline shows query as a process with an end. Logged → queried → officer asked to explain → resolved. It closes either by clearing the entry, if a genuine basis is confirmed, or by escalating to the officer's line manager. A query is not a permanent shadow; it concludes.
  • The surface is honest that review cannot un-see data. The "what the query cannot do" panel says it plainly — the officer already saw the record. Audit review is after the fact: it deters and holds to account, it does not reverse. Prevention lives at the gate in the compliance record view; accountability lives here. Naming that limit is more honest than implying the log fixes everything.
  • This is Decision 6's accountability model, fully operational. Authority held by GES, exercised through a gate, recorded immutably, reviewed upward by GES's own audit — not by schools. The compliance record view and this log are the two halves; together they are the whole control.
  • This completes the access & audit log. The national log with its append-only guarantee (section 1) and a queried entry worked through review (section 2) — the fourth surface of Batch 3. Exports is the last.
