Saved segments, scheduled sends

Two MVP1 features that need each other to be useful — saved segments are infrastructure for repeat sends, scheduled sends need a recurring audience to be defined. Combined here as "reminder series": admin defines an audience (segment with filter logic), schedule (frequency + time + end), and template (one of the established message blueprints), then the system fires the series automatically until paused or end-dated.

Why this layout

  • Bundled saved-segments + scheduled-sends as "reminder series." The two features only become useful together — saved segments without scheduling are a list-builder; scheduling without saved segments is a manual one-shot. The combined surface treats them as one feature with two halves.
  • Sidebar adds Reminder series as a sub-nav under Announcements with gold "NEW" tag · sister to "All sends" and "Templates" that exist in MVP1. Communications hierarchy stays flat — three siblings, not nested.
  • Hero pillar in editorial Fraunces: "3 series sending to 208 unique parents." Then meta line: "14 sends fired so far this term · 0 manual sends required for routine reminders · GHS 4.20 in SMS costs." Tells Florence the value: zero manual sends required because the system handles them.
  • 4 series cards visible: 3 active + 1 paused. Each shows audience (with current count + recent trend), schedule, channels, template, next send time, and three small actions (pause/edit/more). The card answers "what is this series doing?" without opening it.
  • Audience trend lines on each card: "Currently: 47 parents · was 52 last week, 61 the week before." Shows segment dynamism over time — the BECE segment shrinks as parents register, the overdue segment shrinks as parents pay. Florence sees the system working.
  • Daily attendance SMS shown as a series too, even though it predates Tier 3. Existing automation gets retroactively framed as a series so admin has one place to see all recurring sends, not separate UI for "the daily SMS we always had" vs "the new ones I made."
  • Paused series visible with dashed border + neutral status. Term-end parent meeting RSVP series shown as paused — meeting happened, but the schedule is preserved for next term so admin doesn't have to rebuild it. Pause ≠ delete; reversible state.
  • Builder card with gold border + shadow sits below the active list, demonstrating the editor flow. 4 steps: audience / schedule / template / review. Builder is in the same screen as the list — no modal, no separate page. Admin sees her existing series and the new one being built side by side.
  • Audience step has 4 cards: 3 saved segments (Overdue families selected, JHS 3 BECE-relevant, All active families) + "+ Build a custom segment" dashed card. Saved segments are first-class · custom segments are an escape hatch that opens a segment builder (own future surface).
  • Schedule step uses pill-based pickers: frequency (Daily/Weekly/2×Weekly/Monthly), end condition (End of term/After N sends/Specific date/Indefinite). The day & time gets a gold-bordered display field. Schedule summary at the bottom narrates the choices: "Will fire every Friday at 9:00 AM · first send this Friday 24 Oct · last send Friday 19 Dec · estimated 9 sends total over the term."
  • Template step shows 2 message variants (Overdue · soft tone selected, Overdue · firm tone alternative) with full preview text including variables. Variables shown explicitly: "{parent_name}", "{student_name}", "{balance}" — admin sees what gets personalised per recipient.
  • Confirm card narrates the series in plain English: "Sends to 47 parents · When: Every Friday at 9:00 AM · How: WhatsApp where opted in (38 parents) · SMS fallback (9 parents) · Cost: GHS 4.05 over the term · Stops automatically: when balance reaches zero." Italic gold on every user-tunable value; admin reads aloud and approves.
  • "Stops automatically: For each parent the moment their balance reaches zero" is the most important sentence on the confirm card. The series respects the audience semantics: a paid parent stops getting reminded immediately, not at end of term. This is what saved-segment auto-recomputation enables.
  • Save bar in gold-bordered card with editorial summary: "Activate this reminder series? First send fires Friday 24 Oct at 9:00 AM · you'll get a notification 1 hour before · pause any time." Two-step pattern: save draft now, activate when ready · same as bookkeeping settings activation.
  • Notification 1 hour before each send — admin gets a heads-up before automated sends fire. Last chance to intervene; if something has changed in the school's situation (a tragedy, a fee waiver decision), admin can pause before the message goes out.
  • Cost is itemised honestly: "GHS 4.05 over the term · 9 weekly fires × ~9 SMS · WA bundled". Same SMS cost vocabulary as send-reminders flow; admin sees marginal cost of automation as a small finite number, not a mystery operational expense.
