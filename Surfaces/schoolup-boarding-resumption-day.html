Resumption day · 700 boarders, ten hours, one screen

What happens twice a year, in nothing but chaos and paper at most SHSs: students arrive in waves, parents drop them, fees get checked, prospectus items get inspected, bunks get re-confirmed, and by 6 PM the school is full and the gate is closed. This surface compresses that into one live view. Live arrival counter, House-by-House progress, GES prospectus checklist per student, fee-owing flags, issues queue. The Senior Housemaster watches; six Housemasters work the gate and the dorms. The same surface flips on vacation day to run the inverse — sign-outs, room clear-outs, transport contact verification.

What this surface replaces

  • A clipboard. Every previous resumption day at most SHSs is run on a printed roster with HM and parent both signing each entry. By 6 PM, the clipboard is illegible and the next morning the data is being keyed in manually.
  • A WhatsApp group. The Senior HM coordinates HMs by phone calls and screenshots. Issues are tracked nowhere.
  • The fee desk queue. Accountants & HMs duplicate effort. This surface puts the fee status on the same row as the prospectus row.

The 90-second gate check

  • HM scans student ID badge · pulls up the row · GES prospectus 6 items + fee status + bunk all visible
  • Tap to mark each item · ok / partial / missing
  • Fee status from billing · pulled live · no manual cross-check
  • Bunk auto-confirmed · student walks to their bunk · HM moves to next student
  • SMS to parent · "[Student] safely arrived at [School]. Items checked. Welcome back."

Vacation day · the same surface inverse

  • Mode flip: "arriving" becomes "departing"
  • Different checklist: bunk-cleared, locker emptied, chop box collected, transport contact verified, exeat-card returned
  • SMS: "[Student] has departed [School] for vacation. Safe travels."
  • Final state: all 712 students checked out · House cleaned · doors locked · keys to Senior HM

Why one day matters more than fifty
Editorial · the chaos day as design challenge
The two-days-a-year surface

A surface used twice a year · and worth designing harder than any other.

Most surfaces in this codebase get used daily — attendance, prep, inspection, the VLC session. Boarding's exeat surface, monthly. Discipline, weekly. The dashboards, daily. But resumption-day operations is used precisely two days per academic year: once at start-of-term and once at the inverse, end-of-term vacation. Why give a low-frequency surface this much design attention?

Because the consequences of getting those two days wrong are larger than any other twenty days combined. On a normal Tuesday, if attendance is mis-marked for one student, the system absorbs it — the next morning, someone notices, someone fixes it. On resumption day, if a student isn't checked in correctly, the school doesn't know whether they're missing. If a fee-owing student is admitted without follow-up, the school's revenue loss compounds. If a prospectus shortfall isn't logged, the student spends the term with the wrong bedding, mosquitoes get in, malaria follows. If transport delay isn't flagged with name attached, the parent isn't called, and the student's grandfather is sitting at the gate for three hours wondering where to ask.

The design pattern this surface uses is what we've called compression of a chaotic day into a single screen. Senior HM doesn't navigate between four tabs. The arrival counter, the per-House progress, the per-student checklist, and the issues queue are all on one page, and they auto-refresh. The HMs at the six gate stations all run their own version of the same surface scoped to their House, but they see the school-wide totals at the top. One source of truth, six staff members operating against it simultaneously, no merge conflicts.

Two design decisions worth defending. The arrival windows are configurable but defaulted. Forms 3 first (5—7 AM), Forms 2 next, Forms 1 last. This is what most SHSs already do — staggered windows spread the gate load — and it's what the surface assumes unless edited. Schools that do single-window mass arrival can edit the windows to overlap, but they'll see the gate-load consequences in the next column. The default encodes the better practice.

The same surface flips on vacation day, with mode toggling everything. Not two separate surfaces. The same data model, the same staff, the same six gate stations, the same checklist (inverted: bunk cleared instead of bunk confirmed; transport contact verified instead of arrival logged). One surface to learn, two days to use it, every operational year of every Senior Housemaster's career. Symmetry isn't laziness — it's mercy on the user. A surface used twice a year would be re-learned every time if it had no continuity with the surfaces used daily. By matching the pattern of the daily life surface, resumption day feels less like a different planet.

The numbers that drove the design

  • 712 boarders ÷ ~10 hours = 71 per hour throughput. At 90 seconds per student per HM, six HMs working in parallel handle 240 per hour. Headroom built in.
  • Gate-close at 6 PM is hard. Supper is at 6:30. The signing-in book closes at 6:30. Past 6:30, students are "absent for resumption" and parents are SMS'd by Senior HM.
  • The unaccounted-at-window bell rings 60 minutes after a window closes. HH. Roberts arrived after 10:30; she was due 9:30; the SMS to parent fires automatically.
