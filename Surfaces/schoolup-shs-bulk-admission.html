MVP2 · SHS · Admissions · bulk intake · new default surface
Omnischools Senior · CSSPS placement intake · the September moment

Two hundred and forty students at once.

Every September, GES's Computerised School Selection and Placement System (CSSPS) auto-places ~447,000 BECE candidates into the country's SHS, SHTS, and TVET institutions. For each receiving school, that's a list of incoming Form 1s arriving over the next four weeks — often two hundred or more. Heads access the list via hm.cssps.gov.gh, print it, and an admissions clerk re-types it into the school's records. Omnischools's prior per-student admission surface was a poor default for this moment. This bulk surface is the new default: paste the portal list (or upload CSV / PDF), review and resolve flags, confirm programmes, assign Houses, commit in one transaction. The per-student flow remains for self-placement students, walk-in transfers, and late arrivals.

The three-path reality of CSSPS intake

  • GES gives no API and no download. The heads portal exposes view and print only. Every input path Omnischools offers is downstream of the head having physically interacted with hm.cssps.gov.gh — there is no shortcut around that fact, only adapters for what comes out.
  • Paste-from-portal is recommended because the data shape is consistent. CSSPS renders the same column order every year: index, name, sex, DOB, aggregate, programme, choice number, JHS, district, residency. The parser is purpose-built for this exact layout.
  • CSV is fallback for the district-office workflow. Some districts distribute spreadsheets to schools in their catchment; the head doesn't have to log into the portal at all. Template enforcement on column headers prevents misalignment.
  • PDF/printed list is last resort. OCR on Ghanaian names (especially with diacritics) is imperfect; the head will need to verify 10–20% of rows by hand. We surface this honestly: "expect to spend 10–15 minutes verifying."
  • Walkthrough card up top is operational, not marketing. A real head sees this on day one of using Omnischools; if we don't tell them the four-step portal procedure they'll get stuck. Treating it as part of the surface, not external help, respects that this is genuinely a multi-system workflow.
  • Ad-hoc cross-link sits beneath the main flow, not above. Bulk is default; per-student is the second option, available but not promoted. A parent at the gate today before bulk is sourced is a real edge case — the link is there so the head doesn't have to navigate away.

The review discipline

  • 97% first-parse clean rate is the realistic target. CSSPS portal data is mostly structured; the ~3% flagged are predictable categories — siblings with the same surname (most common), missing parent phone (portal exports drop this fairly often), and returning students who withdrew and re-placed (rare but real).
  • Duplicate match is not auto-merge. The head confirms whether the match is a sibling (link both records under one family), a returning student (merge records, restore history), or a coincidence (same surname, different family — admit as new). Three flag types fold into one column with sub-text explaining the specific match reason.
  • Missing parent phone is a hard stop. Of all the fields the portal export drops, parent phone is the one Omnischools refuses to commit without. Every downstream surface (fees, sickbay, comms) depends on it; a student with no parent phone is operationally a ghost. Calling the parent to capture it is the head's clerk's job — not avoidable.
  • Filter pills drop to "Flagged" by default. The head doesn't need to see 232 clean rows; they need to work on the 8 that need attention. Pills carry counts in mono so the operational state is always visible.
  • "Cannot continue until flags resolved" is structural, not advisory. No partial commit. The bulk operation either succeeds as a clean 240-row insert or it doesn't run. Tier B undo (24-hour window with diff modal) applies to the batch as one undo unit, per the bulk-undo decision.

One screen, three decisions, mostly auto

  • Programme allocation is almost always identity-mapped. CSSPS uses the school's declared vacancy split to drive placement; the head declared 60/60/80/40 to GES at the start of the cycle, and CSSPS placed exactly 60/60/80/40 students. Confirmation is a formality but it's an audit moment — if the head had declared different numbers, the gap surfaces here before commit.
  • House algorithm respects sibling continuity first. Of all the rules, "place a student in the same House as their already-enrolled sibling" is the one parents notice and complain about if violated. The algorithm makes this Rule 1; the override count card shows 3 / 240 because three of the duplicate-match flags from review were confirmed as siblings and routed accordingly.
  • Residency is 100% CSSPS-preference-driven by default. Students chose Day or Boarding when they made their CSSPS selections; Omnischools does not second-guess that. Override is per-student (e.g., medical board recommends day for a chronic-condition student), not bulk.
  • The trio is one screen on purpose. Splitting into three separate screens would mean three commit decisions; combining them is faster because the inter-dependencies are visible (House capacity tracks programme split tracks residency demand). A head can confirm the whole trio in 90 seconds if the algorithm got it right.
  • Tier B undo classification appears here. Bulk admission settles into reports (class rosters, House rosters, Free SHS claim) but no external state changes yet — SMS hasn't fired, GES claim hasn't filed. 24-hour window with diff modal lets the head reverse if a mistake surfaces overnight. Matches the bulk-undo tier model locked in BUILD_STACK.

The story this surface tells

  • 240 commit shifts to 242 net within two weeks. Every SHS goes through this. The bulk number is what GES placed; the actual cohort is bulk minus no-shows plus walk-ins. The reconciliation surface makes both sides of that math visible — heads don't have to keep mental ledgers across paper lists and spreadsheets.
  • "Self-placed elsewhere" is a real category, not an exception. A student auto-placed at St. Theresa's may decline and self-place at a school closer to home or with better fit for their aggregate. Omnischools shouldn't pretend this is fraud or a system failure — it's how Ghanaian families actually navigate placement. The status pill ("Self-placed elsewhere") reads operationally, not judgmentally.
  • The per-student flow is the secondary pipeline, not a fallback for the bulk's mistakes. 14 walk-ins is normal volume for a 240-student intake — about 6%. The bulk surface explicitly links to per-student as the right tool for that volume, not as an "if bulk fails" path. Both pipelines feed one cohort, with provenance preserved.
  • The 24-hour Tier B undo window expired the day after commit. Reconciliation is not undo — it's the natural multi-week aftermath. A correction at this stage is a per-student withdrawal or a per-student admission, not a bulk reversal. The tier model from BUILD_STACK plays out exactly as designed: bulk gets one undo window, then everything is per-record from there.
  • "File GES return →" is the surface's terminal action. Once reconciliation closes (10 October), the head files the official Free SHS return with 228 bulk + 14 ad-hoc enrolled, 12 placed-non-enrolled. That filing is the moment the admission cycle officially ends. Until then, this surface keeps showing the gap.
