02
Why the 48-hour reflection · the half-point split
Editorial · the participation contract
The design call

Attendance is necessary but not sufficient for the full CPD point.

Teachers can attend a PLC session and learn nothing. The point of PLC isn't to occupy bodies in a room — it's to provoke change in classroom practice. The reflection requirement closes the loop: each teacher writes briefly about what they took away, what they'll do differently, what they want to raise next time. The reflection is the bridge from "I sat through a session" to "I'm carrying something into next week's lesson."

Splitting the point into 0.5 attendance + 0.5 reflection has three effects. First, a teacher who shows up but isn't engaged still earns something (0.5) — we don't punish honest attendance. Second, the bonus 0.5 means a small effort (3 short questions, 48-hour window) gets a real reward. Third, the reflection becomes a data signal: if 30% of attendees don't reflect, the PLC content probably isn't landing — which is information the facilitator and the Headmaster need.

01
Session closes · 4:30 PM

Facilitator hits "Close session." Omnischools sends each attending teacher an SMS with the reflection link + an in-app prompt. The 48-hour countdown starts.

02
Within 48 hours

Teacher opens the link or app, answers three short questions (takeaway, commitment, question for next time). Typed or voice-to-text. Mobile-first.

03
Auto-posted to ledger

Submission triggers +0.5 to the teacher's CPD ledger. Source tagged as PLC_REFLECT. Facilitator sees it land in their dashboard. NTC sync queued.

The reflection contract

  • 3 questions, not 30. The reflection prompt is short on purpose. A 40-question survey gets ignored. Three questions, answerable in 5 minutes, mobile-friendly.
  • 48 hours, not 7 days. The reflection needs to be close enough to the session that memory is fresh. By Monday morning the teacher is in the classroom, the moment has passed.
  • Voice-to-text matters. Many teachers will type a few words on phone. Some will dictate. Both feed the same field.
  • Facilitator can see who reflected. The PLC facilitator (HoD) can see the reflection status per teacher in their dashboard. Useful for the next session: "Mr Yeboah, you mentioned in your reflection that you wanted to discuss assessment of Form 1 students — let's spend 10 minutes on that today."
  • Skipping reflection is a yellow flag. Skipping once is fine. Skipping repeatedly surfaces in the school-wide dashboard for the HoD's notice.
