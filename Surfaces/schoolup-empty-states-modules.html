Why this layout

  • Three tiles, one recommended. The "best path" gold ribbon removes choice paralysis without forcing the decision. Heads who know their situation will pick freely; heads who don't have a clear winner.
  • Each tile has a "Best for" footnote. "50+ students" / "Under 50" gives a concrete signal — admin self-identifies by school size, not by guessing what fits them.
  • Third tile (migration) is the long-tail catch. Schools moving from another system are a real subset; offering a path makes them feel seen even though it's a "talk to us" workflow in MVP1.
  • Reversibility strip in green is the most important reassurance on this screen. Heads are nervous about importing 200 records badly. "Imports are one undoable batch" removes that anxiety.
  • Template strip below the path tiles sits as a passive utility. Heads who already have a CSV won't need it; heads who don't will find it within reach.
  • No fake table preview. Some products show "0 students" with a stylised empty grid. We don't — empty grids look broken, and the path tiles are the work itself.
  • "Mix and match" is stated explicitly in the lede. Many heads assume picking one path locks them in; the line removes that assumption upfront.
  • Page title is "Add your first students," not "Students" or "No students yet." Action-framed; tells the head what this page is for.

Why this layout

  • Class context strip up top tells the teacher exactly where they are. Important when a teacher has 3 classes and the URL alone isn't enough orientation.
  • Term Exam / CA / Final pills shown active, not greyed out. Even with no data, the structure is visible — teacher learns the navigation while looking at the empty state.
  • Student names are real, not placeholders. The roster has loaded; what's missing is just the assessment columns. This is the entire teaching of the empty state — "your students are here, you just haven't graded anything yet."
  • Floating CTA card sits over a faded gradebook, not over a blank canvas. Shows the eventual shape of the page; reduces the cognitive jump from this screen to a filled one.
  • Em-dashes (—) in cells, not zeros. A zero is a grade; a dash is "no value." Critical distinction for fairness — never imply a student got 0 by default.
  • "+ Add your first column" header in the table spans where assessment columns will go. It's both an explanation and a clickable affordance — same surface, two roles.
  • "Use a quick template" as a softer second CTA. Teachers who don't know what to add first can pick from a preset (3 quizzes, 2 assignments, 1 term exam — the standard JHS shape).
  • Save and Export buttons disabled in the top right, not hidden. Visible-but-inactive teaches the teacher what these buttons will do once they have something to save.

Why this layout

  • "Issue Term 1 invoices" as the page title, not "Billing." Action-framed — admin came here to do something specific, the title confirms that's exactly what's about to happen.
  • State card on the left, invoice preview on the right. The preview is the most reassuring object on the page — head sees what each parent will receive before sending. Removes the "I have to send 381 of these and I don't know what they look like" anxiety.
  • "This is the moment fees go live" is the key sentence. Issuing invoices triggers SMS to every guardian — this is irreversible in spirit (parents will see the SMS) even if technically reversible. The tone signals weight without alarm.
  • "Edit fee structure first" sits as a secondary CTA. Heads who realise mid-page that fees aren't right shouldn't have to navigate elsewhere — escape hatch right here.
  • Three-step "what happens" strip in gold answers the unspoken question: "what actually happens when I click that gold button?" Each step is concrete — scoped per student, SMS preview, payments arrive.
  • Roman numerals for the steps, not 1-2-3. Subtle but they read as a sequence rather than a checklist. Aligns with the Fraunces italic tone elsewhere in the system.
  • SMS preview text is verbatim. "Akosua's Term 1 fees of GHS 320.00 are due by Sep 30..." — head sees the actual SMS copy, builds trust that nothing weird gets sent in their school's name.
  • Optional fees shown in muted color in the preview. Transport is opt-in per family; the preview shows it but signals it won't be included by default. Honest visual communication of how the model works.
