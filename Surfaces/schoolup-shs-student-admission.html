Why one screen, not a wizard

Admission happens 280 times in early September — the admin doing this 280 times needs a single fast surface, not a click-click-click wizard. The grouped sections create cognitive structure (personal · academic · house · residency · family) without requiring page transitions. The right-hand summary panel updates in real-time as fields are filled, so the admin sees what they're committing to before they click Admit. Speed where it's earned, density where it's needed.

Calls worth flagging

  • CSSPS verification is the killer feature for SHS admins — the placement number auto-fills name, DOB, gender, BECE aggregate, and the system flags if anyone tries to admit a student to the wrong school. Removes data-entry burden and prevents fraud.
  • The programme defaults to CSSPS first choice but can be changed — a student might have placed for Science but the school wants to move them to Arts (with parent consent). Don't force the CSSPS choice; surface it as the default.
  • Auto-balancing for sections and Houses is the right default. Manual override is one click away. Admins who want to assign by sibling-grouping or pastoral need can override; admins who just want to admit 280 students fast trust the algorithm.
  • Greyed-out Houses ("girls only" for a boy) prevent the most common error — accidentally putting a boy in a girls' House. Show them, but disable them. Don't hide options; explain why they're not available.
  • The "MoMo OK" verify tag is a holdover from MVP1 — same logic; checks the parent's phone is a valid MoMo wallet for future fee collection (or in Kofi's case, fee waivers under Free SHS).
  • "Free SHS" is auto-detected from school ownership (public school) and student status (placed via CSSPS). Private SHS schools see normal fee structure. The right thing happens by default.

Additive, not divergent

The SHS admission flow is the basic-school admission flow plus four new fields (programme, House, residency, CSSPS) and one expanded field (class becomes 3-dimensional). Everything else is identical. Same form library, same component vocabulary, same validation patterns. The user who has admitted basic students will recognise the surface immediately and just learn where the new fields live. No re-learning, just extension.

Calls worth flagging

  • The "3-DIM" tag on class assignment captures that SHS classes are a tuple of (Form, Programme, Section) — three dimensions. Basic schools collapse to just (Year, Section) or even just (Year). The surface absorbs this without making the basic-school admin care.
  • "BECE aggregate" appears on SHS only because SHS schools care about academic level at admission (drives placement decisions, tracks against WASSCE prediction). Basic schools don't have this concept — KG admissions don't have BECE.
  • The "EXT" tag on Fees (rather than "NEW") signals that this is the existing fee field, extended with SHS-specific options. Same column in the database; different UI dropdown choices.
  • The compare layout is also a design-doc artefact — at the engineering handoff, this is the diff to implement. Four new fields, one expanded field, eight shared fields. Scoped work.
  • For Multi-tier schools, both forms exist — admin choosing "Admit student" gets a tier picker first (Basic side or SHS side), then sees the appropriate form. No conditional rendering inside one form. Cleaner separation by tier.
