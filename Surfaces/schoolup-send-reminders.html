Why this layout

  • Drawer not modal. Drawer matches the Record Payment pattern already in the system — admin's mental model is "transactional flows slide in from the right." Modals interrupt; drawers compose. The blurred report behind stays visible so admin remembers what they're acting on.
  • Header carries the headline number. "You're about to message 64 parents about overdue fees totalling GHS 8,420.00." Two numbers admin needs to anchor everything else against. Putting them in Fraunces gives them weight; the rest of the body is supporting detail.
  • Numbered sections (1, 2, 3, 4) with gold circle tags create a scannable progression — Recipients → Message → Channel & cost → When. Admin can read top-to-bottom or jump to whichever section concerns them. The numbers are quiet; they're a navigational aid, not a wizard ladder.
  • Recipient buckets aligned to the aging-bucket pattern from the outstanding-balances report. Same 1–7 / 8–30 / 30+ split, same gold/warn/terra colours. Visual continuity means admin's brain doesn't have to retranslate categories. The 30+ days bucket in terra is the clearest "these are the painful ones."
  • "Sample of who'll receive this" shows seven actual parent names plus "and 57 others." Admin can spot-check — "wait, Patricia paid yesterday, why is she on this list?" The "View full recipient list" link is for when something looks wrong; most of the time the sample is enough.
  • "Customise list" link in the section eyebrow. Admin can drop the 30+ days bucket if they want to send only to the recently-overdue, or remove specific parents. Out of scope to fully design here, but the entry point is preserved.
  • Message preview shows fully-rendered text with one real sample's variables filled in. Variables are visually distinguished with gold-bg highlighting like this so admin can see the templating without having to imagine it. Char count and parts in the footer (147 chars · 1 part) avoids surprise multi-part SMS billing.
  • Template name in the preview header ("fee_reminder_v2") in mono — auditable identifier. If admin changes their mind about the message, "Edit message" link goes to template editor.
  • "Sample shows Patricia Boateng's message" with link to view 3 random samples. Honest about whose data is filling the preview; offers the "show me a different one" affordance for when admin worries about edge cases (parents with very long names, large amounts, etc).
  • Channel row with cost on the right. SMS is the only active channel in MVP1; the row shows the per-message cost (GHS 0.05), the recipient count (64 numbers), and the total (GHS 3.20). Math is shown explicitly: "64 × GHS 0.05" so admin doesn't have to do it in their head.
  • WhatsApp row shown but tagged MVP2. Dashed border, lower opacity, "MVP2" pill. Reveals the future state without breaking the present one. Confirms WhatsApp will fall back to SMS for non-opted-in parents — important for admin to know the channel mix won't degrade reach.
  • Send timing as two tiles. "Send now" selected by default with the actual time it'll go out ("Today, 10:42 AM GMT · within 2 minutes"). Schedule is the alternative. Two options not three — admin doesn't need to decide between "now" and "in 5 minutes." Notice the line below: "It's a Friday morning during school hours — a fine time to message parents about fees." A small contextual sanity check.
  • Footer is sticky. Total cost + recipient summary on the left, action buttons on the right. The cost is in Fraunces 14px so it's bigger than the supporting text but smaller than the headline numbers up top. Admin's eye lands on it as the final check before clicking Send.
  • "Send 64 reminders →" in navy primary. The number in the button matches the number in the header — same fact, surfaced where admin needs it most. The arrow signals forward motion.

Why this layout

  • Warnings appear at the top of the drawer body — before the main breakdown sections. Their job is to interrupt confidence before it becomes momentum. If admin scrolls past the warnings to send anyway, that's their choice; they don't get to claim they didn't see them.
  • Two warning levels: terra (urgent) and warn (caution). Unreconciled payments are terra because sending a reminder to someone who already paid is a relationship-damaging error. Recent reminders are warn because it's annoying but not factually wrong.
  • The terra warning lists names inline. "Patricia Boateng, Mary Asante, Kwame Owusu, Esther Quaye, Joseph Mensah" — five names is short enough to read; admin can recognise specific parents and judge whether the warning applies. This is the difference between "5 parents have unreconciled payments" (abstract) and "Patricia might already have paid" (concrete).
  • Each warning has two buttons: "Review pending payments →" (the right thing to do) and "Send anyway" (the escape hatch). The right thing is the styled button; the escape hatch is a quiet text link. Admin can override but the override has friction proportional to the risk.
  • Header subtitle changes: "Two issues need your attention before sending." The numbers also adjust: 58 not 64, GHS 7,640.00 not 8,420. The drawer reflects the actual state after suppressions; admin doesn't have to do mental arithmetic.
  • Suppression pills below the recipient buckets show what was removed and why. "5 with pending payments · removed", "6 messaged in last 3 days · removed", "2 opted out of SMS · removed". Each pill is a discrete fact admin can evaluate. Total: 13 removed (matches "58 not 64" math).
  • Section title becomes "Recipients · adjusted" — the small additional word signals that this isn't the full original list. Continuity with the clean state but acknowledgment that something changed.
  • SMS opt-outs are always-removed and never overridden. Unlike the other suppressions which have "include anyway" buttons, opt-out is final. Compliance — schools cannot legally message parents who explicitly said no. The pill shows the count without an action.
  • "When" tile defaults to Schedule in the warnings state, not "Send now." Defensive default — when there are unreconciled payments, the right answer is usually to wait. The smart default for the scheduled time is Monday 8:30 AM with explanation: "lets the unreconciled payments settle over the weekend." This is the system being thoughtful so admin doesn't have to be.
  • Date and time fields appear when Schedule is selected. "Mon · 5 May 2026" / "08:30 AM" with GMT explicit. Editable but pre-filled sensibly. Admin can override the smart default; the system isn't being prescriptive, just helpful.
  • Footer summary changes: "scheduled Mon 5 May · 8:30 AM · 13 removed". The schedule and the suppression count are the two facts admin most likely wants to verify before clicking. Both surface in the footer.
  • Send button changes verb: "Schedule 58 reminders →" instead of "Send 58 reminders →". The verb matches the timing choice. Small detail; reinforces that this isn't going out right now.
