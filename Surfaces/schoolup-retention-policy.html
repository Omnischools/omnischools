How long do we keep your records?

Retention isn't one decision — it's three layered ones. Statutory submissions to GES become permanent record once submitted (legal requirement, not configurable). Financial records have a 7-year regulatory floor (configurable above that, never below). General communications are configurable from forever down to a 3-year rolling window or any custom period. The settings panel separates the three so admins make informed choices rather than one global setting that misaligns with Ghanaian record-retention norms.

Why this matters

Three kinds of records, three different rules

Some of your school's data is permanent by law. Some has a regulatory floor. Some you can keep forever or discard early. The policy below separates the three so you make informed choices rather than one blanket setting that conflicts with Ghana's record-retention norms.

⚖ Aligned with Ghana Data Protection Act 2012 + GRA tax records guidance
The three layers
Each layer has its own retention rule. The first two are locked at minimum by law/regulation; the third is fully configurable by you.

Why this layout

  • Three layers shown explicitly before any chooser appears. Statutory (Forever, locked) / Financial (7-year minimum, locked floor) / Communications (configurable). Admin understands what is and isn't on the table before deciding — no false sense that "retention" is one slider.
  • Layer policy tags use distinct colour vocabulary: green "Forever · locked" for statutory, warn "7 years · minimum" for financial, gold "Configurable" for communications. Visual encoding of authority: green = legally fixed, warn = floor-locked, gold = your choice.
  • Hero card in navy/gold positions retention as a serious decision: "Three kinds of records, three different rules." Then green "Aligned with Ghana Data Protection Act 2012 + GRA tax records guidance" tag. Establishes legal grounding; the policy isn't arbitrary, it tracks Ghanaian regulatory norms.
  • 4 retention options as cards: Forever (current default, gold tag) / 7 years rolling (selected, recommended for uniformity with financial floor) / 3 years rolling (data-minimisation framing) / Custom period (5 years example with input). Each has plain-English "What happens" footer in a tinted box explaining the practical consequence.
  • "3 years rolling" framed as JHS cohort lifecycle: "communications from a graduated cohort roll out roughly when those students leave." Concrete operational meaning rather than abstract privacy rhetoric — admin connects the choice to the rhythm of the school.
  • "7 years rolling" framed as alignment with the financial-records floor: "retention is uniform across modules." Recommends the cohesive choice — picking 7 years means everything purges on the same schedule rather than messages outliving receipts or vice versa.
  • Custom period card has an inline value input with the "5" displayed and "preview impact ↓" link in italic gold. Live preview encourages experimentation; admin can scrub the value and see the impact table update before committing.
  • Impact preview region shows what would actually happen: "Switching to 7-years-rolling today would purge 28 communications from before 22 Oct 2018." Specific number, specific date, specific category (routine SMS/WhatsApp reminders) — no surprises.
  • Purge table has 4 columns: Record category / Total in system / Older than threshold / Action. Statutory submissions row shows "Kept · permanent" in green even though 3 returns from 2018 qualify by date — because they're locked. Financial records row shows "Kept · within 7-yr floor" for the 184 entries from 2017 — because financial records floor is 7 years. Only the SMS/WhatsApp row shows actual purge action.
  • Internal admin notes / email broadcasts rows show "none qualify yet" because the school's earliest notes/emails fall within the 7-year window. Honest about which categories will and won't be affected; doesn't pretend everything purges uniformly.
  • Total row at the bottom of the purge table: "28 records · Approx. 0.5% of all communications." Frames the magnitude: this isn't a destructive policy, it's a small steady rollover.
  • Acknowledgement card in warn-tinted treatment (same as activation segregation's solo-admin path) makes the irreversibility explicit: "permanently delete · this is irreversible · purged content cannot be recovered, only the summary count remains." Doesn't soften the consequence.
  • "Parents who request their data after a purge will see the same redacted summary" — connects retention to the parent-portal view (file 2). Cross-module integrity: the parent view filter rules and the retention policy purge rules produce the same end-state for old records.
  • Acknowledgement checkbox lists specific consequences: 28 records purged, irreversible, audit summary remains, statutory + financial untouched, timestamped with name, future changes don't undo past purges. Admin agrees to specifics, not to a vague "yes I understand."
  • Save bar mentions "First rollover fires 1 November 2025 at 2 AM GMT" — gives admin a specific moment to remember when the first purge happens. Email notification sent to all admins on rollover ensures no one is surprised by missing data.
  • Policy history card at the bottom shows the audit trail of policy changes themselves: "17 Oct 2022 · default policy: Keep forever / 22 Oct 2025 · pending change: Keep forever → 7 years rolling." Policy changes are append-only; the system records who changed what and when, in addition to the data they're governing.
  • What this design deliberately doesn't do: doesn't allow disabling retention for statutory records (locked legally), doesn't allow shorter than 7 years for financial (locked at floor), doesn't auto-purge without admin acknowledgement (irreversible action requires explicit consent), doesn't conflate retention with deletion-on-graduation (those are separate decisions handled by student data lifecycle).
