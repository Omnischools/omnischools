02

Backfill past months?

Optional · enables full-year reporting now
Backfill from 1 September 2025
Enter monthly summary totals for the academic year so far. Term 1 P&L will be complete from day one. Backfill entries are clearly tagged "backfilled · admin estimate" in audit trails — bank reviewers can distinguish them from contemporaneously-recorded entries.
Start fresh from today
Books begin from 22 October 2025. Term 1 P&L will be partial until next year. Cleaner audit trail because every entry is recorded contemporaneously, but no current-year comparative for your first 12 months.

04

Other preferences

Defaults you can change later
Fiscal year boundary
Most Ghanaian schools align with the academic year (Sep — Aug). Calendar year (Jan — Dec) is also supported for tax-aligned reporting.
Depreciation method
Straight-line is the simplest and what most Ghanaian schools use. Declining balance is more aggressive in early years; recommended only if your accountant uses it.
Audit trail · append-only
Every change creates a new entry rather than overwriting the old one. Cannot be disabled · this protects you and demonstrates integrity to bank reviewers.
Anomaly alerts
Flag unusual entries on the dashboard (utility spike, repairs anomaly, low bank runway). On by default.
Auto-categorise from billing & staff
When salaries are run, fee invoices are paid, or Hubtel fees are deducted — auto-create matching ledger entries. Saves rekeying; entries can still be edited.

Why this layout

  • Activation hero in navy/gold gradient with editorial framing: "Three decisions take about 5 minutes." Sets the tone: this is a guided 5-minute setup, not a 50-page accounting wizard. Removes intimidation around the bookkeeping concept.
  • Three mode cards (Off / Aggregate / Detailed) with feature comparison and "Ideal for" footer on each. Aggregate marked "Recommended for new schools" with gold gradient + tag — the safest starting point that doesn't force commitment to detailed transaction recording.
  • Each mode card lists what's included with check/X marks. Off mode honestly says "no P&L or balance sheet · annual census infrastructure stays manual" — not selling the choice; describing it. Lets schools self-select.
  • Backfill choice as 2 radio cards: backfill from 1 Sep (selected by default) or start fresh from today. The selected option's description acknowledges the audit-trail concern: backfilled entries are tagged "backfilled · admin estimate" so bank reviewers can distinguish them from contemporaneously-recorded data.
  • Chart of accounts editor with 5 income + 8 expense locked categories + custom slots. Locked categories cannot be deleted because they're "required for P&L & balance sheet structure" — comparison across schools and standardised reports require a stable backbone. Custom categories supplement, never replace.
  • Custom category example: "Examination expenses" for BECE invigilator stipends & supplies. Realistic Ghanaian school nuance: BECE-related costs are large and recurring for JHS schools; some operators want them as a distinct category, others bucket them in Other. Choice respected.
  • "Add custom income category · up to 5" caps customisation — preventing the chart of accounts from sprawling into 30 fragmented categories that defeat the purpose of having structure. Limits with explanation.
  • Other preferences: fiscal year boundary / depreciation method / audit trail / anomaly alerts / auto-categorise. Most defaults match Ghanaian school norms (academic year, straight-line, audit on). Audit trail toggle is forced-on with greyed lock — non-negotiable; the language explains why ("protects you and demonstrates integrity to bank reviewers").
  • Confirm bar in gold-bordered card with editorial summary: "Activate books in aggregate mode · backfilled from 1 Sep 2025" then meta-line with 4 secondary settings. One sentence captures the choices; admin reads aloud and decides.
  • "Save as draft" alongside "Activate & start using →" — admin can configure now and activate later. The activation is intentional, not accidental. Two-step pattern protects against premature commitment.
