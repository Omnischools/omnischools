Student readiness.
One candidate, one trajectory.

The cohort dashboard shows distributions; the student page shows a person. For Y. Aidoo — Mock 1 aggregate 14, Mock 2 aggregate 10, target KNUST Biochemistry (cut-off 11), currently inpatient at Asankrangwa Government Hospital with severe malaria — this is what the Head of Academics, Form Master, and parent each open on Wednesday 14 May at 14:45 to see one trajectory, one set of teacher comments, one university match panel, one process for the missed papers. The medical disruption banner is sticky to the top while the case is live.

The predictor STPSHS structurally cannot run. STPSHS is the WAEC regulator's terminal ledger — it captures each subject's final Year 1, 2, and 3 score, and that is its purpose. It holds no in-term trajectory: no F1 Semester 1 mid-sem, no Form 1 project work, no portfolio cadence across the three years. Omnischools holds all of it, in the same five-category structure (assignments, mid-semester exam, end-of-semester exam, project work, portfolio) the score ledger spec defines, and that structure is what makes the predictor possible. Aggregate 10 is not a guess against Mock 2 — it is read off Y. Aidoo's six-subject ledger across the six semesters of F1, F2, and F3 against the cohort's own historical Mock-to-WASSCE correlations. STPSHS will see the eventual A1–F9 grades when WAEC returns them; this surface is what helps the school act before then.

Schedule design rules

  • WAEC schedule is the source of truth. Omnischools pulls the schedule from WAEC's published timetable and overlays each candidate's specific subjects.
  • Three statuses, not five. Sat · Missed · Upcoming. Granular sub-statuses (filed SC, awaiting reschedule, etc.) live in the action column.
  • Make-up sittings are off-centre. WAEC handles make-ups at regional offices, not at the school. Transport and accompaniment is the school's responsibility.
  • Fit-to-sit is a clinician call. Matron coordinates with the hospital clinician; the Head of Academics confirms scheduling. The candidate's parent is informed of all three steps.

What STPSHS sees
3 terminal weighted totals · one per year

F1 · 66 · F2 · 75 · F3 · 81 (projected). The WAEC regulator's ledger receives one weighted figure per (subject × year) — the school's own end-of-year computation, terminal and externally moderated only by WAEC's later WASSCE result. There is no field for in-term cadence, no portfolio history, no record of how the F1 figure was built from assignments versus end-of-sem exam, no record of which terms the student was strong in mid-sem vs project work. STPSHS captures the destination, not the journey.

What Omnischools sees
30 in-semester cells · five categories × six semesters

The whole table above. Every assignment-batch average, every mid-sem exam, every end-of-sem score, every project return, every portfolio entry across the six semesters of F1, F2, and F3 — captured in the score-ledger spec's five-category structure, weighted at Asankrangwa SHS's configured 15/15/40/15/15, audit-trailed by who-entered-what-when, surfaced through Path A, B, or C depending on how the subject teacher works. The trajectory's slope — 61.5 climbing to 81.4 with an acceleration in F3 — is what the predictor reads, and is what makes Mock 2's B2 projection hold even with the medical disruption on the English papers.

How this trajectory produces the projected B2

The model that turns ledger trajectory into WASSCE-grade projection reads three signals from this table. Level — F3 S2 weighted total 81.4 sits squarely in the A1 / B2 boundary band given Asankrangwa SHS's historical Mock-to-WASSCE correlations. Slope — the F2-S2-to-F3-S2 acceleration (73.5 → 81.4 across three semesters) is itself predictive: students whose ledger climbs into F3 outperform their Mock 2 in over 70% of cases at this school. Component shape — end-of-sem scores (62 → 82) climbing faster than mid-sem (58 → 78) is the WASSCE-shaped profile, since WASSCE is itself a terminal exam. Three predictive signals, all reading from data STPSHS does not hold and is not asked to. Mock 2 said B2; the trajectory says B2 is the floor, not the ceiling. The projection holds the floor.

Why this section is the structural answer to the STPSHS question

  • STPSHS is the regulator's ledger. It captures the terminal weighted score per subject per year — three numbers per subject for the full three-year course — because that is what WAEC and the Ministry need for regulatory reconciliation. It is not designed for projection, and it is not weakened by that — it is correct for its purpose.
  • The five-category ledger is the operational artifact underneath. Every Ghanaian SHS, public or private, keeps it — most on paper, some digital, some both. NaCCA names the five categories in the Government Year 1 Teacher Manual; weights are configured per (subject × school) and shown on every report so a parent can see how the year-end figure was built.
  • The 30-cell trajectory is what Omnischools captures structurally. Path A populates from in-semester Omnischools entries; Path B from scanned paper-ledger with verification-first OCR; Path C from direct digital entry on the grid. The data model is the same across paths — six semesters × five categories per (student × subject) — and every cell has a path-of-record, an audit history, a Reference ID, and a sync state.
  • The predictor reads slope and shape, not just level. A flat-then-decline trajectory at the same Mock 2 score reads very differently from this climbing trajectory. STPSHS cannot make that distinction because it does not hold the data that produces it. Omnischools can.
  • The Reference IDs match across systems. Each term-row's Reference ID is the same identifier that appears on Y. Aidoo's STPSHS submission record. A teacher who wants to reconcile their digital trajectory with WAEC's regulator view can do it row by row — the IDs are the bridge.
  • The 21-of-24 STPSHS state from Section 1 is the same data as the bottom row above. The terminal F1 and F2 figures are submitted (the green pips); the F3 figure is locally complete but waits for the 14 July 2026 STPSHS window to auto-push. Same underlying record, two views — the terminal-state view in Section 1 for the operational status, the trajectory view here for the predictive reasoning.

How aggregate works

WASSCE grades convert to points on a 1–9 scale: A1=1, B2=2, B3=3, C4=4, C5=5, C6=6, D7=7, E8=8, F9=9. A1–C6 are credit passes (count for university). D7–F9 do not count.

The aggregate is the sum of points from the best 3 cores plus the best 3 electives. The minimum possible is 6 (six A1s); the maximum is 54.

Lower aggregate = better. Universities publish a cut-off, which is the worst aggregate they admit for that programme. KNUST Biochemistry cut-off is 11 — Y. Aidoo's 10 is one place inside.

Projection caveat

English Language is projected at B3 from Mock 2. If the SC-12 make-up sitting yields a different grade, aggregate updates automatically. Current projection holds at 10 until WAEC releases scores in mid-August.

Aggregate rules

  • Best 3 + best 3. The WAEC formula is universal across all five anglophone member countries.
  • D7 doesn't count. If a candidate has only D7 or below in a core, they cannot get a credit pass — university admission depends on credits.
  • Dropped subjects stay visible. The card grays out but the row stays, so the calculation is auditable.
  • Projection updates on real data. When WAEC releases scores (Aug), aggregate recomputes once. No manual adjustment.

How matching works

  • Cut-off is the worst aggregate admitted. Lower = more competitive.
  • Trend is 3 years. Cut-offs shift modestly; Omnischools keeps the rolling 3-year history.
  • Prerequisites are subject-checked. Biochem needs Chem + Bio credit at C6 or better — flagged in candidate's match.
  • Five-tier band. Target · Comfortable · Match · Stretch · Safety. The guidance counsellor lands a candidate on one target plus 3–5 supporting choices.
