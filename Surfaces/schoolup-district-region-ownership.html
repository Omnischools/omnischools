District, region, ownership

Three small fields, one big consequence. Where the school is (district + region), who owns it (public, private, mission, international), and what it agrees to share with GES — these determine whether the school appears in the future Oversight tier, which jurisdictional view it belongs to, and what subset of its data flows into the analytics pipeline. The admin fills this in once during setup, never thinks about it again. Quietly important.

Why this admin surface is small but consequential

The three fields on this page — geography, ownership type, and data-sharing scope — are tiny in volume but enormous in implication. They determine which jurisdiction the school belongs to, which fee model applies, and what slice of data flows into the analytics database that feeds the Oversight tier. Most admins fill this in once during setup and never think about it again — which is exactly the right behaviour. The surface exists so that when an admin does need to revisit it (e.g. when a new GES policy changes data-sharing requirements), they can.

Calls worth flagging

  • The region/district dropdowns are locked once students are admitted — changing a school's district is a serious operation that affects historical reporting. Forcing it through support prevents accidental changes. The locked-state visual (gold-tinted background) signals the field's protected status.
  • Ownership type affects defaults but never enforces — a private school can still toggle Free SHS on if they choose, an international school can still opt-in to GES sharing. The type sets defaults; admin overrides per case. Soft guardrails, not hard rails.
  • The data-sharing scope checklist is opt-in checkbox-by-checkbox, not all-or-nothing. A school can share attendance but not finance, share PLC but not VLC. The current "PARTIAL" pill at the top of the section reflects that some items are checked and some are not. Granular consent.
  • "School identity" is the only can't-be-unchecked item — if data sharing is on at all, the school's name and basic identity have to be visible to be assigned to a district. This is the architectural minimum.
  • The District Director's name auto-filling is convenience, not contract — when district directorships change (often), Omnischools pulls the latest from a GES directory that we'll maintain. Surface admin doesn't have to keep this up to date manually.
  • The Oversight preview at the bottom is educational, not functional — admins benefit from seeing what their data will look like to GES so they can make informed sharing decisions. Most settings surfaces don't show "what consequences this has elsewhere"; this one does because the consequences matter.

For the build phase

This is the cheapest insurance policy in batch 0 — 4 small schema additions today that unlock the entire Oversight tier later. The cost is one short migration script and a settings page. The benefit is that when GES Oversight launches in 12–18 months, you're not retro-fitting district relationships into a database with 200+ school subscribers. Plant the hooks early, harvest the structure later.

Calls worth flagging

  • The ges_data_sharing_agreements table is a wide row with one record per school — could be done as columns on the schools table, but separating into its own table makes the audit story cleaner ("who consented to what when, who revoked, when") and makes it easier to evolve the scope flags without affecting the core schools table.
  • consented_by references a staff user_id, not just a name — when a private school revokes consent in 2028 because of a policy change, the audit trail shows that Florence A. (admin) consented originally, and Kwame Y. (new admin) revoked. Accountable both directions.
  • The revoked_at nullable field is the soft-delete pattern — we don't delete the consent record when revoked, just timestamp the revocation. Historical record preserved. Forward-only audit pattern applies here too.
  • The 16 regions / 260 districts reference data is GES's official list as of 2026 — these change rarely but do change (region splits, new districts created). The build phase should treat the seed as a versioned snapshot, with a small process for updating when GES revises boundaries.
  • For Multi-tier schools (Basic + Senior), geography and ownership apply at the school-group level, not per-tier. The JHS and SHS share a district. Data sharing scope, however, can be different per tier — e.g. share SHS metrics with GES but not JHS metrics. Granular within reason.
  • Circuits are the smallest jurisdictional unit below district — populated lazily because there are 2000+ of them across Ghana and we don't need them all on day one. Admin selects circuit from a dropdown if known; if not, blank is fine.
