When the sickbay holds the dose

Sickbay setup tells the system three things: what kind of medical capacity this school actually has, who runs it, and which hospitals catch the cases the sickbay can't. The Ghanaian reality is bimodal — about half of public SHS have no sickbay at all and rely on student health prefects with hospital referral. The other half range from a first-aid station with a matron to a full sickbay with beds, isolation, and a visiting doctor. One module, three operational modes.

Standing orders & stock

  • Standing orders are the Matron's clinical authority, registered with the visiting doctor under N&MC scope of practice. The list defines what she can treat without escalation; everything else either waits for Dr Mensah on Thursdays or routes to referral.
  • Eight standing orders cover the canonical Ghanaian SHS sickbay caseload: headache, menstrual pain, sore throat, diarrhoea, suspected malaria, cuts, sprains, allergic skin reactions. Each pairs a complaint with the first-line treatment and the escalation trigger — when the treatment fails or the symptoms cross a line, refer.
  • Suspected malaria has the most operationally specific protocol: RDT test first, then if positive, AL first dose + refer to OPD same day. Malaria is the top SHS complaint by volume in Ghana (especially Semester 2 onward as rainy season begins); the protocol matches Ghana National Drugs Programme guidance.
  • Cuts & abrasions include a tetanus-check cross-reference to the chronic register. The fields aren't isolated — a routine first-aid event triggers a check against the student's immunization history.
  • Drug stock register shows 12 of 24 items. Three categories: OK (above reorder point) / Low (at or just above) / Reorder (below). The 47% of schools that cite "inadequate drug supply" as their top challenge need a stock register that surfaces this proactively, not after the fact.
  • Hydroxyurea has a sub-line "for Adwoa Mensa". Chronic-condition meds are tied to specific students through the chronic register. If Adwoa transfers or graduates, the schema knows her supply was a function of her case; the stock returns to general inventory.
  • Critical reorder warning uses terra-bg pill, not red alarm. Calm-but-clear urgency. The dashed cluster-note below the table translates the numbers into operational consequence: "Hydroxyurea needs ordering this week to avoid a gap in Adwoa Mensa's chronic dose schedule." The system narrates what action is needed, not just what's low.

Referral hospitals

  • Four-tier referral hierarchy matches Ghana's actual public health structure: primary (Asankrangwa Govt., 4.2km) → district (Wassa Akropong, 38km) → tertiary (Komfo Anokyé Teaching Hospital, Kumasi). Plus a private option (St. Martin's) for after-hours.
  • Primary referral gets the gold treatment — heavier border, gradient background, the gold "Primary referral" tag. The matron knows where to call first without scanning the list. Default for any new referral.
  • Distance is captured because it matters operationally. 4.2km to Asankrangwa Govt. is reachable on motorbike or school van within 15 minutes. 198km to KATH means an overnight stay and a chartered transport. The schema's distance_km field drives realistic time-to-care estimates.
  • NHIS acceptance tagged on every hospital. Cost is a real barrier for Ghanaian families; the matron should know whether a referral will or won't be covered. Komfo Anokyé and the district hospital accept NHIS; St. Martin's is private. Adwoa's family carries the NHIS card.
  • Wassa Akropong tagged "After-hours backup" because it has 2 ambulances Asankrangwa Govt. doesn't reliably have. When a 23:00 emergency happens and there's no transport, the district 38km away is the realistic option.
  • KATH listed even though 198km away because for specialist cases (paediatric oncology, neurosurgery, certain sickle-cell crises) the chain ends there. Schema captures the realistic onward-referral path; if a student is sent to Asankrangwa Govt. with a sickle-cell crisis severe enough, KATH is the destination not the district.
  • The "One SHS, One Sickbay" policy debate sits behind this surface. Schools without a sickbay are essentially in Mode C and rely entirely on this list; their first-response is to call the primary hospital and arrange transport. Whether a school has a sickbay or not, the referral list still matters.

Sickbay / Setup / Policy anchors

Policy anchors

The two outside frameworks this sickbay sits inside · and the three-tier parent notification rule that turns sickbay events into parent calls
GES side

SHEP · School Health Education Programme

The Ghana Education Service's unit responsible for school-based health promotion and delivery. SHEP coordinates the School Health Prefect roster, sets the curriculum for health education, and links public schools to district health teams. The sickbay's prefects, drug stock guidance, and outbreak reporting all sit under SHEP.
MoH side

N&MC · Scope of Practice

Nursing and Midwifery Council of Ghana, the statutory regulator (LI 683, 1971). The Matron's clinical authority derives from her N&MC license and her registered standing orders with the visiting doctor. Anything outside scope routes to Dr Mensah or the hospital. The N&MC license number is captured on the staff record and shows on every dispensary action she signs.

Three-tier parent notification rule

Boarding-school practice is settled on this: parents are not told about every minor scratch or headache, but admissions and hospital handoffs are notified immediately. The rule below is what the system fires automatically once an event hits the right tier.

01
Routine visit · discharge same day
Headache treated, sore throat, period pain, minor cut dressed · student walks back to class or dorm within the hour. No automatic notification; weekly digest mentions the visit if parent has opted in.
No notification
Visible in parent portal · weekly digest if opted in
02
Sickbay admission · overnight or beyond class hours
Student admitted to a sickbay bed · suspected malaria with positive RDT · chronic-condition pain episode · injury requiring observation. Parent notified by SMS within the hour; matron's note includes condition and expected duration.

SMS within 1 hour · phone call by HM if no acknowledgement

Policy & notification

  • Two policy anchor cards in navy/gold gradient · SHEP (GES side) and N&MC scope of practice (MoH side). The sickbay sits at the intersection of two government frameworks; making them visible at setup signals to the headmaster that this isn't a generic feature, it's designed for the Ghanaian regulatory environment.
  • SHEP card explains where prefects sit in policy. The School Health Prefect roster on the staff card above isn't arbitrary — it's the GES-recommended structure for sickbay-less and sickbay-having schools alike. The system inherits SHEP vocabulary rather than reinventing it.
  • N&MC card grounds the matron's clinical authority. Why is Mrs Bediako allowed to administer paracetamol without doctor sign-off? Because of her N&MC license + registered standing orders. The legal basis for every dispensary action is one click away.
  • Three-tier notification rule sits in a layered card with progressively saturated background tints · neutral bg / gold-bg / terra-bg-gradient. Visual progression matches escalation severity; the eye reads top-to-bottom as "calm → attention → urgent."
  • Tier 01 routine visits don't notify by default, but parent can opt in to a weekly digest. Boarding-school norm matches the schema: parents don't want a ping for every headache, but some want a summary. The opt-in lives on the parent's preference record.
  • Tier 02 admission notifies within 1 hour by SMS, with phone-call escalation by HM if no acknowledgement. Two-step protocol matches Ghanaian reality where SMS is read but not always promptly; the HM call ensures the parent actually knows.
  • Tier 03 hospital referral is phone-first, immediate. Matron + HM in parallel, not in sequence — both call so the parent gets through to one of them. SMS confirms after the call so there's a written record of what was said. The audit trail captures both.
  • Save bar reminds the headmaster of the outstanding Hydroxyurea reorder · pulled from section 03's stock alert. One workflow surface, multiple subsystems; the save action makes the configuration live and the alert visible.
