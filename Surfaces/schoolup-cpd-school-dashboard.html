Forty-seven teachers, one screen

The Headmaster glances at this surface once a month. Who's on track for licence renewal, who's at risk, who needs a nudge. Sortable, filterable, drilldown to each teacher's individual ledger. The at-risk callout brings the worst three cases to the surface so the conversation is teed up before the staff meeting. The licence renewal calendar shows the rolling 24 months ahead — useful when planning who covers a class while a teacher attends their renewal workshop. This is the same data as the per-teacher ledger, rolled up for the people responsible for the school's compliance.

Dashboard design

  • One-glance summary up top. Five tiles: school average, on-track count, watch count, at-risk count, licences due in 12 months. The Headmaster gets the picture in 3 seconds.
  • At-risk teachers ordered first. Default sort puts the 3 most-at-risk teachers at the top of the table. Don't make the Headmaster scroll to find the problems.
  • Filter chips for context. By status, by department, by special category (new teachers, GES-seconded, licence-due-soon). Match the way HoDs actually think about staff.
  • Department pill is visual aid. Each subject area has its own colour. Helps scan across the table quickly.
  • At-risk callout is opinionated. Doesn't just show the 3 names — explains why each is behind and what to suggest. The Headmaster can walk into the staff meeting with a plan.
  • Rolling licence calendar. 6 months ahead by default · 24-month view available on click. Helps with cover planning, with proactive renewal reminders, with knowing when workshop budgets are needed.

Permissions

  • Headmaster sees all staff.
  • HoD sees only their department. Same UI, scoped data.
  • Form Master sees CPD only on their own page, not on this dashboard.
  • Class teachers see only themselves.

Why this is the surface the Headmaster actually uses
Editorial · the operational frame
The Headmaster's reality

Compliance is recurring work, not a year-end audit panic.

Most schools deal with CPD compliance once a year — typically in August or September, when the NTC asks for the previous year's points return and the school scrambles to back-fill records. By then, half the year is unrecoverable: a teacher who hasn't attended a workshop in 9 months can't attend 4 of them in 4 weeks. The damage is already done.

This dashboard is designed to make the year-end panic impossible. If you check it monthly, at-risk teachers surface in October — when they can still attend the GAMA conference in August, take a course in November, mentor a new teacher in Term 3. The conversation is "we noticed you're at 6 of 20 — what would help" not "your licence renewal is in 60 days and you have 8 points."

The dashboard also makes the school-wide story visible to the Headmaster in a way it isn't otherwise. 81% on-track is a reasonable number; 50% would be a crisis. The number is right there. No spreadsheet to maintain, no quarterly request to HoDs, no scramble in September. The point of building this is not to produce a dashboard — it's to make the Headmaster's job actually doable on this dimension.

The recurring rhythm

  • Headmaster checks monthly. First Monday of each month, 5 minutes. Look at the at-risk callout. Schedule any needed conversations.
  • HoDs check biweekly. Filter to their department. Surface conversation topics for next dept meeting.
  • Term-end review. Headmaster reviews term-by-term trends. Patterns in who's lagging.
  • Pre-NTC sync. Before the monthly NTC sync, review what's queued vs synced. Surface 5 of this batch (NTC portal sync) is the next-level drilldown.
