Omnischools Oversight · the coverage instrument

Which schools are actually on the platform.

Every Oversight surface carries a coverage figure — 34 of 40 schools, 431 of 508, 75.6% nationally — and this is the surface that manages it. The data-sharing agreement is the instrument that brings a school onto Omnischools's Oversight reporting: it records the agreed scope (which categories of data, and that named records stay gated), the agreement version, and the school's onboarding status. To be clear about what it is not — a school does not "opt in" to GES oversight; GES is the governing body and schools report to it by statute. The agreement is the practical onboarding contract for the Omnischools platform itself, the document that turns a registered-but-invisible school into a reporting one. This is a national-tier surface; the demo is the national Oversight administrator working the agreement register, with the Western Region's gap in focus.

The instrument behind every coverage figure

  • The funnel makes the coverage number's construction visible. 9,180 registered → 7,610 with an agreement → 6,940 live and reporting. The 75.6% every dashboard shows is the last over the first — and the funnel shows the two distinct gaps: schools with no agreement at all, and signed schools still mid-onboarding. They need different work.
  • This is an onboarding register, not an opt-in ledger. The page is explicit about it: schools report to GES by statute, not by consent. The agreement is the practical contract for joining the Omnischools platform — the document that turns a registered-but-invisible school into a reporting one. The framing matters, and the surface states it rather than implying a permission relationship.
  • Three statuses, because the middle one is real. No agreement, signed-but-onboarding, live-and-reporting. "Signed" is not yet "live" — 670 schools nationally sit in that gap. A register that collapsed it to a binary would misrepresent how much coverage work is actually outstanding.
  • Agreement version is tracked per school. Samreboi sits on superseded v1.4 against a current v2.1; 214 schools nationally are on an old version. The surface flags them — a school reporting under outdated agreed scope is a compliance loose end the national tier needs to close.
  • Every row drills to the agreement detail. Status, version and signing date on the row; the agreed scope and version history one tap down. The register is the index, section 2 is the document.

The agreement document, and the gap it measures

  • The scope table is the aggregate-vs-gated boundary written down. Four categories aggregate, two gated — the exact distinction Decision 6 sets out and the compliance record view enforces. The agreement is where that boundary is formally recorded; the surfaces downstream implement it.
  • Scope is standardised, not negotiated. Every v2.1 school reports under the identical scope — the agreement version is the scope. This keeps Oversight coherent: a national figure means the same thing everywhere because no school agreed to a bespoke arrangement.
  • Version history is kept for a reason. When the agreement is revised, schools re-sign — and the old versions stay on record, so it is always clear which scope a school reported under at a past date. That matters for any retrospective audit of what data was legitimately collected when.
  • The regional gap panel turns coverage into a work programme. 508 registered, 462 signed, 431 live, 46 with no agreement — and the 46 are named to two districts. The surface does not just measure the gap, it routes the onboarding effort to where it closes fastest.
  • This surface is the source of the coverage caveat everywhere else. The enrolment surface's "unmeasured gap," the dashboards' "X of Y schools," the anomaly queue's COV-GAP rule — all of it traces back to this register. Closing agreements here is what makes every other Oversight figure more complete.
  • This completes the data-sharing agreement surface. The national register with its three-stage funnel (section 1) and one agreement's scope, history and regional gap (section 2) — the instrument that governs what Oversight can see. The third surface of Batch 3.
