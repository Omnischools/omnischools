What the register holds

  • Care plans, not diagnoses. Each entry has a daily medication schedule, emergency protocol, NHIS card detail, and an emergency parent contact. Diagnoses without plans don't get added — they belong in the visit history instead.
  • Six is the right number to know by name. Asankrangwa's matron can carry these six in her head. If the register grew to 30, the design changes — we'd add subgroups and condition-led views.
  • Condition pills follow severity colour. Sickle cell terracotta, asthma warn-amber, epilepsy purple, anaphylaxis gold (high-attention not high-frequency), mental health navy (different system), diabetes green-blue.
  • Status separates from condition. Asthma can be "stable" or "monitor" or "active"; SCD can be "stable" or "crisis". A condition is forever, a status is today.
  • HM grants visible inline. The avatar stack on each row shows who outside the matron + admin can see this record. It's a constant reminder of the privacy choice.

How the plan is structured

  • Sickle cell anchors the design. The detail and protocol depth here is what other chronic conditions inherit — they don't always need all five protocol steps, but the structure scales down better than it scales up.
  • Medication schedule maps to rounds. 06:30, 13:00, 21:00 columns line up with the morning, lunch, and evening rounds in surface 2 ("Today's sickbay"). When a round fires, Adwoa's entries appear in the round task list automatically.
  • Emergency protocol is matron's plan in their own words. Not a generic SCD reference — this is what Mrs Bediako will actually do when Adwoa presents. KATH paediatric haematology contact is named because the contact relationship matters more than the institution.
  • Dorm-side artefact is the bridge. HMs aren't clinicians and shouldn't have full medical detail; the artefact gives them the four things they need — triggers, red flags, action, who to call. Printed and posted, not in the app.
  • NHIS panel reflects Ghana reality. Card number, expiry, what's covered, what costs the family. SCD is a chronic exemption category in some NHIS interpretations — that nuance lives here.

Mental health, told honestly

  • Most Ghanaian SHS don't have a counsellor either. J's school does — Mrs Owusu via the VLC system. Schools without one would route to a community psychosocial support officer or the DMHU directly. The register accommodates both.
  • Two-system handoff is explicit. Clinical home (DMHU), pastoral home (VLC), sickbay role (monitoring). When everyone knows their lane, no one falls through.
  • "What the matron doesn't do" is in the plan. Naming the limits protects both the student and the matron. Mrs Bediako is an excellent matron, not a mental health professional, and saying so is care.
  • Somatic presentations are flagged. Adolescent anxiety often shows up as headache, stomach pain, fatigue. The register tells the matron to treat the symptom and route the concern — not to ignore either.
  • Cross-link to VLC is one click. If Mrs Bediako needs context on J's recent sessions, "Open VLC case" takes her to Mrs Owusu's case file (read-only · with Mrs Owusu's prior grant).

Update
Audit trail is append-only
Reads, writes, grants, revocations, exports — every action is recorded with timestamp and actor. Cannot be edited or deleted, even by Headmaster. Retention: 7 years after student leaves the school per GES records policy.

Privacy mechanics

  • Four default-access staff. Matron, Headmaster, Deputy Head (Welfare), School Nurse if appointed. Anyone else is per-grant.
  • Three scope levels. Full plan (HMs for life-threatening conditions like anaphylaxis, epilepsy, diabetes). Partial (HMs for less acute conditions — dorm-side card only). Referral-only / Allergen-only / Restrict-only (specialists who need one specific fact, not the medical context).
  • HM grants are role-based, not personal. When E. Akoto moves out of Slessor next semester, his grants auto-transfer to the new HM. J. Manu's housemaster change last month is the only audit row that flipped a grant.
  • Time-limited grants expire automatically. Class teachers for exam accommodations expire end of semester. Dorm-mate alerts expire at house reassignment.
  • Every read is in the trail. The Headmaster looking at Adwoa's plan after admission this morning is logged. So is the Bursar pulling up the kitchen alert. Audit shows what was looked at and when — not just changes.
  • Append-only is real. No edit, no delete, not even by the Head. If a grant was made in error, the action is "revoke" — the original grant stays in the trail.
