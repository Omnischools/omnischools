Why this layout

  • Featured hero for school-level posts. Institutional achievements (BECE results, school awards) get full-width treatment — they're the reason a parent or teacher comes back to this page.
  • Filter strip with three groups. Type (Academic / Non-academic), source (school / teachers / students / parents), and the "Endorsed only" filter. Three logical axes, visually separated by dividers.
  • Endorsement marker is the gold ★ in a circle on the photo top-right. Subtle but unmistakable — endorsed posts feel verified without making unendorsed posts feel lesser.
  • Type badge top-left of each photo — gold for Academic, white for Non-academic. Lets the eye pre-sort the feed without reading every title.
  • Recipient row separates the celebration from the source. "Akosua Boateng · JHS 2A" is the recipient; "Posted by Mrs. Owusu" is the author. Confusing these is a real risk in school posts; the visual separation prevents it.
  • Parent posts pending endorsement get a gold banner across the top: "pending school endorsement — currently visible only to family circle." The card visibly distinguishes itself, and the scope chip shows "Class · requested" so admins know what the parent intended.
  • The endorse action on a pending parent post says "★ ENDORSE & PUBLISH" instead of just "ENDORSE" — the language signals that this single click both verifies and broadcasts. One action, two clear consequences.
  • Comments thread on the Akosua card shows the feature when comments are enabled school-wide. Two comments — one from the headmistress (admin), one from the parent. The comment input has a name placeholder ("Add a comment as Florence...") to remind the user of their role identity.
  • Students cannot comment, even when comments are on. No comment row from a student on the thread. This is enforced at the role level, not by absence — the input field is simply not shown on screens viewed by students.
  • Role tags inside comments ("Head", "Parent", "Teacher") use the existing color system — gold for school staff, green for parent. Quick visual signal of authority weight.
  • "+ ENDORSE" vs "★ ENDORSED" — same place, different state. Anyone with endorsement rights sees the action; everyone else sees the static badge.
  • The four photos use the brand palette as gradient placeholders. Real photos go here in production.

How class & school posts work for parents: Your post will be visible to your family circle right away. To share it with the class or school, an admin or teacher needs to endorse it. We'll notify them automatically — most posts are endorsed within a day.

Why this layout

  • Showing the parent's view rather than the teacher's because that's where the new endorsement-pending behavior matters. Teacher's flow is the same minus the pending notice — endorsements auto-apply for teachers.
  • Recipient is locked. Parents post for their own children only — no picker needed. The "Locked" tag on the row makes the scoping explicit.
  • Type comes first, not recipient. Type shapes how the post will appear in the feed; picking it first is a cleaner mental sequence.
  • Three scope options with explicit "until then" language. Each School-wide and Class option spells out the gate: "If a school admin endorses" + "Until then, only your family circle sees it." No ambiguity about what happens at post time.
  • "Just our family circle" is highlighted as no-endorsement-needed. If a parent wants their post visible immediately and only to family, this is the option that does it. Removing the gate from the option that doesn't need one prevents confusion.
  • Pending notice appears below the scope picker when School-wide or Class is selected. Reinforces what's about to happen at post time, not just what each option means in the abstract.
  • "We'll notify them automatically" reassures the parent that they're not on the hook for chasing a teacher down. The system handles the handoff.
  • Rough timing expectation ("most posts are endorsed within a day") sets reality without making a binding commitment.
  • Photo zone is gold-dashed, matching the logo upload pattern from onboarding. Consistent affordance for "upload an image" across the product.

Why these layouts

  • Student tab labels are "Mine / Class / School" — three concentric circles of relevance, ordered closest-out. The active default is "Mine" because that's the personal hook.
  • Parent tab labels are "All / Academic / Other" — type-filtering, not source-filtering. Parents care less about who posted and more about what kind.
  • "Akosua's wins" as the parent header — personal, warm, possessive. Different from the institutional "Achievements" header on desktop.
  • Big "+ Add" prompt at top of feed on both, with role-specific copy. Student: "your own achievement." Parent: "post one for Akosua."
  • Pending banner on parent's own post. The Sunday school card shows the small gold pulsing dot + "Pending school endorsement · family circle only for now." Parent sees their own post is in flight; status is unambiguous.
  • Source row says "requested Class" with the requested scope in gold. Tells the parent what they asked for, separate from what's currently visible. When endorsed, this becomes the actual scope.
  • Endorsement star carries through to mobile, same gold treatment. Visual continuity between desktop and mobile.
  • Source row distinguishes "you" vs "Mrs. Owusu" vs "school". Three avatar treatments — gold-bg for individual, navy-bg for school. Makes the post-source unambiguous.
  • The "+" button in the top right mirrors the gold treatment of the announcements/payments add buttons across the app.
  • Comments visible on mobile too when enabled school-wide — student view shows the thread but never an input field (students can't comment in MVP1, regardless of role-on-screen). Parent view shows thread + input when commenting is enabled.

Achievement preferences

Comments on achievements

Allow conversations under achievement posts. Useful for celebration and recognition; comes with a small moderation responsibility.
ON
Who can comment Admin Teachers Parents Students
In MVP1, students cannot comment on achievement posts — even when comments are on. This keeps the conversation among adults and avoids moderation overhead in the early product. We'll revisit student commenting once we have a clear picture of how schools use the feature.

Endorsement policy

When a parent posts an achievement targeted at the school or class, the post is private to the family until an admin or teacher endorses it.
ON
Recommended. Without this gate, a parent could post anything school-wide. The endorsement step protects the school's voice while still letting parents celebrate their children freely within their family circle. This is on by default and we strongly recommend keeping it on.

Auto-add achievements to transcripts

Endorsed academic achievements automatically appear on student transcripts at year-end. Coming in MVP3.
SOON

Share aggregate data with the Ministry

Anonymised counts of academic achievements per district contribute to national education insights. Configurable from main school settings.
OFF

Why this layout

  • Comments off by default in production (shown ON here for the wireframe to demonstrate the active state). Schools should turn it on deliberately when they're ready for moderation.
  • "Who can comment" surfaces the role rules right under the main toggle. Admin / Teachers / Parents are checked; Students appears struck through and visibly blocked.
  • The footnote explicitly explains why students can't comment in MVP1 — and signals it's revisitable. Better than silently omitting them.
  • Endorsement policy as a separate setting, also defaulted on. Toggling it off would let parent posts go school-wide immediately — a real risk we discourage with the "Recommended" frame and the gold-bordered explanation.
  • Ghosted "coming soon" settings for transcript integration and ministry data sharing. Tells admin what's on the roadmap without pretending the features exist.
  • Two ON cards full opacity, two coming-soon cards at 55%. The visual hierarchy of available vs upcoming is unmistakable; admin doesn't waste time toggling something inert.
  • Settings live under their feature, not in a global Settings dump. "Settings / Achievements" makes the scope clear in the breadcrumb.
